# Video Brief: Dave's Hot Chicken × Zerocap

**Format:** TikTok / Reels / Shorts
**Length:** ~20 seconds
**Concept:** Capture Dave's Hot Chicken (trending) with Zerocap and transform it into a 3D pet

## Hook Strategy

### Flash-forward the final pet (0.5s)

Show the cute 3D pet for half a second at the very start — almost subliminal. This is the proven structure for transformation content on TikTok (Lensa, Ghibli filter, Remini all used it).

Why it works:
- Creates an **open loop** — viewer sees something unexpected and needs to know how
- **Pattern interrupt** — signals "this isn't just a food video" to people scrolling past food content
- Gives a reason to watch all 20 seconds → boosts completion rate (target: 70%+)

Don't linger on the pet. 0.5 seconds max. If you show too much, no payoff left.

### Use a statement, not a question

"How to make a chicken burger pet" sounds tutorial-y. TikTok hooks that convert are statements — more confident, more absurd, more scroll-stopping.

**Best options:**
- "I turned Dave's Hot Chicken into a pet"
- "This chicken burger is alive now"
- "Dave's Hot Chicken didn't expect this"

The first one does double duty — it's the hook AND names the trending brand, so TikTok's semantic indexing picks up Dave's Hot Chicken and pushes it to that audience.

## Recommended Structure

| Time | What |
|------|------|
| 0.0–0.5s | Flash the final pet (fast cut, almost a blink) |
| 0.5–1.5s | Text overlay: "I turned Dave's Hot Chicken into a pet" |
| 1.5–3s | The box, opening it, revealing the burger |
| 3–15s | Zerocap capture and transformation process |
| 15–20s | Full pet reveal + interaction, CTA or app name |

## Notes

- Voiceover should match the text overlay statement — keep it casual, not scripted
- Dave's Hot Chicken is trending, so include the brand name in text, voiceover, and hashtags for algorithmic lift
- This is real product footage (not AI-generated) — that authenticity is an advantage
