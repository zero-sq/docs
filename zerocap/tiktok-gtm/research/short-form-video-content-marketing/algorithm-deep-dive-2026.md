## 1. TikTok: The For You Page Algorithm in 2026

### How Distribution Works Now

TikTok's algorithm has one goal: maximize watch time and engagement by showing the right content to the right person at the right time. In 2026, the system has become significantly more sophisticated than the "spray and pray" era of 2021-2023.

**The Three-Phase Distribution Model:**

```
Phase 1: Seed Test (first 200-500 views)
    Your video is shown to a small seed audience based on:
    - Your follower base (follower-first testing is NEW in 2026)
    - Topic/niche signals from your previous content
    - Semantic analysis of your video content

Phase 2: Expansion (500-10K views)
    If seed metrics are strong, the algorithm expands to:
    - Similar interest groups
    - Related niche audiences
    - Broader geographic pools

Phase 3: Viral Distribution (10K+ views)
    If expansion metrics hold, the video enters broad distribution:
    - For You Page of non-niche users
    - Search results
    - Cross-niche recommendations
```

### The Signals That Matter (Ranked by Weight)

| Signal | Weight | What It Means | SpaceZero Implication |
|--------|--------|---------------|----------------------|
| **Watch time & completion rate** | 40-50% | How much of the video people watch. TikTok measures at checkpoints: 3s, 15s, 30s, 60s. Replays are weighted more heavily than any other metric. | Default to 15-25 second videos. At this length, 70%+ completion is achievable. |
| **Early engagement (first hour)** | 25-35% | Likes, comments, shares, saves in the first 60 minutes after posting. | Reply to every comment within 30 minutes. Post when your audience is online. |
| **Engagement quality** | 15-20% | Shares are weighted 3x higher than likes. Saves indicate "future intent" and signal lasting value. Comments with replies (threads) signal deeper engagement. | Design content that is shareable ("tag someone who does this") and saveable ("tutorial I need to try later"). |
| **Niche authority** | Emerging | TikTok now scores creators on topical consistency. Posting within the same niche builds "authority" that the algorithm rewards. | Stay in the sandbox/builder/gaming niche. Do NOT chase random trends outside your lane. |

### Critical 2026 Changes

**1. Completion Rate Threshold Raised to 70%**
In 2024, you needed ~50% completion rate for viral distribution. In 2026, that bar is 70%. Videos below 70% completion rarely break 10,000 views. This is the single most important metric.

**2. "Qualified Views" at 5 Seconds**
TikTok now distinguishes between "views" (video starts playing) and "qualified views" (viewer watches at least 5 seconds). Only qualified views count toward distribution scoring. Your hook needs to hold viewers for 5 seconds minimum, not just 3.

**3. Semantic Indexing (TikTok as a Search Engine)**
The algorithm now indexes content through three layers:
- **Layer 1: Audio Transcription** -- Every spoken word is auto-transcribed and assigned a relevance score. Say your keywords out loud.
- **Layer 2: Computer Vision** -- AI scans video at 20 frames/second, recognizing objects and reading text overlays. What appears on screen matters for discovery.
- **Layer 3: Caption/Hashtag Analysis** -- Caption text is cross-referenced with search queries.

For SpaceZero, this means: if you say "mobile building game," show building gameplay, write "mobile building game" in text overlay, and include it in your caption, you will rank for "mobile building game" searches on TikTok.

**4. Niche Authority Scoring**
The algorithm now builds detailed user profiles: "This user likes [specific game type] content, posted by creators who [specific style]." Creators who consistently serve the same audience are rewarded over those who chase trending topics across different niches. Consistency in topic matters MORE than consistency in posting frequency. Three to four high-quality, niche-relevant videos per week outperform daily low-effort content.

**5. Follower-First Testing**
New in 2026: videos are first shown to your existing followers before expanding to non-followers. This means your follower base quality matters more than it used to. Building a small but engaged follower base of actual gaming/sandbox fans is more valuable than inflated follower counts from viral one-offs.

### TikTok Optimization Checklist for SpaceZero

- [ ] Keep videos 15-25 seconds for maximum completion rate
- [ ] Hook must hold viewers for 5+ seconds (qualified view threshold)
- [ ] Say target keywords aloud in the video
- [ ] Show target keywords as text overlay
- [ ] Include target keywords in caption
- [ ] Use 3-5 hashtags (TikTok limits to 5 in 2026)
- [ ] Post during peak gaming hours: 7 PM-12 AM, and 12 PM-3 PM on weekdays
- [ ] Reply to all comments within 30 minutes of posting
- [ ] Stay in the gaming/building/sandbox niche consistently
- [ ] Target 70%+ completion rate on every video

## 2. YouTube Shorts: How Shorts Discovery Works in 2026

### Fundamental Difference from TikTok

YouTube Shorts operates on a fundamentally different philosophy than TikTok. Where TikTok prioritizes content regardless of creator, YouTube judges channels holistically. Your Shorts performance is influenced by your overall channel health, not just individual video quality.

**Key distinction: TikTok is a content machine. YouTube is a channel machine.**

### Discovery Mechanics

**The 74% Non-Subscriber Stat:**
74% of YouTube Shorts views come from non-subscribers. This makes Shorts YouTube's primary discovery format. For SpaceZero, this means Shorts is the best way to reach new audiences on YouTube -- better than trying to rank long-form videos with a new channel.

**How a Short Gets Picked Up:**

```
Step 1: Upload Short
    YouTube shows it to a small test audience (subscribers + topic-matched users)

Step 2: Initial Metrics (first 2-4 hours)
    YouTube evaluates:
    - Swipe-away rate (inverse of retention)
    - Average view duration
    - Engagement actions (likes, comments, shares)

Step 3: Expansion or Suppression
    If metrics are strong → expanded to broader "Shorts shelf" audience
    If metrics are weak → distribution slows dramatically

Step 4: Long-Tail Discovery (unique to YouTube)
    Unlike TikTok where videos peak and die within days,
    YouTube Shorts can continue gaining views for MONTHS
    through search results and recommendations.
```

### Key Ranking Signals for YouTube Shorts

| Signal | How It Differs from TikTok | SpaceZero Action |
|--------|---------------------------|------------------|
| **Swipe-away rate** | YouTube measures when someone swipes to the next Short. Lower swipe-away = better. | Strong visual hook in first 2 seconds. |
| **Average view duration** | YouTube cares about total time on platform, not just your video. | Shorts that lead to more Shorts consumption are rewarded. |
| **Engaged Views** | YouTube distinguishes between Views (starts/loops) and Engaged Views (meaningful interaction). Only Engaged Views count for monetization. | Design for interaction, not passive watching. |
| **Channel authority** | YouTube evaluates your channel as a whole, not just individual Shorts. | Build a consistent channel identity. Channels using Shorts + long-form grow 41% faster. |
| **Subscriber conversion** | YouTube tracks whether Shorts viewers subscribe. This feeds channel-level scoring. | Include a clear channel value proposition in your Shorts. |

### YouTube Shorts vs. TikTok: Practical Differences

| Factor | TikTok | YouTube Shorts |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| **Virality speed** | Can blow up in hours, but fades in days | Slower build, but views accumulate for months |
| **Creator dependency** | Content-first. New accounts can go viral immediately. | Channel-first. Channel history influences distribution. |
| **Optimal length** | 15-25 seconds | 15-60 seconds. Shorts at 13 seconds and 60 seconds perform best. |
| **Search discoverability** | Growing (TikTok SEO) but still primarily FYP-driven | Strong. Shorts appear in YouTube search results alongside regular videos. |
| **Monetization** | Creator Fund (limited) + brand deals | YouTube Partner Program revenue sharing (more predictable income) |
| **Long-form bridge** | None. TikTok is short-form only. | Shorts feed subscribers to long-form content. Channels that use Shorts + long-form grow 41% faster. |
| **Gaming performance** | Gaming averages ~5.9% engagement rate | Gaming averages 8.5% retention. Shorts leads all platforms with 5.91% engagement rate. |

### YouTube Shorts Optimization Checklist for SpaceZero

- [ ] Aim for 15-60 second Shorts (test both 13s and 60s as sweet spots)
- [ ] Target 70%+ intro retention (first 3 seconds)
- [ ] Target 60%+ completion rate for Shorts under 30 seconds
- [ ] Post 3-7 Shorts per week (consistency > volume)
- [ ] Use keyword-rich titles (YouTube search is powerful)
- [ ] Add to relevant playlists (grouping Shorts boosts channel authority)
- [ ] Consider companion long-form devlog content (41% faster channel growth)
- [ ] Use Shorts as a testing ground for topics before creating long-form versions
- [ ] Add end screens pointing to longer content when relevant

## 3. Instagram Reels: How Reels Discovery Works in 2026

### The DM Share Signal

Instagram head Adam Mosseri has explicitly stated: **DM shares are the #1 ranking signal for Reels in 2026.** This is the defining characteristic of the Instagram algorithm and what makes it fundamentally different from TikTok and YouTube.

**Engagement signal hierarchy on Instagram (confirmed by Mosseri):**

```
1. Sends (DM shares) ←── 3-5x more valuable than likes for reaching new audiences
2. Watch time
3. Likes per reach
4. Comments
5. Saves
```

This changes the content strategy entirely. On TikTok, you optimize for completion rate. On Instagram, you optimize for **shareability** -- making content that people want to send to a friend via DM.

### Two Distribution Systems

Instagram operates a dual distribution system:

**1. Connected Reach (shown to followers)**
- Based on relationship strength and past engagement
- How recently the follower interacted with your content
- Favors accounts that the user actively engages with

**2. Unconnected Reach (shown to non-followers via Reels tab, Explore, recommendations)**
- Based on content quality signals and topic relevance
- This is where growth happens
- Driven primarily by sends/DM shares, watch time, and engagement rate

**For new accounts (0-10K followers), focus 70% of effort on unconnected reach tactics:** shareability, hooks, trending audio.

### Key Differences from TikTok and YouTube Shorts

| Factor | Instagram Reels | TikTok | YouTube Shorts |
|--------|----------------|--------|----------------|
| **Primary signal** | DM shares (sends) | Completion rate | Swipe-away rate + channel health |
| **Optimal length** | 30-90 seconds for engagement. 7-15 seconds for virality. | 15-25 seconds | 15-60 seconds |
| **Reach potential** | Reels = 35% of total Instagram screen time. 2B+ monthly users. | Largest short-form audience | Growing fast. 200B+ daily Shorts views. |
| **Hashtag strategy** | 3-5 highly relevant hashtags. Keyword SEO in captions is more important. | 3-5 hashtags. TikTok limits to 5. | Keywords in title matter most. |
| **Small account advantage** | Smaller accounts (<50K) achieve higher engagement rates than large accounts. This is the sweet spot for organic growth. | Small accounts (<5K) get 4.20% avg engagement by views. | New creators are actively pushed in 2026. 74% views from non-subscribers. |
| **Content discovery** | "Your Algorithm" topic controls let users customize interests. Reels must match topics users actively choose. | Semantic indexing + FYP personalization | YouTube search + recommendations |
| **Audio** | Trending audio still matters more than on other platforms. | Original audio now factors into ranking. | Audio matters less; visual hooks dominate. |

### The "Your Algorithm" Feature (New in 2026)

Instagram now lets users view and customize the interest topics that influence their Reels recommendations. Users can add or remove topics. This means:

- If SpaceZero content is tagged/categorized under "gaming" and "creative/building," it will only reach users who have kept those topics active.
- If there's a mismatch between your content topic and what a user has selected, your content won't surface, no matter how well-made it is.
- **Action item:** Ensure your content clearly signals "gaming" and "creative" topics through hashtags, audio, captions, and visual content.

### Instagram Reels Optimization Checklist for SpaceZero

- [ ] Design for shareability first (what would make someone DM this to a friend?)
- [ ] Test two length buckets: 7-15 seconds (virality) and 30-60 seconds (engagement)
- [ ] Use trending audio when relevant to gaming/building content
- [ ] 3-5 highly relevant hashtags (not 30)
- [ ] Write keyword-rich captions (Instagram SEO is caption-first, not hashtag-first)
- [ ] Add alt text to Reels with keyword descriptions
- [ ] Hook must work with sound OFF (many Instagram users browse silently)
- [ ] Post when audience is most active (check Instagram Insights once available)
- [ ] Create content that triggers "@ your friend who does this" comments
- [ ] Reels under 90 seconds have highest chance of landing on the Reels tab

## 4. Cross-Platform Strategy Matrix

| "DM this to your friend" content | Instagram Reels | DM shares are the #1 algorithm signal | TikTok (shares weighted 3x) |
| Tutorial/educational builds | YouTube Shorts | YouTube search captures "how to build X" queries | TikTok (TikTok SEO growing) |

| Challenge/interactive content | TikTok | Duet/stitch features + challenge culture | Instagram Reels |
| Long-form devlogs (3-10 min) | YouTube (regular) | No short-form alternative for long-form | None |

### Platform-Specific Adjustments When Cross-Posting

The SAME video should be posted to all three platforms, but with these adjustments:

**TikTok version:**
- Add TikTok-trending audio if relevant
- Include "Part X" series language
- Use 3-5 hashtags including #SpaceZero #mobilegaming #sandboxgame
- CTA: "Search SpaceZero on the App Store"

**YouTube Shorts version:**
- Keyword-rich title (YouTube search matters most here)
- Add to a playlist ("SpaceZero Builds" playlist)
- Description with App Store link
- Consider slightly longer cut (YouTube rewards 60s Shorts)

**Instagram Reels version:**
- Use trending Instagram audio
- Caption that encourages DM sharing ("Send this to someone who needs this game")
- 3-5 hashtags focused on niche (#mobilegaming #3dcreator #sandboxgame)
- Stories share: repost the Reel to Stories with a poll or question sticker
