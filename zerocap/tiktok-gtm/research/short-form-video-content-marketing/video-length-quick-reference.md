# Video Length Quick Reference (March 2026)

Practical cheat sheet for video length optimization and platform quirks. For full algorithm analysis, see `algorithm-deep-dive-2026.md`.

## Ideal Video Length by Platform

| Platform | Sweet Spot | Use Case | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **TikTok** | 11-18 sec | Maximum virality (completion + replay loops) | Up to 34 sec for storytelling, 60 sec for educational |
| **Instagram Reels** | 7-15 sec | Broad reach and replays | Optimizes for view count and shareability |
| **Instagram Reels** | 60-90 sec | Deep engagement (comments, shares, saves) | Long enough for narrative, short enough to finish |
| **YouTube Shorts** | 50-58 sec | Best performance | YT Shorts viewers expect substance; push close to the 60 sec limit |

**2026 consensus: Pacing > Length.** A tight 15-second video outperforms a slow 15-second video every time. Focus on how well content engages throughout, not just duration.

### Cross-Posting Trade-Off

Each platform rewards a different length, so cross-posting the same video to all three means compromising on at least one. Consider platform-specific cuts when possible:
- TikTok cut: 11-18 sec, tight and loopable
- YouTube Shorts cut: push toward 50-58 sec with more substance
- Instagram Reels: depends on goal (reach vs. engagement)

## TikTok Zero Views After Posting

Getting 0 views right after posting is normal. Don't panic and don't delete the video.

**Why it happens:**
1. **Moderation review** -- TikTok scans every video before pushing it to the For You Page. This can take minutes to hours.
2. **View counter bug** -- Known issue where people are watching (you may even get likes/comments) but the view count stays at 0. It catches up later.
3. **Different distribution model** -- Instagram and YouTube push to followers first (instant views). TikTok tests with a small batch of strangers first, evaluates watch time and engagement, then decides whether to expand. The initial evaluation takes time.

**What to do:**
- Don't delete it -- TikTok penalizes frequent re-uploads, and deleting interrupts the algorithm's evaluation
- Give it 2-3 hours minimum before worrying
- Double-check visibility isn't set to "Friends only" or "Private"
- If still at 0 after 24 hours, there may be a shadow restriction or guideline flag

TikTok's three-phase distribution (seed test -> expansion -> viral) means the first few hours are evaluation, not distribution. See `algorithm-deep-dive-2026.md` Phase 1 for details.

## Minimum Video Length

There is no law or regulation requiring minimum video length on any platform. These are purely algorithmic preferences:

- **TikTok**: No minimum, but videos under 5-10 sec get less distribution (harder to evaluate watch-time metrics)
- **Instagram Reels**: No minimum
- **YouTube Shorts**: Must be under 60 sec, no minimum

You can post a 1-second video on all three -- it just won't get pushed as hard.

## YouTube Shorts Thumbnails

Custom Shorts thumbnails are a mess in 2026:
- **YouTube mobile app**: Can only pick a frame from the video during upload. Cannot change after publishing. Cannot upload a custom image.
- **YouTube Studio desktop**: Can upload a custom thumbnail image, but only if your account has the feature (rolling out to YouTube Partner Program members first).
- **YouTube Studio mobile app**: Not supported.

If you need to change a Shorts thumbnail after publishing, the only option is to delete and re-upload.
