## Platform Deep Dives

### 1. Collabstr

**Model**: Self-serve marketplace

**How it works**: Brands browse a public marketplace of creators who list pre-priced content packages. You find a creator, purchase their package (or send a custom offer), and the platform holds payment in escrow until you approve the work. Campaign briefs can also be broadcast to the full creator network, letting creators apply to your project.

**Pricing**:
- No subscription required on the Basic plan — pay-per-transaction with a **10% marketplace fee**
- Premium plan reduces fee to **5%** and unlocks unlimited campaigns, advanced filters, priority support
- Average micro-influencer post costs **~$198**
- No long-term contracts required

**Creator pool**: ~550,000 influencers on the platform. Covers TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube. Strong in lifestyle, beauty, and tech niches. Includes both influencer posts and UGC-only creators.

**Key features**:
- Pre-priced packages (transparent before purchase)
- Escrow payment protection
- Influencer price calculator
- Fake follower checker (Instagram + TikTok)
- Campaign briefs broadcast to full network
- UGC marketplace alongside sponsored post marketplace

**Best for**: Small brands, micro-influencer campaigns, teams that want zero commitment and transparent per-creator pricing. Ideal for testing influencer marketing without platform lock-in.

**Pros**:
- No subscription needed — true pay-as-you-go
- Transparent pricing before you commit
- Escrow protects both sides
- Easy to use for first-timers
- Strong micro/nano creator pool

**Cons**:
- Limited analytics and reporting vs. SaaS platforms
- Creator quality varies — need to vet manually
- Less control over campaign management workflow
- 10% fee adds up (5% on Premium)

**Realistic for a 4-person team with $2K–5K?** **Yes** — this is one of the most accessible platforms for small teams. You can start spending immediately with no subscription.

### 2. Billo

**Model**: Creator marketplace (primarily UGC, expanding to creator posts)

**How it works**: Brands create a task/brief, creators apply, you select your preferred creator, ship product, and receive video content. Originally focused purely on UGC (content delivered to brand for use in ads), Billo has expanded to support **creator-handle posting** — where creators post natively from their own Instagram or TikTok profiles.

**Pricing**:
- UGC videos starting at **$59 per video**
- Platform editions range from **$0 to $30,000** depending on scale
- 30-day refund if no creator match
- Creator posts (on their own feed) cost more than base UGC

**Creator pool**: 5,000+ vetted creators. Smaller pool than Collabstr but more curated. Strongest in product-focused verticals (DTC, beauty, food, fitness).

**Key features**:
- Task-based workflow (post brief → creators apply → select → deliver)
- Supports both UGC delivery and organic creator-handle posting
- Script angle guidance and creator casting
- TikTok Spark Ads and Meta Partnership Ads support
- Creator growth path (UGC → repeat partnerships → bigger payouts)

**Best for**: Brands that primarily need UGC video content for ads but want the option to have creators post organically too. Strong for product-demo style content.

**Pros**:
- Very affordable entry point ($59/video for UGC)
- Curated creator quality
- Good for testing ad creative at low cost
- Expanding into creator-posted content

**Cons**:
- **Primary focus is still UGC, not feed posts** — creator posting is secondary
- Smaller creator pool limits niche options
- Less discovery/search tooling than dedicated platforms
- Not ideal if your main goal is sponsored feed posts

**Realistic for a 4-person team with $2K–5K?** **Yes for UGC, partially for feed posts.** Good if you want cheap ad creative. Less ideal if sponsored feed posts are the primary goal.

### 3. Insense

**Model**: SaaS platform + marketplace

**How it works**: Brands subscribe to a plan, post campaign briefs to a vetted creator marketplace, and manage the full workflow (creator matching, content review, payment, usage rights) in-platform. Strong integration with TikTok and Meta for running creator content as paid ads.

**Pricing**:
- **Trial**: $500/month (1 campaign, up to 10 creator hires)
- **Brand**: $500/month billed quarterly ($1,500 upfront) — unlimited campaigns and creators
- **Agency**: $800/month billed quarterly ($2,400 upfront)
- **Marketplace fee**: 10% on creator payments (covers payment processing)
- Creator payments separate: ~$100+ for UGC video, ~$125+ for nano-influencer posts

**Creator pool**: 20,000–75,500+ vetted creators from 35+ countries. Strong on TikTok and Instagram. Supports UGC, influencer posts, and whitelisted/Spark Ad content.

**Key features**:
- **TikTok Spark Ads integration** — creators provide Spark Ad codes directly through platform chat, enabling brands to boost creator posts in TikTok Ads Manager without file transfers
- **Meta Partnership Ads** support
- Campaign brief broadcasting
- Content approval workflow
- Creator whitelisting licenses (run ads from creator handles)
- Direct chat with creators
- Shopify integration

**Best for**: DTC/e-commerce brands running performance marketing that want both organic creator posts AND the ability to boost those posts as Spark Ads or Partnership Ads. Strong for TikTok-first strategies.

**Pros**:
- Best-in-class TikTok Spark Ads workflow
- Combines UGC + influencer posts + paid amplification in one platform
- Vetted creator quality
- Good for performance marketing teams
- Official TikTok Creative Marketing Partner

**Cons**:
- **$500/month minimum** + quarterly billing = $1,500 upfront commitment
- 10% marketplace fee on top of subscription
- Smaller creator pool than discovery-only tools
- Auto-upgrades from Trial to Brand plan if you don't cancel

**Realistic for a 4-person team with $2K–5K?** **Tight but possible.** $500/month platform + $1,500–3,000 in creator payments + fees = you'd burn through a $5K budget in ~2 months. Works if you're committed to TikTok Spark Ads as a growth strategy.

### 4. Aspire (formerly AspireIQ)

**Model**: SaaS platform (enterprise-leaning)

**How it works**: Full-service influencer marketing platform with AI-powered creator discovery, campaign management, content workflows, payment automation, and analytics. Brands search a massive database, manage relationships CRM-style, and run multi-channel campaigns.

**Pricing**:
- Starts at **$2,000/month with annual commitment** (~$24,000/year)
- No public pricing tiers — requires sales call
- Creator payments separate from platform cost

**Creator pool**: 170+ million influencer profiles in the database, with 1 million opt-in creators in the marketplace. Covers Instagram, YouTube, TikTok, Facebook, Pinterest. Image Recognition AI matches creators to your brand aesthetic.

**Key features**:
- AI-powered creator discovery with visual/aesthetic matching
- 1M opt-in creator marketplace
- Campaign workflow automation (payments, contracts, content reviews)
- Product seeding and gifting management
- Shopify + WooCommerce integration
- Affiliate link and discount code tracking
- Content library for reuse

**Best for**: Mid-size to enterprise e-commerce brands running ongoing influencer programs with 10+ creators at a time. Strong for ambassador and affiliate programs.

**Pros**:
- Massive creator database
- Sophisticated automation and workflow tools
- Strong e-commerce integrations
- Good for scaling from 10 to 100+ creator relationships

**Cons**:
- **$2,000/month minimum with annual lock-in** — way too expensive for a $2K–5K test budget
- Overkill for small teams running 2–5 creator partnerships
- Sales-gated pricing (no self-serve)
- Steep learning curve

**Realistic for a 4-person team with $2K–5K?** **No.** The platform fee alone exceeds the entire budget. Aspire is for brands spending $50K+/year on influencer marketing.

**Creator pool**: 190+ million influencer profiles across Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube. No opt-in marketplace — brands search and do outreach directly.

**Key features**:
- Deep e-commerce integrations (Shopify, WooCommerce, Magento)
- CRM dashboard (logs emails, DMs, Slack messages)
- Automated follow-ups, contract management, content deadlines
- Product shipment management with unique discount codes
- Sales attribution and ROI tracking
- Content rights management

**Best for**: DTC e-commerce companies with established influencer programs. Designed for brands that ship physical products and want to track influencer-driven sales.

**Pros**:
- Best-in-class e-commerce integration
- Strong CRM and relationship management
- Detailed ROI attribution
- Handles product seeding logistics

**Cons**:
- **Very expensive** — custom pricing but consistently reported as high-cost
- Rigid 12-month contracts
- Overkill for app marketing (built for DTC product companies)
- No self-serve option
- Sales-gated everything

**Realistic for a 4-person team with $2K–5K?** **No.** Built for e-commerce companies with larger budgets and physical product seeding. Not a fit for a mobile app with a small test budget.

### 7. Upfluence

**Model**: SaaS platform

**How it works**: Influencer search engine + campaign management platform. Brands discover influencers through detailed filtering, manage campaigns, and integrate with their e-commerce and CRM tools. Unique feature: identifies influential people among your existing customers.

**Pricing**:
- Starts at **~$478/month** (various modules priced separately)
- **Mandatory 12-month commitment**
- No public pricing page — requires demo call
- Pricing reported as "high, often higher than teams expect"

**Creator pool**: Database across Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, Twitter, Pinterest, Twitch, and blogs. Exact size not publicly stated but extensive. Supports audience demographic filtering and engagement rate analysis.

**Key features**:
- Powerful search engine with detailed demographic filters
- Identifies influencers among your existing customers (via Shopify/WooCommerce/Klaviyo integration)
- Content library auto-compilation (Reels, Stories, Shorts, posts)
- Jaice AI assistant for managing collaborations
- Campaign management and automated outreach
- Payment processing

**Best for**: E-commerce brands that want to activate their own customers as influencers. Strong for brands with existing Shopify stores and email lists.

**Pros**:
- Unique "find influencers in your customer base" feature
- Comprehensive search and filtering
- Multi-platform coverage including Twitch
- AI assistant for workflow management

**Cons**:
- **12-month mandatory commitment** at ~$478+/month
- Opaque pricing — no transparency until sales call
- Reports of drawn-out sales process
- Not ideal for apps without existing e-commerce customers

**Realistic for a 4-person team with $2K–5K?** **No.** The 12-month lock-in at $478+/month = $5,700+ just for the platform, leaving nothing for creator payments. Also built for e-commerce, not app marketing.

### 8. #paid

**Model**: Creator marketplace (managed)

**How it works**: Brands post campaign briefs, and creators "handraise" — they pitch why they're a fit with a creative concept. The platform's AI (Affinity Score) matches creators to campaigns. Content is reviewed, approved, and posted through the platform. Designed for authentic sponsored posts on creator feeds.

**Pricing**:
- **Just Content**: $499/month
- **Grow**: $699/month (2 creator whitelisting licenses)
- **Advanced**: $999/month (15 handraised creators per campaign, dedicated Customer Success Manager)
- **Custom**: for larger brands and agencies
- All plans include unlimited campaigns
- Creator payments are separate (cost-per-engagement model with fixed CPE per influencer)

**Creator pool**: Not publicly stated but described as a curated network. Covers Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, Facebook. Focuses on quality over quantity — creators must pitch to participate.

**Key features**:
- Creator "handraise" model — creators pitch why they're a fit (you don't cold-search)
- Affinity Score (image recognition + ML for ideal creator matching)
- Content approval and scheduling
- Creator whitelisting (run ads from creator accounts)
- Campaign benchmarks from 30K+ respondents
- Multi-user permissions for team collaboration

**Best for**: Brands that want high-quality sponsored feed posts where creators develop their own creative concepts. The handraise model ensures creators are genuinely interested in your brand.

**Pros**:
- Creators pitch to you (higher quality, more authentic content)
- Strong creative concept development
- Good for sponsored feed posts specifically
- Whitelisting capability

**Cons**:
- **$499/month minimum** + creator payments on top
- Smaller, more curated network (less volume)
- Fixed CPE model means less pricing flexibility
- Higher-end positioning may not match small app budgets

**Realistic for a 4-person team with $2K–5K?** **Marginal.** $499/month leaves $1,500–4,500 for creators over 1 month. Could work for a focused one-month test, but the platform fee eats into a tight budget.

### 9. Heepsy

**Model**: Discovery/search SaaS tool + marketplace

**How it works**: Primarily an influencer search engine. Brands search and filter a database of 11M+ influencer profiles by location, niche, audience demographics, engagement rate, and authenticity score. Also has a marketplace where brands post campaigns and creators apply.

**Pricing**:
- **Starter**: $89/month (15K search results, 50 profile analyses, 50 outreach emails)
- **Plus**: $249/month
- **Advanced**: $369/month
- Free tier available for basic browsing

**Creator pool**: 11+ million profiles on Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, and Twitch. Global coverage with location-based filtering.

**Key features**:
- Advanced search filters (location, category, audience demographics, engagement)
- Audience Authenticity Filter (fake follower detection)
- Influencer analytics reports (reach, engagement rate, avg likes/comments/views)
- Campaign marketplace (brands post, creators apply)
- Outreach email integration
- Cost estimator

**Best for**: Brands that want to find and evaluate influencers themselves, then negotiate deals directly. Great as a discovery tool paired with manual outreach.

**Pros**:
- Most affordable SaaS option ($89/month entry)
- Large, global database
- Strong authenticity/fraud detection
- Good filters for finding niche creators
- No long-term commitment required

**Cons**:
- **Primarily a search tool, not a campaign management platform**
- No payment escrow or contract management
- No content approval workflow
- You handle outreach, negotiation, contracts, and payment yourself
- No Spark Ads or whitelisting integration

**Realistic for a 4-person team with $2K–5K?** **Yes** — $89/month is very affordable for discovery. But you'll need to handle all outreach, contracts, and payments manually. Best paired with direct DM/email outreach.

### 10. TikTok Creator Marketplace (TTCM / TikTok One)

**Model**: Official first-party marketplace (free platform)

**How it works**: TikTok's official platform connecting brands with TikTok creators. Brands browse creator profiles, view performance data, and initiate collaborations. Creators post sponsored content on their own TikTok feeds. Now accessible through **TikTok One**, TikTok's unified creative suite.

**Pricing**:
- **Platform is free** — no subscription fee
- You pay creators directly through TTCM Pay
- Typical creator rates:
  - Nano (1K–10K followers): $5–$25/post
  - Micro (10K–100K): $25–$125/post
  - Mid-tier (100K–500K): $125–$1,200/post
  - Macro (500K–1M): $1,200–$2,500/post
  - Mega (1M+): $2,500+/post

**Requirements**:
- **Brands**: Need a TikTok Business account
- **Creators**: 10,000+ followers, 1,000+ views in last 30 days, 18+ years old, creator account type
- Currently prioritizes US-based creators (expanding globally)

**Creator pool**: All eligible TikTok creators who opt in. Massive pool but limited to TikTok only. Real performance data directly from TikTok (not estimated).

**Key features**:
- **First-party data** — actual reach, engagement, and audience demographics from TikTok (most accurate available)
- TTCM Pay for secure payments
- Content review and approval
- Direct integration with TikTok Ads Manager for Spark Ads
- Creator performance history and past campaign data
- Free to use

**Best for**: Any brand running TikTok influencer campaigns. The most accurate data source for TikTok creator performance. Ideal for Spark Ads campaigns.

**Pros**:
- **Free platform** — zero subscription cost
- Most accurate TikTok creator data (first-party)
- Direct Spark Ads integration
- Built-in payment processing
- Large creator pool

**Cons**:
- **TikTok only** — no Instagram, YouTube, etc.
- Creator minimum: 10K followers (excludes nano-influencers)
- Interface can be clunky
- Less sophisticated search/filter than third-party tools
- Limited campaign management features
- Some creators don't opt in (missing from marketplace)

**Realistic for a 4-person team with $2K–5K?** **Yes — this should be your first stop for TikTok.** Free platform means 100% of budget goes to creators. At $25–125/post for micro-influencers, $2K–5K gets you 16–200 creator posts.

**Creator pool**: 250M+ creator profiles across Instagram, YouTube, and TikTok. Largest database available. No creator sign-up required — public data indexed automatically.

**Key features**:
- Largest creator discovery database (250M+)
- Audience demographics and authenticity analysis
- Email contact unlocking
- Content tracking and collection
- Performance reporting
- Product gifting workflow
- Discount codes and affiliate links
- Payments handling
- Shopify integration

**Pros**:

## Strategy Notes

### Why Sponsored Feed Posts (not just UGC)

For Zerocap specifically, sponsored feed posts are more valuable than UGC because:
- **Social proof**: A creator's followers see them using the app, creating organic discovery
- **Algorithm amplification**: TikTok/IG algorithms can push creator posts to non-followers
- **Credibility transfer**: When a creator posts about Zerocap on their own feed, it carries their personal endorsement
- **Spark Ads potential**: The best-performing organic posts can be boosted as paid ads while retaining all the social proof (likes, comments)

### Recommended Approach for $2K–5K

1. **Start with TTCM** (free) — run 5–10 TikTok creator posts at $25–100 each. Total: $250–1,000
2. **Use Collabstr** for Instagram coverage — book 5–8 micro-influencer IG posts/Reels at ~$150–250 each. Total: $750–2,000
3. **Boost winners with Spark Ads** — take the top 2–3 performing TikTok posts and put $500–1,500 behind them as Spark Ads
4. **Optional: Heepsy** for discovery if TTCM and Collabstr don't surface the right niche creators — $89/month for targeted searching

### Creator Brief Tips for Zerocap

- Frame the brief around **"meet my new AI pet"** or **"this app raised my pet from scratch"** — authentic reaction content
- Let creators show the **emotional connection** (naming their pet, watching it grow, interacting with it)
- **Don't over-script** — TikTok/IG algorithms punish content that feels like an ad
- Include a **clear CTA** (download link in bio, promo code) but keep it natural
- Request **vertical video** for TikTok and Reels
- Look for creators in: virtual pets, Tamagotchi nostalgia, AI/tech, cozy gaming, digital lifestyle, casual gaming

### Key Metrics to Track

- **CPD (Cost per Download)**: Total spend / app installs attributed to campaign
- **Engagement rate**: Likes + comments + shares / views on each creator post
- **View-through rate**: How many people watch past 3 seconds
- **Spark Ad ROAS**: Revenue or installs from boosted creator posts vs. ad spend
- **Creator conversion rate**: Which creator tiers and niches drive the most installs
