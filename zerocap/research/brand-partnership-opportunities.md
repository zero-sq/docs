# Brand Partnership & IP Collaboration Opportunities for Zerocap

Research date: 2026-03-13

Zerocap is an iOS AI companion/tamagotchi app where you point your camera at real-world objects and AI transforms them into cute living pets with personality traits (6 million unique trait combinations). This research analyzes how the "scan real objects" mechanic creates a uniquely powerful brand partnership vehicle -- and how to monetize it from pre-scale through mass adoption.

---

## 1. The "Scan Real Objects" Mechanic as a Brand Partnership Vehicle

Zerocap's core mechanic -- point your camera at a real-world object, AI transforms it into a living pet -- is fundamentally different from traditional in-app advertising. The brand's product becomes the *input* to a delightful experience. The user isn't watching an ad; they're playing with the brand.

This inverts the typical brand partnership dynamic. Instead of the app interrupting the user experience with sponsored content, the brand's product *is* the user experience. That distinction matters enormously for partnership conversations.

### 1.1 Food & Beverage Brands

Food is the highest-performing content category for Zerocap's TikTok GTM strategy (see `tiktok-gtm/content-concepts.md`). Food-themed pets are visually rich, culturally relevant, and ride existing social media trends.

**Why food brands should care:**
- Food content dominates TikTok discovery -- Dave's Hot Chicken alone has 200M+ TikTok views
- "Scan your meal" is a natural behavior for Gen Z (they already photograph food before eating it)
- A food-themed pet is inherently shareable -- "my Starbucks latte turned into a pet that's an introvert who loves jazz"
- The brand gets organic UGC every time someone scans their product

**Target partners and mechanics:**

| Brand | Mechanic | Why It Works |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| **McDonald's** | Scan your Happy Meal / McFlurry / Big Mac | McDonald's pioneered in-app gaming partnerships (Happy Meal toys, Pokemon GO sponsored PokeStops in Japan). They understand digital engagement. CosMc's experimental concept signals willingness to innovate. |
| **Starbucks** | Scan your seasonal drink (Pumpkin Spice, Pink Drink) | Starbucks was one of Pokemon GO's first sponsored location partners (7,800 US stores became PokeStops/Gyms in 2016). They actively seek digital experiences that drive foot traffic and social sharing. |
| **Boba / milk tea shops** (Kung Fu Tea, Tiger Sugar, The Alley, Gong Cha) | Scan your boba tea | Boba is identity content for Gen Z. The straw-piercing-the-seal moment is already a TikTok audio staple. Boba chains are aggressively expanding and open to digital partnerships. |
| **Dave's Hot Chicken** | Scan your chicken sandwich | Already in our content pipeline (see `daves-hot-chicken-video-brief.md`). 200M+ TikTok views, 4M followers, Gen Z cult favorite. An organic video becomes a partnership proof-of-concept. |

**The partnership pitch for food brands:** "Your customers are already scanning your products with our app. We can make that official -- exclusive limited-edition pets that only appear when scanning your packaging, driving foot traffic and social sharing."

## 2. IP Collaboration Models

### 2.1 Licensed Characters as Scannable Pets

The most direct IP partnership: a recognizable character becomes a Zerocap pet you can interact with, care for, and collect. Unlike static merchandise, the AI personality layer makes each licensed pet feel alive.

**How it works:**
1. IP holder licenses character likeness to Zerocap
2. Zerocap creates a "pet version" of the character (chibi/cute style) with a personality matching the character's canon traits
3. Users unlock the character pet through a special scan mechanic (scan the movie poster, scan the toy, scan a QR code on packaging)
4. The character pet lives in the user's collection alongside their organic pets

**High-value IP targets:**

| IP | Why | Scan Trigger |
|----|-----|-------------|
| **Sanrio** (Hello Kitty, Cinnamoroll, My Melody) | Perfect aesthetic overlap with Zerocap's cute style. Sanrio licensing is a $5B+ annual business. They actively license to apps and games. | Scan Sanrio merchandise, stores, or packaging |
| **Pokemon** | The original "gotta catch 'em all" collectible creatures. Pokemon GO proved the camera-creatures mechanic at $8B+ scale. | Would require Nintendo/TPC partnership (aspirational, long-term) |
| **Disney / Pixar characters** | Universal recognition. Disney actively licenses to mobile games (Disney Dreamlight Valley, Disney Mirrorverse). | Scan Disney merchandise, movie posters, theme park items |
| **Studio Ghibli** | The Ghibli AI filter (March 2025) generated 700M+ images in one week -- proving massive demand for "Ghibli-style cute things." | Scan themed merchandise or limited-time activation |
| **Demon Slayer / One Piece / Jujutsu Kaisen** | Anime IP licensing to Tamagotchi has been hugely successful (Bandai released dedicated Demon Slayer, Evangelion, and One Piece Tamagotchi devices). Proven audience overlap. | Scan manga volumes, anime merchandise |
| **Squishmallows** | The dominant collectible brand. 1B+ units sold. The physical-to-digital bridge is a natural extension of the collecting behavior. | Scan your Squishmallow |

**Licensing economics for a pre-scale app:**
- Minimum guarantee (MG): $5K-$50K for small/indie game licenses from mid-tier IP holders
- Royalty rate: Typically 5-15% of net revenue attributable to the licensed content
- Term: Usually 1-3 years with renewal options
- For major IP (Disney, Pokemon): Likely out of reach pre-scale; approach after demonstrating 100K+ MAU and strong engagement metrics
- For mid-tier IP (indie anime, emerging collectible brands): Accessible at lower MGs; they benefit from exposure to your audience

### 2.2 Co-Branded Limited-Edition Pets

More accessible than full licensing, co-branding means creating *new* pet designs inspired by a partner brand's aesthetic, without directly replicating copyrighted characters.

**Examples:**
- A coffee-themed pet series in Starbucks seasonal colors (no copyrighted characters used, just the aesthetic)
- A sneaker-inspired pet collection with colorways matching popular shoe releases (no logos, just inspired designs)
- A "spicy" pet collection themed around hot sauce brands (red palette, fire personality traits)

**Why co-branding is the right entry point for Zerocap:**
- Lower legal barrier -- no trademark licensing required
- Faster execution -- no approval chain through corporate IP departments
- The brand gets custom content; Zerocap gets partnership credibility
- Can be structured as a marketing spend for the brand (not a licensing deal)

### 2.3 Event-Based Collaborations

Time-limited partnerships tied to cultural moments create urgency and drive engagement spikes.

| Event Type | Example | Mechanic |
|------------|---------|----------|
| **Movie releases** | Scan your movie ticket/poster for exclusive themed pets | A pet themed to the movie's aesthetic (not the characters themselves, unless licensed) |
| **Product launches** | New sneaker drop = limited-edition sneaker pet available for 72 hours | Scarcity drives FOMO; social sharing of the rare pet markets the product |
| **Holidays / seasons** | Halloween pets, Valentine's pets, Lunar New Year pets | Standard live-ops, but co-branded versions with partners add revenue |
| **Music releases** | Album drop = scan the album art for a themed pet | Artists already market through apps (Travis Scott x Fortnite generated $20M+) |
| **Food launches** | PepsiCo Flavor Swap chips launch (already social-first on TikTok Shop) = scan the new chip bag | Food launches are increasingly digital-first; Zerocap offers a novel activation |
| **Festivals / events** | SXSW, ComplexCon, Coachella = location-exclusive pets | Drives foot traffic to the event, gives attendees a digital souvenir |

## 3. How Companion/Pet Apps Have Done Brand Partnerships Historically

### 3.1 Pokemon GO: The Gold Standard ($8B+ Revenue)

Pokemon GO pioneered the model of monetizing real-world locations through digital engagement. Key partnership models:

**Sponsored Locations (2016-present):**
- McDonald's Japan: First sponsor (July 2016). All ~3,000 McDonald's Japan locations became PokeStops and Gyms. McDonald's Japan paid Niantic on a per-visit basis (estimated $0.15-$0.50 per visit). Result: McDonald's Japan reported a 26% increase in store visits from Pokemon GO players in the first month.
- Starbucks US: 7,800 Starbucks locations became PokeStops/Gyms (December 2016). Co-created a Pokemon GO Frappuccino exclusive drink. The partnership drove foot traffic and social media content simultaneously.
- Sprint/T-Mobile: 10,500 Sprint stores became PokeStops. After Sprint merged with T-Mobile, the partnership continued.
- 7-Eleven (Mexico, Japan): Convenience store locations became PokeStops.
- Verizon: Replaced Sprint as US telecom sponsor.

**Sponsored In-Game Items:**
- Branded stickers and gifts that players send to friends (sponsored by Samsung, Verizon, Circle K)
- Sponsored Research tasks (complete brand-themed tasks for in-game rewards)
- Sponsored Balloons (brand-themed hot air balloons appear on the map)

**Revenue model:** Brands paid per impression (CPM model) or per store visit (cost-per-visit model). Niantic reportedly charged $0.15-$0.50 per sponsored interaction, with major deals running $1M-$10M annually per sponsor.

**Key insight for Zerocap:** Pokemon GO proved that brands will pay significant sums to be integrated into gameplay -- *if* the integration feels natural and drives measurable foot traffic. Zerocap's scan mechanic is an even more natural brand integration than a PokeStop, because the user is literally engaging with the brand's physical product.

### 3.2 Tamagotchi: IP Crossover Mastery

Bandai Namco turned Tamagotchi into a collaboration machine. The device's simple format (a small screen with a character to care for) makes it easy to reskin for any IP:

**Anime/Entertainment IP Tamagotchis:**
- Demon Slayer (Kimetsu no Yaiba) Tamagotchi -- released 2020, became a bestseller
- Evangelion Tamagotchi -- raise an Angel
- One Piece Tamagotchi -- care for a Going Merry crew member
- Star Wars Tamagotchi (R2-D2 edition, Grogu/Baby Yoda edition) -- Disney/Lucasfilm license
- Jujutsu Kaisen Tamagotchi
- Spy x Family Tamagotchi
- BT21 Tamagotchi (BTS characters)
- Pac-Man Tamagotchi (internal Bandai Namco IP)
- Hello Kitty Tamagotchi (Sanrio crossover)
- Twisted Wonderland Tamagotchi (Disney Japan mobile game)

**Fashion/Brand Collaborations:**
- Uniqlo x Tamagotchi collaboration T-shirts
- Gucci x Tamagotchi (discussed in fashion press, limited edition concept)
- Various streetwear collaborations for the 25th anniversary

**Revenue model:** Typically structured as co-branded limited-edition hardware. The IP holder licenses the characters; Bandai manufactures and sells the device. Revenue split: Bandai keeps manufacturing margin, pays royalty (typically 8-15%) to the IP holder. Retail price premium: Co-branded Tamagotchis sell for $20-$30 (vs. $15-$20 for standard models), with some limited editions reaching $60+.

**Key insight for Zerocap:** Tamagotchi proves that the virtual pet format is endlessly reskinnable for IP collaborations. Zerocap has an even stronger proposition -- digital pets can be updated instantly (no manufacturing), distributed globally (no supply chain), and themed to trending cultural moments in real-time.

### 3.3 Peridot (Niantic): The Cautionary Tale

Peridot launched with a TikTok-first marketing strategy (partnering with Gravity Road agency) and Niantic's built-in location expertise, but struggled to attract brand partners.

**What happened:**
- Launch generated 124M TikTok views via hashtags, but downloads dropped to ~10.5K/month by Q4 2023
- No major brand partnerships materialized publicly
- The game lacked a natural brand integration point -- Peridot's creatures were random, not derived from real-world objects
- Pivoted toward Snap Spectacles (AR glasses) integration rather than brand sponsorships

**Key insight for Zerocap:** Peridot failed at brand partnerships because the gameplay had no natural connection to physical products or locations. Zerocap's "scan a real product" mechanic is the missing link that makes brand integration organic rather than forced.

### 3.4 Talking Tom (Outfit7): Advertising-First Monetization

The Talking Tom franchise (26B+ downloads) monetized primarily through in-app advertising rather than brand partnerships:

- Heavy interstitial and rewarded video ads via ad networks
- The character became IP itself (YouTube animated series: 32B+ views)
- Limited formal brand partnerships, but the IP was licensed for merchandise (toys, clothing, accessories)

**Key insight:** At massive scale, even "dumb" advertising (interstitials) generates enormous revenue. But brand partnerships are higher margin and more user-friendly than interstitial ads.

### 3.5 Character.AI: The AI Companion Partnership Model

Character.AI (20M MAU, $32.2M revenue in 2024) has explored brand partnerships through licensed character personas -- users can chat with AI versions of fictional characters. While not a pet app, it demonstrates that AI-powered character interactions are a viable partnership vehicle.

## 4. Revenue Models for Brand Partnerships

### 4.1 Sponsorship (Flat Fee)

The brand pays a fixed fee for a defined partnership scope.

| Tier | What the Brand Gets | Price Range | Best For |
|------|---------------------|-------------|----------|
| **Nano** | Social media mention, branded content video featuring their product being scanned | $500-$5,000 | Local businesses, micro-brands |
| **Small** | Limited-edition co-branded pet available for 1-4 weeks, promoted in-app and on social | $5,000-$25,000 | Mid-size DTC brands, regional chains |
| **Medium** | Exclusive branded pet collection, in-app event, co-marketing campaign, social amplification | $25,000-$100,000 | National brands, major food/bev chains |
| **Large** | Deep integration: branded scan mechanic, exclusive pet line, co-branded AR experience, cross-platform promotion | $100,000-$500,000+ | Fortune 500, major entertainment IP |

**When to use:** Pre-scale. Flat fees are easier to negotiate when you can't guarantee impressions. Frame it as "we'll create X pieces of content featuring your brand" rather than "we'll deliver Y impressions."

### 4.2 Revenue Share

The brand earns a percentage of revenue generated from branded content.

- **In-app purchases:** If branded pets are sold as premium items ($0.99-$4.99), the brand receives 15-30% of net revenue (after Apple's 30% cut)
- **Subscription uplift:** If branded content drives new subscriptions, attribute a share to the partner
- **Example math:** 10,000 users buy a $2.99 branded pet = $29,900 gross. After Apple 30% cut = $20,930 net. At 20% partner share = $4,186 to the brand.

**When to use:** Post-scale, when you have proven conversion rates and can project revenue confidently.

### 4.3 Licensing Fees (for IP Collaborations)

Zerocap pays the IP holder for the right to use their characters.

| Component | Typical Range | Notes |
|-----------|---------------|-------|
| **Minimum guarantee (MG)** | $5,000-$500,000+ | Depends on IP tier; indie/emerging IP can be $5K-$25K; major IP (Disney) is $100K+ |
| **Royalty rate** | 5-15% of attributable net revenue | Paid quarterly, against the MG |
| **Advance** | 50% of MG upfront, 50% at launch | Standard licensing structure |
| **Term** | 1-3 years | With renewal options |
| **Territory** | Usually worldwide for digital | Physical merchandise may be territory-restricted |

**When to use:** When the IP is strong enough to drive measurable user acquisition or revenue uplift that exceeds the licensing cost.

### 4.4 Hybrid: "Free" Partnerships with Mutual Value

The most realistic model for a pre-scale app. No money changes hands; both parties contribute value.

- Zerocap creates branded content featuring the partner's product
- The partner promotes the content to their audience (social media cross-promotion, newsletter mention, in-store signage)
- Both parties get exposure to each other's audience

**This is the starting point.** Every major brand partnership started with a free proof-of-concept that demonstrated value.

## 5. How Early-Stage Apps (Pre-Scale) Can Land Brand Partnerships

### 5.1 The Traction Threshold

Brands generally start paying attention to app partnerships at these thresholds:

| Metric | "Interesting" | "Investable" | "Priority" |
|--------|--------------|--------------|-----------|
| **MAU** | 10,000 | 50,000 | 500,000+ |
| **Social following** | 10,000 | 50,000 | 500,000+ |
| **Engagement rate** | >5% | >8% | >10% |
| **Demo match** | Overlaps with brand's target | Strong overlap | Perfect alignment |

Below 10K MAU, you're not pitching impressions -- you're pitching the *story* and the *mechanic*.

### 5.2 Micro-Partnerships with Local Businesses

The entry point for pre-scale partnerships. Local businesses are accessible, decision-making is fast, and the stakes are low for both sides.

**Playbook:**

1. **Walk into a photogenic local business** (boba shop, artisan bakery, trendy restaurant, sneaker boutique)
2. **Scan their products on camera** -- create a TikTok/Reels video showing their product transforming into a pet
3. **Tag the business** -- most local businesses actively monitor their tags and will engage
4. **Offer to make it official** -- "We'd love to create a limited-edition pet inspired by your shop. We'll make content featuring your products and tag you. In exchange, put a small sign/QR code in your shop that says 'Scan your [product] with Zerocap'"
5. **Document the results** -- views, engagement, new followers for both parties

**Why local businesses say yes:**
- Zero cost to them
- Novel content they can repost
- Tech-forward image (great for social media)
- No legal/approval process -- the owner decides on the spot

**Target local partners:**
- Boba tea shops (The Alley, Tiger Sugar, Kung Fu Tea locations)
- Artisan coffee shops (photogenic latte art = great scan subjects)
- Independent sneaker stores
- Trendy dessert spots (Crumbl Cookies, specialty ice cream, matcha shops)
- Local pet stores (meta-partnership: scan pet products to make AI pets)

### 5.3 Creator Collaborations as Proto-Partnerships

Creators who feature branded products in Zerocap content are effectively running unsponsored brand campaigns. This creates a paper trail of brand engagement that can be leveraged in partnership pitches.

**The pipeline:**
1. Creator scans a brand's product in their Zerocap video
2. Video performs well (brand gets free exposure)
3. Track and screenshot the performance data
4. Approach the brand: "Your product was featured in X Zerocap videos, generating Y views and Z engagement. Users love turning your products into pets. Let's make it official."

This converts *organic* brand content into *evidence* for a paid partnership conversation.

### 5.4 The "Dave's Hot Chicken Video" Approach

Already in Zerocap's GTM pipeline (see `daves-hot-chicken-video-brief.md`). The strategy:

1. Create high-quality Zerocap content featuring a trending brand's product (Dave's Hot Chicken, PepsiCo Flavor Swap, etc.)
2. Post organically -- the brand name in the content is algorithmic fuel
3. If the video performs well, the brand may notice organically (their social listening tools will flag it)
4. Regardless, use the content as a proof-of-concept: "Here's what a Zerocap x [Brand] activation looks like. It got X views."
5. Approach the brand with a formal partnership proposal

**This is the most capital-efficient way to build a brand partnership portfolio.** You create the content first, demonstrate value, then monetize the relationship.

**Brands to target with this approach (Q2 2026):**

## 6. UGC as Partnership Leverage

### 6.1 Users Are Already Scanning Branded Objects Organically

This is Zerocap's single biggest partnership asset. Because the core mechanic is "scan any real-world object," users will inevitably scan branded products -- McDonald's wrappers, Starbucks cups, Nike shoes, iPhone cases -- without any partnership in place.

**Why this matters for partnership pitches:**
- You can show brands *actual user behavior* featuring their products
- The engagement is authentic (not paid, not prompted)
- Every scanned branded object is implicit demand for a partnership
- The data tells you which brands users associate with Zerocap

**How to capture and leverage this data:**
1. Track which objects users scan most frequently (by category and brand recognition)
2. Create a "brand scan leaderboard" -- which brands appear most in user-generated pets?
3. Use this data in partnership pitches: "Your product was scanned X times last month by Y unique users, generating Z social shares"
4. This is zero-cost market research that no other app can replicate

### 6.2 The UGC Flywheel

```
User scans brand product → Creates shareable pet → Posts on TikTok/Instagram
     ↓
Brand's social listening picks it up → Brand sees organic engagement
     ↓
Brand approaches Zerocap (or Zerocap approaches brand with data)
     ↓
Official partnership → Exclusive branded pets → More users scan brand products
     ↓
More UGC → More brand visibility → More partnerships
```

This flywheel is self-reinforcing. The more users scan branded objects, the more compelling the partnership pitch becomes. And the more partnerships you have, the more reasons users have to scan branded objects.

### 6.3 Making UGC Partnership-Ready

Design decisions that maximize UGC's value as partnership leverage:

| Feature | Why It Matters |
|---------|---------------|
| **Object recognition tags** | When a user scans a recognizable branded product, tag it internally (even if you don't show the tag to the user). This builds the data set for partnership pitches. |
| **Share templates** | "I turned my [brand] into a pet!" auto-generated share text that includes the brand name. This creates trackable organic mentions. |
| **Watermarked exports** | Every shared pet image/video has a subtle Zerocap watermark. When a brand sees their product in a viral Zerocap video, they know exactly who to call. |
| **Collection pages** | Users can organize pets by source. "My food pets" or "my sneaker pets" creates themed collections that brands can sponsor. |

## 7. Partnership Pitch Framework

### 7.1 The One-Pager Structure

Every brand pitch should fit on a single page. Decision-makers at brands skim -- give them the value prop in 30 seconds.

```
[ZEROCAP x BRAND NAME] Partnership Proposal

THE MECHANIC
Your customers point their phone at [Brand Product]. AI transforms it into a
cute living pet with its own personality. They care for it, name it, and share
it on social media -- with your brand front and center.

THE OPPORTUNITY
- [X] users have already scanned [Brand] products organically
- Zerocap pet content averages [Y]% engagement rate on TikTok (vs. 2.5% platform average)
- [Relevant data point about the brand's target demo matching Zerocap's]

THE ACTIVATION
- [Tier]: [Description of what the brand gets]
- Timeline: [X weeks]
- Investment: [$ or value exchange]

THE PROOF
- [Link to organic video featuring their product, with view/engagement metrics]
- [Link to similar brand activation with results]

CONTACT
[Name, email, phone]
```

### 7.2 Pitch by Brand Size

**Local businesses (0-3 locations):**
- Walk in, demo the app, show them a video of their product as a pet
- Offer: Free co-branded content in exchange for in-store promotion
- Decision: Made by the owner, usually on the spot
- Timeline: Same week

**Regional chains (4-50 locations):**
- Email the marketing manager with a 30-second demo video
- Offer: Limited-edition pet series + social content package ($2,500-$10,000)
- Decision: Marketing manager + owner approval, 2-4 weeks
- Timeline: 4-6 weeks from pitch to launch

**National / enterprise brands (50+ locations or DTC brands with national reach):**
- Warm introduction through mutual connection, or cold outreach to brand partnerships / digital innovation team
- Offer: Full partnership proposal with tiered options ($25,000-$100,000+)
- Decision: Brand partnerships team + legal + marketing, 2-6 months
- Timeline: 3-6 months from pitch to launch

**IP holders (entertainment, anime, collectibles):**
- Approach through licensing agent or direct to licensing department
- Offer: Digital licensing deal (MG + royalty) with product mockups
- Decision: Licensing team + legal + creative approval, 3-12 months
- Timeline: 6-12 months from pitch to launch

### 7.3 What to Include in a Pitch Deck (When You Need More Than a One-Pager)

1. **The product demo** (30-second video of the scan-to-pet transformation)
2. **Market context** (AR market: $120B in 2025, growing at 29.7% CAGR to $1T+ by 2033; AI companion apps: $120M revenue in 2025)
3. **Audience data** (demographics, engagement rates, social reach)
4. **The mechanic explained** (how scanning their product works, what the branded pet looks like)
5. **Partnership examples/mockups** (show what their branded pet could look like)
6. **Organic proof** (if users have already scanned their products, show the data)
7. **Pricing/options** (tiered, from co-marketing to full licensing)
8. **Comparable partnerships** (Pokemon GO x Starbucks, Tamagotchi x Demon Slayer, etc.)

## 9. Legal Considerations

### 9.1 Trademark and the "Scan Real Products" Question

When users scan branded products with Zerocap, two legal questions arise:

**Q: Can users scan trademarked products without the brand's permission?**

A: Yes, with caveats.

- **Nominative fair use** allows referencing a trademark to identify the product (e.g., "I scanned my Nike shoes"). This is similar to how a restaurant review app can photograph and identify a McDonald's burger.
- **The key distinction:** Zerocap doesn't display the brand's trademark on the pet. The user scans a Nike shoe; the AI creates a shoe-inspired pet that doesn't use Nike's logo, name, or protected design elements. This is transformative -- the output is a new creative work inspired by the physical object's shape and colors.
- **Recommendation:** The AI generation pipeline should strip/abstract recognizable logos and brand identifiers from the pet design. The pet should be "inspired by" the object, not a reproduction of it.

**Q: Can Zerocap commercially benefit from user-generated scans of branded products?**

A: With appropriate safeguards.

- If a user scans a Starbucks cup and Zerocap generates a green cup-themed pet, Zerocap is not *using* Starbucks' trademark -- the user chose the input, and the AI created a transformative output.
- **Do not:** Name the pet "Starbucks Pet" or display the Starbucks logo on the pet.
- **Do:** Allow the user to name their own pet whatever they want (including brand names -- that's their speech, not Zerocap's).
- **Risk level:** Low, as long as Zerocap itself doesn't associate brand names with the generated pets in any official or commercial capacity.

### 9.2 Likeness Rights for Scanning Real Products

- Products do not have "likeness rights." A shoe is an object, not a person. You can photograph, scan, and create derivative works from the visual appearance of physical objects.
- **Exception:** Distinctive trade dress. If a product's overall visual appearance is protected as trade dress (e.g., the Coca-Cola bottle shape), creating a pet that is clearly a replica of that trade dress could raise issues. Solution: The AI should abstract the design enough that it's inspired-by, not a copy of.
- **Exception:** Patented designs. Ornamental design patents (e.g., specific iPhone form factor) could theoretically be infringed by a detailed reproduction. Again, the transformative nature of the pet conversion makes this very low risk.

### 9.3 Licensed Character Partnerships: What You Need

For formal IP collaborations, you'll need:

| Document | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| **Licensing agreement** | Defines scope, territory, term, royalties, MG, approval rights |
| **Style guide** | IP holder provides guidelines for how their characters can be depicted |
| **Approval process** | Typically 2-3 rounds of creative review before launch |
| **Quality assurance** | IP holder may require app review before featuring their characters |
| **Indemnification** | Zerocap indemnifies IP holder against claims arising from the app |
| **Insurance** | Many IP holders require errors & omissions (E&O) insurance ($1M-$5M) |

### 9.4 User-Generated Content and Brand Liability

- Users posting "I scanned my McDonald's Happy Meal and made a pet!" is their free speech, not Zerocap's commercial use of McDonald's trademark.
- Zerocap should not *encourage* users to scan specific brands (unless formally partnered). The app says "scan any object," not "scan your McDonald's."
- Once a partnership exists, Zerocap can explicitly say "Scan your McDonald's Happy Meal for an exclusive pet!" -- because the partnership grants that right.

### 9.5 Best Practices Summary

| Practice | Why |
|----------|-----|
| **Don't use brand names in generated pet names/descriptions** | Avoids trademark use claims |
| **Don't reproduce logos on pets** | Logos are the most protected brand element |
| **Do abstract designs into pet-inspired aesthetics** | Transformative use is protected |
| **Do let users freely share their brand-scanning content** | User speech, not commercial use |
| **Do pursue formal partnerships before any official brand integration** | Licensing agreement protects both parties |
| **Do get E&O insurance before signing IP licensing deals** | Standard requirement, protects Zerocap |

## 10. How Partnerships Affect App Store Featuring and Discovery

### 10.1 Apple's Featuring Criteria

Apple's App Store editorial team selects featured apps based on:

1. **User experience** -- cohesive, efficient, valuable functionality
2. **UI design** -- usability, visual appeal, intuitive controls
3. **Innovation** -- new technologies solving unique problems
4. **Uniqueness** -- fresh approach to familiar categories
5. **Accessibility** -- inclusive design for broad user range
6. **Localization** -- multi-language support
7. **App Store product page** -- compelling screenshots, previews, ratings

There is no formal checkbox for "brand partnerships." However, partnerships affect featuring through several indirect channels.

### 10.2 How Partnerships Help Featuring

| Factor | How Partnerships Contribute |
|--------|---------------------------|
| **Innovation signal** | A Zerocap x Nike partnership demonstrates novel use of camera/AI technology -- exactly the kind of innovation Apple highlights |
| **Press coverage** | Brand partnerships generate press, which Apple's editorial team reads. Being in TechCrunch for a Starbucks partnership is a featuring signal. |
| **Download velocity** | Partnerships drive download spikes, which trigger Apple's algorithmic featuring recommendations |
| **Rating/review volume** | More users = more reviews = better product page = higher featuring probability |
| **In-App Events** | Apple's In-App Events feature (visible on the App Store) can showcase partnership-driven events. "Zerocap x [Brand] Limited Pet Collection" appears on the app's product page and in search results. |
| **Cultural relevance** | Apple features apps tied to cultural moments. A partnership timed to a movie release, holiday, or viral trend makes Zerocap editorially relevant. |
| **AR/AI showcase** | Apple invests heavily in ARKit and Core ML. An app that demonstrates compelling real-world AR use (scanning brand products to create AI pets) is exactly what Apple wants to showcase as a platform capability. |

### 10.3 How to Leverage Partnerships for Featuring

1. **Submit featuring nominations** via App Store Connect 3 months before a major partnership launch
2. **Frame the partnership as an Apple technology showcase** -- "This activation uses ARKit + Core ML to transform real-world branded products into AI companions"
3. **Use In-App Events** to surface partnership content on the App Store
4. **Time partnership launches with Apple editorial calendars** (Back to School, Holiday, New Year New You, etc.)
5. **Request a developer story** -- Apple's Today tab features developer stories; a Zerocap partnership story is compelling editorial content

### 10.4 App Store Search Optimization from Partnerships

Brand partnerships create new keyword opportunities:

- Partnership with Starbucks allows ASO targeting "Starbucks game," "Starbucks app," etc.
- Partnership with Nike opens "Nike AR," "Nike digital," "sneaker game," etc.
- Each partnership expands the keyword universe that drives organic discovery

## 12. Key Takeaways

### 1. Zerocap's scan mechanic is the most natural brand integration vehicle in consumer apps

Unlike ads that interrupt, or sponsored content that feels forced, scanning a real brand product to create a pet is *the core user experience*. The brand doesn't sponsor the fun -- the brand *is* the fun.

### 2. Start with organic brand content, not partnership pitches

Create content featuring branded products first. Build the proof-of-concept portfolio. The data from organic brand content is more convincing than any pitch deck.

### 3. Local micro-partnerships are the entry point

Walk into boba shops and bakeries this week. Zero cost, fast execution, and each one is a case study for the next partnership.

### 4. The UGC flywheel is the long-term moat

When users organically scan branded products, you accumulate partnership leverage automatically. Track this data religiously.

### 5. IP licensing is a Phase 3+ play

Licensing Sanrio or Disney characters requires scale, revenue, and legal infrastructure. Build toward it, but don't lead with it.

### 6. Partnerships accelerate App Store featuring

Brand partnerships generate press, download spikes, and innovation signals that Apple's editorial team notices. Time partnership launches with Apple's editorial calendar for maximum featuring impact.

### 7. Legal risk from scanning branded products is low, but manageable

Don't reproduce logos. Abstract designs into pet-inspired aesthetics. Pursue formal partnerships before any official brand integration.
