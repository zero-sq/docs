## 2. How to Find Creators Manually (Free Methods)

Manual discovery is the right approach at Zerocap's stage. Platforms like Modash, GRIN, or Aspire cost $500-$2,000/month and are overkill until you're spending $5K+/month on creators. Here's how to find 50-100 qualified creators without spending a dollar.

### 2.1 TikTok Search Strategies

TikTok is the primary discovery platform for Zerocap creators. The algorithm surfaces content by quality, not follower count -- meaning you can find hidden gems that platforms miss.

**Method A: Hashtag Search**

Go to TikTok Search, type a hashtag, browse the "Videos" tab. Look at the creators behind the best videos, not just the videos themselves.

**Primary hashtags (highest relevance to Zerocap):**
- `#aipet` / `#aipets` -- direct niche, small but perfectly targeted
- `#tamagotchi` -- 124.7K posts, 788.4K monthly searches
- `#virtualpet` / `#virtualpets` -- virtual pet community
- `#aifilter` / `#aiart` / `#aitrend` -- AI visual trends
- `#cutepet` / `#cutepets` -- 100M+ views, cute creature content
- `#petreveal` / `#petmakeover` -- before/after transformation format
- `#petsoftiktok` / `#dogsoftiktok` / `#catsoftiktok` -- massive pet communities
- `#kawaii` / `#sanrio` / `#cuteaesthetic` -- kawaii community
- `#aiapp` / `#appoftheday` / `#appsyouneed` -- app discovery
- `#newapp` -- people looking for apps to try
- `#tamagotchinostalgia` / `#y2k` / `#nostalgia` -- nostalgia hook
- `#momtok` / `#parentingtiktok` -- family niche
- `#satisfying` / `#oddlysatisfying` -- transformation content
- `#beforeandafter` -- the exact content format Zerocap produces
- `#techreview` / `#appreview` -- app review creators

**Method B: Keyword Search**

TikTok functions as a search engine. Type natural-language queries directly:
- "cute AI app"
- "new app you need"
- "tamagotchi 2026"
- "AI pet app"
- "turning objects into pets"
- "satisfying app transformation"
- "virtual pet collection"
- "apps that feel like magic"
- "AI camera app"

**Method C: The "Related Videos" Chain**

1. Find one video in your target niche
2. Watch it fully (tells the algorithm what you want)
3. Check "Related videos" and the creator's other content
4. The algorithm starts surfacing similar creators in your For You Page within hours
5. Repeat daily -- passive discovery that compounds

**Method D: Comment Section Mining**

This is one of the most underrated discovery techniques:

1. Find a viral video about a similar app (Lensa, Ghibli filter, any AI photo app)
2. Scroll through the comments section
3. Look for comments from accounts with creator-style profile pictures, bios, or verified badges
4. Smaller creators frequently comment on viral posts to get visibility -- their profiles often reveal they make similar content
5. Check their profiles: do they have 1K-100K followers? Do they make relevant content?

**Where to mine comments specifically for Zerocap:**
- Viral AI filter videos (Ghibli, Remini, Lensa)
- Tamagotchi nostalgia videos
- "Apps you need" roundup videos
- Pet transformation/makeover videos
- Any competitor app review (if competitors exist)
- Virtual pet game review videos

**Method E: Sound Search**

1. When you find a video with a trending sound that fits Zerocap's vibe (cute, transformation, reveal)
2. Tap the sound at the bottom of the video
3. Browse all videos using that sound
4. Identify creators who used the sound in a niche relevant to Zerocap

**Method F: TikTok Creator Marketplace (TTCM)**

Requires a TikTok Ads account (free to set up). Once inside:
- Filter creators by category, audience demographics, follower count
- See audience analytics (age, gender, location, device) before contacting
- Only lists creators with 10K+ who opted in -- not comprehensive but useful for mid-tier discovery

### 2.5 The "Comment Section Mining" Technique (Expanded)

This technique deserves its own section because it's the highest-signal free discovery method.

**The logic:** When a video goes viral about an AI app or pet-related content, every small creator in adjacent niches comments hoping to get visibility. These comments are pre-qualified leads -- the creator already self-selected into your niche.

**Step-by-step process:**

1. **Identify seed videos.** Find 5-10 viral videos (100K+ views) about:
   - AI photo/camera apps
   - Virtual pets / Tamagotchi
   - Pet transformation content
   - "Apps you need" compilations
   - Any competing or adjacent product

2. **Open the comments.** Sort by "Top" first, then switch to "Newest" for fresher leads.

3. **Scan for creator signals in comments:**
   - Profile picture is clearly "branded" (not a default avatar)
   - Username has "creates," "studio," "media," or niche keywords
   - Comment is substantive (not just "nice!") -- shows knowledge
   - They're asking questions about how to use the app (genuinely interested)
   - They tag other accounts ("@friend you need this")

4. **Click through to their profile.** Check:
   - Follower count (target 1K-100K)
   - Content niche (relevant to Zerocap?)
   - Recent posting activity (active in last 2 weeks?)
   - Engagement on their own posts (real comments?)

5. **Log qualified leads.** Add to your tracking spreadsheet with a note: "Found via comment on [video link]."

**Volume:** You can typically find 5-15 qualified creator leads per viral video's comment section. Mining 10 videos = 50-150 leads in an afternoon.

## 3. How to Vet Creators Before Paying

42% of Instagram influencers have at least one-third fake followers (Favikon 2025 report). Vetting is not optional -- it's the difference between $300 well spent and $300 burned.

### 3.1 Engagement Rate Calculations

**Formula:** (Likes + Comments) / Followers x 100 = Engagement Rate %

Modern formula (2026): (Likes + Comments + Saves + Shares) / Followers x 100

**What's healthy vs. fake by platform:**

**TikTok:**
| Tier | Healthy ER | Excellent ER | Red Flag (Fake) |
|------|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Nano (1K-10K) | 8-12% | 12%+ | Below 4% or above 25% |
| Micro (10K-50K) | 5-8% | 8%+ | Below 3% or above 15% |
| Mid (50K-200K) | 4-6% | 6%+ | Below 2% or above 12% |
| Macro (200K-1M) | 2-4% | 4%+ | Below 1.5% |

**Instagram:**
| Tier | Healthy ER | Excellent ER | Red Flag (Fake) |
|------|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Nano (1K-10K) | 4-6% | 6%+ | Below 2% or above 15% |
| Micro (10K-50K) | 2-4% | 4%+ | Below 1.5% or above 10% |
| Mid (50K-200K) | 1.5-3% | 3%+ | Below 1% or above 8% |
| Macro (200K-1M) | 0.8-1.5% | 1.5%+ | Below 0.5% |

Overall Instagram average as of 2026: ~0.5% (HypeAuditor). This is driven down by the massive number of large, low-engagement accounts. Nano accounts significantly outperform.

**YouTube:**
| Tier | Healthy ER (on views) | Red Flag |
|------|----------------------|----------|
| Nano (1K-10K subs) | 5-8% | Below 3% |
| Micro (10K-50K subs) | 3-5% | Below 2% |
| Mid (50K-200K subs) | 2-4% | Below 1.5% |

YouTube Shorts engagement: 3.4-5.9%, closer to TikTok numbers.

**Critical rule:** An abnormally HIGH engagement rate (10%+ for accounts over 50K) is just as suspicious as a low one -- it often indicates engagement pods or bought engagement.

### 3.2 Follower Growth Patterns (Organic vs. Bought)

Use Social Blade (free) to check any account's growth history.

**Organic growth looks like:**
- Gradual upward curve with occasional small spikes (viral video moments)
- Spikes correlate with content (a video went viral = spike makes sense)
- Steady daily follower gains (10-100/day for micro accounts)
- Small dips are normal (people unfollow, accounts get purged)

**Bought followers look like:**
- Sudden spike of 5K+ followers in a single day with no corresponding viral content
- Staircase pattern: flat, spike, flat, spike (buying followers in batches)
- Erratic growth: up 10K, down 5K, up 15K (buying + platform purges)
- Growth that doesn't correlate with content performance at all
- Massive follower count but views/engagement didn't increase proportionally

**How to check on Social Blade:**
1. Go to socialblade.com
2. Enter the creator's handle + platform
3. Look at the "Daily" and "Monthly" tabs
4. Check for suspicious patterns in the growth chart

### 3.3 Audience Demographics -- How to Check

Knowing a creator has 50K followers is useless if those followers are in a different country or age bracket than your target.

**Methods to check audience demographics:**

1. **Ask the creator directly.** Serious creators with 10K+ followers have access to platform analytics. Ask them to screenshot their audience demographics (age, gender, location). If they refuse or can't provide it, that's a yellow flag.

2. **HypeAuditor free audit (Instagram).** Run any public Instagram account through hypeauditor.com/free-tools/instagram-audit/ -- it shows estimated audience country distribution, age, and gender.

3. **TikTok Creator Marketplace.** If the creator opted in, TTCM shows audience age, gender, location, and device type for free (requires a TikTok Ads account).

4. **Comment language analysis.** Scroll through their comments. If the creator makes English content but comments are predominantly in Portuguese, Arabic, or Bahasa -- the audience doesn't match.

5. **Content clues.** Location tags, language used, cultural references, and time-zone-specific posting patterns all indicate where the audience is.

**What to look for (Zerocap target):**
- **Age:** 16-28 primary, 18-24 sweet spot (Gen Z)
- **Gender:** Mixed, slight female lean for pet/kawaii niches; slight male lean for tech/gaming
- **Geography:** English-speaking markets (US, UK, Canada, Australia) -- these deliver higher LTV
- **Device:** iOS users (Zerocap is iOS-only). If the creator's audience is 80% Android, skip them.

### 3.4 Content Quality and Consistency Assessment

**Scroll through their last 20 posts and evaluate:**

1. **Visual quality.** Is the lighting good? Is the framing intentional? Would you be proud to have Zerocap featured in this style?

2. **Audio quality.** Clear voice? Good music choices? No background noise? (Especially important for ASMR niche.)

3. **Posting consistency.** At least 2-3x per week is minimum. If they posted 3 months ago, then nothing, then a burst -- they're inconsistent and your sponsored post may underperform.

4. **Content niche coherence.** Are they consistently in one niche, or do they post random lifestyle mixed with gaming mixed with political commentary? Niche coherence means a more targeted, receptive audience.

5. **Hook quality.** Do their videos grab attention in the first 3 seconds? The creator's hook-writing ability directly determines whether your sponsored video performs.

6. **Storytelling ability.** Can they take a product and weave it naturally into content? Or does everything feel like a forced commercial?

---

### 3.5 Previous Sponsored Content Assessment

**Find their past brand deals and evaluate:**

1. **Does sponsored content feel natural?** Great creators integrate products seamlessly. Bad ones do obvious reads from scripts that kill engagement.

2. **How does sponsored content perform vs. organic?** If their organic posts get 100K views but sponsored ones get 5K, their audience rejects ads. Check the view counts.

3. **FTC compliance.** Do they disclose properly (#ad, #sponsored, platform tools)? If they don't disclose, they're either unprofessional or don't do many brand deals. Both are yellow flags.

4. **Brand quality.** What brands have they worked with? If it's all sketchy dropshipping products and crypto scams, their audience has been trained to ignore sponsored content.

5. **How many sponsored posts?** If every other post is sponsored, audience fatigue has set in. Look for creators where sponsored content is <25% of their feed.

### 3.6 Red Flags Checklist

**Engagement Pods:**
- Same 10-20 accounts comment on every single post
- Comments arrive within the first 10 minutes, then flatline
- Comments are generic ("amazing!", fire emojis, "love this!") with no reference to actual content
- High likes but zero meaningful comments or saves

**Follow/Unfollow:**
- Following count fluctuates wildly (follows 500, unfollows 400, follows 500 again)
- Following-to-follower ratio is suspicious (following 5,000 accounts with 10K followers = likely used follow/unfollow growth tactics)

**Comment Spam:**
- Their own comments on other creators' posts are clearly self-promotional
- They reply to their own posts with different accounts (sockpuppeting)

**Sudden Follower Spikes:**
- 5K+ followers gained in a single day with no viral content to explain it
- Growth rate suddenly 10x normal, then immediately returns to baseline

**Content Farms:**
- Reposted/stolen content (no original footage)
- Posting 5-10+ times per day with identical content across platforms
- No personal voice, no on-camera presence
- Bio lists 10+ "brand ambassador" titles

**Geography Mismatch:**
- US-focused creator but audience audit shows 60%+ from India/Brazil/Turkey
- Comments in languages that don't match the creator's content language

### 4.7 How to Negotiate Rates

**Step 1: Understand the market rate.**

Use Collabstr's free calculator (collabstr.com/influencer-price-calculator) to estimate fair pricing by niche, platform, and follower count before any negotiation.

**Step 2: Let them name a price first.**

When a creator responds with interest, ask: "What are your rates for a [TikTok/Reel/Short]?" This anchors the negotiation to their expectation, not yours.

**Step 3: Evaluate their rate against benchmarks.**

Calculate their effective CPM: rate / (average views / 1000). For Zerocap's niches:
- $10-$25 CPM = good deal
- $25-$40 CPM = fair market rate
- $40-$60 CPM = premium (justify with exceptional fit)
- $60+ CPM = overpriced unless they're a perfect-fit mid-tier creator

**Step 4: Negotiate with value, not just price.**

If their rate is above budget:
1. **Acknowledge their value.** "Your rates make total sense for the quality you deliver."
2. **Be honest about budget.** "We're early-stage, so our budget is limited right now."
3. **Offer alternatives:**
   - Reduce deliverables (1 video instead of 2)
   - Hybrid: lower flat fee + performance bonus ($0.50 per 1K views over threshold)
   - Long-term offer: "Lower rate now, first pick for paid campaigns at scale"
   - Bundle: "1 TikTok + 2 Stories" at a package rate
   - Content usage rights trade: they keep full rights = lower rate
4. **Create gentle FOMO.** "We're only partnering with 5-10 creators for the founding creator program."
5. **Walk away gracefully.** "Totally respect that. If our budget grows, I'd love to reconnect."

**What to never do:**
- Don't lowball insultingly ($50 to a 40K-follower creator)
- Don't promise future payment you can't guarantee
- Don't ask for too many deliverables -- one great piece > three forced ones
- Don't demand content approval rights at nano/micro level (kills authenticity)

### 4.10 Handling Creators Who Are Too Expensive

**The conversation framework:**

1. **Listen first.** Understand what they value most (money? access? recognition? creative freedom?)
2. **Validate.** "Your rates make total sense for the value you provide."
3. **Reframe.** "Here's what we CAN offer that most brands can't..."
   - Genuine early access before anyone else sees it
   - Complete creative freedom (no script, no mandatory talking points)
   - Long-term relationship (not a one-and-done)
   - "Founding creator" status with ongoing benefits
   - Revenue share / affiliate commission that could exceed flat fees
4. **Make the ask smaller.** Instead of a full video, can they do a Story or a brief integration?
5. **Leave the door open.** "If this isn't the right time, I'd love to stay connected for when we scale."

**The product seeding alternative:** For creators who are too expensive but a perfect fit, offer free early access with zero obligation. If they genuinely love Zerocap, they may post organically -- and "gifted" collaborations drive 12.9% more engagement than paid partnerships because they feel authentic.
