# Paid Influencer Marketing for Mobile Apps

Comprehensive guide for Zerocap — an iOS AI companion/tamagotchi app where you point your camera at real-world objects and AI transforms them into cute living pets with personality traits.

Focus: **paid campaigns** — buying videos from creators who post content on their own feeds as their own content (sponsored posts that feel organic/native). Not organic outreach, not running your own content.

---

## 1. How Paid Influencer Marketing Works for Mobile Apps

### The Full Workflow

**Step 1: Strategy & Targeting**
Define your campaign goals (installs, awareness, content library), target audience demographics, and budget. Identify which platforms your audience uses most (TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts).

**Step 2: Finding Creators**
Use marketplaces (Collabstr, Insense), platform tools (TikTok Creator Marketplace), direct outreach (DMs, email), or agencies. Filter by niche, engagement rate, audience demographics, and content style.

**Step 3: Negotiating**
Agree on deliverables, pricing, timeline, usage rights, revisions, and exclusivity. Always get it in writing — even a simple email confirmation counts.

**Step 4: Briefing**
Send a creative brief covering key messages, hooks, CTA, do's and don'ts, and reference content. The brief should guide, not script. (Detailed briefing section below.)

**Step 5: Content Review**
Creator submits draft for approval. Typically 1-2 revision rounds are included. Check for accuracy, disclosure compliance, and brand alignment. Don't over-edit — their voice is the point.

**Step 6: Posting**
Creator publishes on their feed at an agreed time. They tag the brand, include disclosure (#ad or paid partnership label), and add the CTA (link in bio, promo code, etc.).

**Step 7: Measurement**
Track performance within 48-72 hours for initial read, then 7-14 days for full picture. Monitor views, engagement, clicks, installs, and downstream retention.

### Types of Paid Content

| Type | What It Is | Typical Cost | Best For |
|------|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **Dedicated video** | Entire video about your app | Highest — full creative effort | Deep feature showcases, tutorials |
| **Integration** | Your app featured within a broader video | Mid-range — shared with other content | Natural discovery moments |
| **Mention** | Brief shoutout (15-30 sec) within their content | Lowest — minimal creative effort | Awareness at scale |
| **Sponsored post** | Feed post designed to look like their regular content | Varies by format | Blending into organic feed |

### Making Sponsored Posts Feel Native

The entire value of influencer marketing is that it *doesn't* look like an ad. The content should be indistinguishable from the creator's normal posts — except for the required disclosure.

**What makes content feel native:**
- Creator uses their own filming setup, editing style, and voice
- The app appears as part of a story, not as the story's only purpose
- Hook matches what the creator normally posts (reaction, POV, day-in-my-life)
- CTA is conversational, not salesy ("link in my bio if you wanna try it" vs "download now at...")
- Creator genuinely uses the app and reacts authentically

**What makes content feel like an ad:**
- Reading from a script or using brand-provided copy verbatim
- Unnatural praise ("this is the BEST app I've EVER used")
- Front-loading the brand mention before any entertainment value
- Using the brand's exact marketing language
- Abrupt shift in production quality (suddenly polished in a raw-content feed)

### FTC / Disclosure Requirements (2025-2026)

The FTC requires disclosure whenever there is a "material connection" between the creator and brand — this includes payment, free products, affiliate relationships, or any compensation.

**What's legally required:**
- Disclosure must be "clear and conspicuous" — a reasonable viewer must notice and understand it immediately
- Acceptable terms: "#ad", "#sponsored", "Paid partnership with [Brand]", "Sponsored by [Brand]"
- NOT acceptable: "#collab", "#ambassador" alone, "#spon", burying disclosure in hashtag soup
- For short-form video (TikTok, Reels): disclosure must appear **in the video itself** (text overlay or verbal), not just in the caption
- Platform-native tools (Instagram's "Paid Partnership" label, YouTube's "Includes paid promotion") are helpful but the FTC says they may not be sufficient alone — pair them with in-content disclosure

**Penalties:**
- Violating an FTC order: fines exceeding $53,000 per violation (each non-compliant post is a separate violation)
- Average influencer settlement in 2025: over $250,000
- Average brand penalty: $1.2 million in recent cases
- Brands are liable when they direct, finance, or benefit from non-compliant endorsements

**Best practice:** Include FTC compliance requirements in every creator brief. Specify exactly where and how disclosure should appear. The brand bears responsibility for ensuring compliance.

### Calculating ROI and Breakeven

**Basic formula:**
```
ROI = (Revenue from installs - Campaign cost) / Campaign cost x 100
```

**Breakeven calculation:**
```
Breakeven installs = Campaign cost / Revenue per user (LTV)

Example:
- Pay creator $500 for one TikTok video
- Average CPI from influencers: $5
- Expected installs: ~100
- If LTV per user = $8 → Revenue = $800 → ROI = 60%
- Breakeven = $500 / $8 = 63 installs needed
```

**Industry average ROI:** $5.20 earned per $1 spent on influencer marketing. Micro-influencer campaigns can reach $20 per $1.

## 3. Where to Find and Buy Creators

### Self-Serve Marketplaces (Best for Small Teams)

#### Collabstr
- **How it works:** Transactional marketplace. Creators list services and set their own prices. Browse, filter, purchase directly. Escrow-style payment — funds held until work is approved.
- **Network:** 200,000+ influencers
- **Pricing:** Free to browse. Paid plans for advanced features (filters, analytics, campaign management). Platform takes a percentage of each transaction.
- **Pros:** Transparent pricing (no negotiation needed), fast turnaround, low barrier to start, good for one-off purchases
- **Cons:** Less control over creator vetting, limited campaign management tools on free tier
- **Best for:** Quick content purchases, testing with nano/micro creators, small budgets
- **URL:** collabstr.com

#### Billo
- **How it works:** UGC-focused platform. Post a task brief, creators apply, you select and approve. Optimized for short-form video ads.
- **Network:** Smaller but curated for quality video content
- **Pricing:** Pay per video. Plans start around $59/video for basic content.
- **Pros:** Fast turnaround (often 5-7 days), quality-focused curation, built for ad content
- **Cons:** More UGC-focused than traditional influencer posts (content may not post to creator feeds), competitive for creators (25 applications per acceptance)
- **Best for:** Video ad content, performance creative, A/B testing hooks
- **URL:** billo.app

#### Insense
- **How it works:** Creator marketplace built for speed. Connect with vetted UGC creators and micro-influencers. Built-in workflows for Meta and TikTok ad amplification (Spark Ads, whitelisting).
- **Network:** 68,500+ influencers
- **Pricing:** Trial month at $450/month (1 brand, 1 campaign). Annual plans available at lower rates.
- **Pros:** Deep integration with Meta and TikTok ad platforms, Spark Ads workflow built in, fast matching, strong for performance marketing
- **Cons:** Monthly subscription required, smaller network than Collabstr
- **Best for:** DTC brands running paid amplification, Spark Ads campaigns, scalable UGC pipeline
- **URL:** insense.pro

## 4. Briefing Creators Effectively

### The Briefing Paradox

The best influencer content looks like the creator came up with it themselves. But without direction, creators may miss your key messages or create something off-brand. The brief must thread the needle: enough direction to hit your goals, enough freedom for authentic execution.

### What to Specify vs. What to Leave to the Creator

**You specify:**
- Key message (one core takeaway — not five)
- Required CTA (promo code, link in bio, specific phrase)
- FTC disclosure requirements (where and how)
- Product accuracy (correct app name, what it actually does)
- Do's and don'ts (competitor mentions, sensitive topics)
- Deadline and revision process

**You leave to the creator:**
- Hook/opening (they know what stops their audience's scroll)
- Filming style, editing, music choices
- Personal reactions and commentary
- Story structure and pacing
- Where in the video to feature the app
- Caption and hashtags (beyond required ones)

### Video Length and Structure Guidelines

- **TikTok/Reels:** 30-60 seconds is the sweet spot for app campaigns. Long enough to show the app, short enough to hold attention.
- **Hook:** First 1-3 seconds must stop the scroll. Let the creator choose their hook — they know their audience.
- **App demo:** 10-20 seconds showing the actual app experience (screen recording or reaction)
- **CTA:** Last 3-5 seconds, conversational tone ("link in my bio")

### Common Briefing Mistakes

1. **Writing a script.** Creators reading scripts sound like actors, not themselves. Give talking points, not lines.
2. **Too many key messages.** One message per video. If you have five features, that's five videos with five different creators.
3. **Over-specifying the hook.** "Start by saying 'OMG you guys have to see this app!'" — no creator naturally talks like your brand deck.
4. **Requiring specific superlatives.** "Say this is the best app you've ever used" — audiences smell inauthenticity instantly.
5. **Ignoring the creator's content style.** If they do comedy skits, don't ask for a straight product review. Work with their format.
6. **Burying the brief in a 10-page PDF.** If it takes more than 2 minutes to read on a phone, cut it down. Use bullets, bold, checklists.
7. **No visual references.** Always include 2-3 example videos that match the vibe you want. "Something like this, in your style."

## 5. Campaign Management and Measurement

### Attribution: Tracking Installs from Influencer Posts

Influencer attribution is harder than paid ads because there's no click-through pixel. Use multiple methods in combination.

**Method 1: Unique Promo Codes**
- Give each creator a unique code (e.g., ZEROCAP-SARAH)
- Users enter the code in-app for a reward (bonus pet, exclusive item)
- Simple, no tech required, tracks per-creator
- Limitation: only captures users who remember and enter the code (~10-30% of actual installs driven)

**Method 2: Custom UTM Links**
- Create unique links per creator with UTM parameters: `zerocap.app/get?utm_source=tiktok&utm_medium=influencer&utm_campaign=sarah_jones`
- Creator puts link in bio or uses link-in-bio tool
- Tracks clicks but not always installs (app store breaks the chain)
- Best paired with an MMP

**Method 3: Mobile Measurement Partners (MMPs)**
- **AppsFlyer** and **Adjust** are the industry standards
- SDK integrated into your app captures install attribution
- Creates trackable deep links that survive the app store redirect
- Can attribute installs to specific creators even without direct clicks (view-through attribution)
- AppsFlyer dynamically determines attribution windows (up to 24 hours for view-through)
- Cost: AppsFlyer starts free for small apps, paid tiers scale with installs

**Method 4: Custom Landing Pages**
- Create unique landing pages per creator (e.g., zerocap.app/sarah)
- Redirects to App Store with tracking parameters
- Clean attribution chain, easy to measure

**Method 5: Post-Install Survey**
- Simple in-app question: "How did you hear about Zerocap?"
- Options: TikTok, Instagram, Friend, App Store, Other
- Captures organic lift that other methods miss
- Won't attribute to specific creators but measures channel effectiveness

**Best practice:** Use promo codes (simple, immediate) + MMP deep links (accurate, scalable) + post-install survey (captures dark social/organic lift).

### KPIs to Track

| KPI | What It Measures | Target Range |
|-----|-----------------|--------------|
| **CPV** (cost per view) | Efficiency of reach | $0.01-$0.05 |
| **CPM** (cost per 1K views) | Cost efficiency at scale | $10-$30 (organic), $2-$8 (viral) |
| **CPI** (cost per install) | Direct acquisition cost | $3-$10 for influencer campaigns |
| **CPA** (cost per action) | Cost of meaningful in-app event | Varies by action definition |
| **Engagement rate** | Content resonance | 3-8% (platform dependent) |
| **View-through rate** | % of viewers who watch to completion | 15-30% for short-form |
| **Click-through rate** | % of viewers who click link | 1-3% for influencer content |
| **D1/D7/D30 retention** | Quality of acquired users | Compare to organic baseline |

### A/B Testing Creators and Content Angles

**Test variables:**
1. **Creator type:** Pet accounts vs. tech accounts vs. lifestyle accounts vs. comedy accounts
2. **Content angle:** "I scanned random objects" vs. "My pet did something unexpected" vs. "POV: you have an AI pet"
3. **Hook style:** Question hook ("What if you could turn anything into a pet?") vs. reaction hook (shocked face at pet) vs. result hook (showing the pet first)
4. **CTA type:** Promo code vs. "link in bio" vs. "search Zerocap on the App Store"
5. **Video length:** 15-second teaser vs. 30-second demo vs. 60-second story

**How to test:**
- Run 3-5 creators simultaneously with different angles
- Same budget per creator to normalize comparison
- Measure CPI and install quality (retention) per creator
- Double down on winning angles, drop losing ones
- Retest with new creators using winning formula

### Whitelisting / Spark Ads — Paid Amplification on Creator Content

This is the most powerful lever in modern influencer marketing. Instead of just relying on the creator's organic reach, you put paid media budget behind their content — running it as an ad from their handle.

**TikTok Spark Ads:**
- Creator posts the video organically, then shares an authorization code
- Brand uses the code in TikTok Ads Manager to run the video as a paid ad
- The ad appears to come from the creator's account (not the brand's)
- Combines organic authenticity with paid targeting precision
- Spark Ads outperform conventional ads by 20-50%

**Instagram Partnership Ads:**
- Creator tags brand as paid partner
- Brand can then boost/promote the post through Ads Manager
- Runs from the creator's handle with "Paid partnership" label
- Access to Instagram's full targeting capabilities

**When to boost vs. let it run organic:**
- **Let it run organic first (24-48 hours):** See if the content has legs on its own. If it's already gaining traction, organic reach is free.
- **Boost immediately if:** You're running a time-sensitive campaign, the content is proven (similar content from this creator performed well before), or you have excess budget to deploy.
- **Boost after 48 hours if:** The content is good but didn't get algorithmic pickup. Paid spend can give it a second life.
- **Don't boost if:** The content underperformed because it wasn't good (throwing money at bad content doesn't fix it) or engagement signals are negative.

**Cost for whitelisting rights:** Creators typically charge 20-25% of their base rate per 30-day flight. Negotiate this upfront — it's much harder to add after the fact.

## 6. Case Studies of Successful Mobile App Influencer Campaigns

### Case Study 1: MindMatch (Dating App for Introverts)
- **Strategy:** Partnered with mental health TikTokers and Discord book club communities
- **Scale:** 18 influencers total
- **Results:** 45,000 installs
- **CPI:** Depending on total spend, this implies highly efficient acquisition through niche-targeted micro-influencers
- **Takeaway:** Hyper-niche creators with small but dedicated audiences can drive massive install volume when the product-audience fit is tight

### Case Study 2: LingoBuddy (Language Learning App)
- **Strategy:** Collaborated with multilingual YouTubers and Reddit community members
- **Results:** +23% increase in 90-day retention (not just installs — quality users)
- **Takeaway:** Influencer-acquired users retain better when the creator's audience genuinely needs the product. The trust transfer from creator to app translates to long-term engagement.

### Case Study 3: Industry Benchmarks (Aggregated)
- Average influencer marketing ROI: $5.20 per $1 spent
- Micro-influencer campaigns: ~$20 per $1 spent (roughly 20:1 return)
- Macro-influencer campaigns: ~$6 per $1 spent (roughly 6:1 return)
- Influencer-driven installs have 50-70% lower churn than paid ad installs

### What Content Styles Drive the Most Installs

Based on aggregated campaign data across mobile app categories:

1. **"I tried [app] and this happened" — Reaction/discovery format**
   - Highest performing for new app launches
   - Works because it's pure entertainment first, promotion second
   - The creator's genuine surprise is the hook

2. **POV/Story format**
   - "POV: you download an app that turns your stuff into pets"
   - Narrative structure keeps viewers watching to the end
   - CTA feels natural at the resolution

3. **Before/After transformation**
   - Shows the real-world object → AI pet transformation
   - Extremely shareable — viewers want to see what their own objects would become
   - Perfect for Zerocap's core mechanic

4. **Challenge/Trend integration**
   - Creator applies the app to an existing TikTok trend
   - Rides algorithmic momentum from trending sounds/formats
   - Lowest production effort, highest viral potential

5. **Day-in-my-life integration**
   - App appears as a natural part of the creator's routine
   - Most authentic feeling, lowest conversion intent
   - Best for awareness, not direct response

### Content Hooks That Convert

Highest-performing hooks for app install campaigns:
- **Curiosity gap:** "I found an app that does something impossible" (don't reveal what until the demo)
- **Social proof:** "Why is everyone downloading this app?" or "This app has been blowing up"
- **Challenge:** "I bet you can't guess what pet this [object] turns into"
- **Shock/reaction:** Creator's face reacting to the AI pet (thumbnail/first frame)
- **Relatable problem:** "I've always wanted a pet but my apartment doesn't allow them"

## 7. Negotiation Tactics

### How to Negotiate Rates

**1. Know the market rate before you start.**
Use the pricing benchmarks in Section 2 as your anchor. If a 15K-follower TikToker quotes $2,000 for a video, you know the market range is $200-$1,000.

**2. Lead with value, not budget.**
Don't open with "what's your rate?" Instead: "We love your content style — especially [specific video]. We're looking for 1-2 TikTok videos showing our app in your style. What does a collaboration like that typically look like for you?"

**3. Bundle deliverables.**
Over 90% of experienced influencer marketers use bundling as their primary negotiation lever. Instead of negotiating down the per-video price, ask for more deliverables at the same total price.

Example:
- Creator quotes $500 for one TikTok
- Counter: "Could we do 2 TikToks + an Instagram Story mention for $700? That's $700 for you instead of $500, and we get more content to test."
- Creator gets more money total, you get a lower per-piece cost

**4. Offer long-term partnerships.**
"We'd love to start with one video, and if it performs well, we'd want to work together monthly." Creators prefer reliable income over one-off payments. This often gets you a lower per-video rate.

**5. Use performance bonuses instead of negotiating base rate down.**
Instead of trying to reduce $500 to $300: "How about $400 base + $2 per install tracked through your code? If the video does well, you could earn $800+."

**6. Negotiate usage rights separately.**
Don't let usage rights be an afterthought. Negotiate them explicitly:
- Non-exclusive, 30-day usage: usually included or +10%
- Non-exclusive, 90-day usage: +20-30%
- Exclusive (no competitor work): +20-30%
- Perpetual usage: 2-5x base rate

### Bundle Deal Structures

**Starter bundle (testing):**
- 2 TikTok videos with different angles
- 1 Instagram Story mention
- 30-day non-exclusive usage rights
- Typical discount: 15-25% vs. buying each separately

**Growth bundle (scaling what works):**
- 1 video per month for 3 months
- Usage rights included for all content
- Spark Ads authorization included
- Typical discount: 20-30% vs. one-off pricing

**Full partnership:**
- Monthly content (2-4 pieces)
- Cross-platform posting (TikTok + Instagram)
- Whitelisting rights included
- Exclusivity in category
- Typical discount: 25-40% vs. a la carte

### Performance-Based Deal Structures

**Base + bonus model (recommended for starting out):**
- Pay 60-80% of their normal rate as guaranteed base
- Add $1-$3 per tracked install as bonus
- Creator is incentivized to make content that actually converts
- You reduce risk on underperforming content

**Revenue share model (for established relationships):**
- Lower or no base fee
- Higher per-install or per-active-user payment
- Works best when creator trusts your tracking and the product is proven
- Rarely accepted by established creators without a base

**Affiliate/commission model:**
- Creator gets a percentage of in-app purchases from users they refer
- Long-term passive income for creator = ongoing promotion
- Requires robust attribution and transparent reporting

### Contract Essentials

Every paid collaboration should have written agreement covering:

1. **Deliverables:** Exact number and type of content pieces, platform(s), and format
2. **Timeline:** Draft submission date, feedback turnaround, revision deadline, posting date/window
3. **Revisions:** Number included (typically 1-2), cost of additional revisions
4. **Compensation:** Amount, payment method, payment timeline (net 15 or net 30 is standard)
5. **Usage rights:** Scope (platforms), duration, exclusivity, right to edit/remix
6. **Whitelisting/Spark Ads:** Whether creator grants authorization, duration, any additional fees
7. **Exclusivity:** Category exclusivity period (if any), definition of competing products
8. **FTC compliance:** Creator's obligation to disclose, specific disclosure requirements
9. **Content ownership:** Who owns the content (usually creator retains ownership, brand gets license)
10. **Cancellation/kill fee:** What happens if either party cancels (typically 25-50% of fee if cancelled after brief is sent)
11. **Performance data:** Creator's obligation to share analytics/insights from the post

### Phased Approach: Test, Scale, Optimize

**Phase 1: Test (Weeks 1-3) — Budget: 20-30% of total**
- Book 3-5 nano/micro creators across 3 different content angles
- Each creator gets a different angle from the brief
- Use unique promo codes per creator for attribution
- Let content run organically — no boosting yet
- **Decision point:** Which angle got the most views, engagement, and installs per dollar?

**Phase 2: Scale Winners (Weeks 4-6) — Budget: 40-50% of total**
- Take the winning angle(s) from Phase 1
- Book 8-12 new creators using the proven formula
- Start Spark Ads on top performers from Phase 1 (they may still be getting views)
- Test the winning angle with 1-2 mid-tier creators to see if it scales
- **Decision point:** Is CPI sustainable? Are mid-tier creators worth the premium?

**Phase 3: Optimize (Weeks 7+) — Budget: 20-30% of total**
- Negotiate ongoing partnerships with top 3-5 performers
- Bundle deals: monthly content + whitelisting rights
- Use top-performing content as templates for new creators
- A/B test variations on winning hooks
- Acquire usage rights for best content → run as your own paid ads
- **Decision point:** Can you maintain CPI under your LTV threshold at increasing volume?

### Key Success Metrics for Zerocap

- **Primary:** Cost per install (target: under $5 for micro, under $8 for mid-tier)
- **Secondary:** D1 retention of influencer-acquired users (compare to organic baseline)
- **Tertiary:** Content library value (number of reusable videos with usage rights)
- **Leading indicator:** Engagement rate on creator posts (comments asking "what app is this?" = high intent)

- [ ] Campaign goal defined (installs, awareness, content library)

- [ ] Deliverables match agreement (number of videos, platforms, format)

- [ ] No competitor mentions or brand-damaging content
- [ ] Usage rights terms are documented

- [ ] Track views, engagement, clicks within first 48 hours

- [ ] Check promo code redemptions and MMP attribution
- [ ] Decide on Spark Ad boost within 48-72 hours
- [ ] Send payment per agreed timeline
- [ ] Screenshot/save the post for records (in case of deletion)
- [ ] Debrief: what worked, what didn't, what to replicate
