# Competition & Contest Duration Research

Deep research on optimal competition/contest duration for UGC competitions, game jams, build competitions, and creative contests. Focused on hard data, case studies, and participation metrics.

---

## 1. Short Contests (1-2 Weeks): Highest Entry Rates

### Marketing Contest Data
- **Short contests (1 week or less) drive 29% more entries** than longer ones. The urgency motivates users to act immediately rather than procrastinate. ([Outgrow](https://outgrow.co/blog/stats-giveaways-contests), [Amra and Elma](https://www.amraandelma.com/contest-planning-marketing-statistics/))
- **Optimal giveaway length is 2 weeks.** The engagement curve is U-shaped: best quality entrants come in the **first 3 days and last 3 days**, with a lull in the middle. A 2-week window gives the optimal gap between these two peaks. ([RafflePress](https://rafflepress.com/how-long-should-a-giveaway-last/), [Gleam](https://gleam.io/faq/competitions/tips/best-duration))
- The "sweet spot" for social media contests specifically is **7-10 days** -- long enough to build buzz, short enough for urgency. ([ShortStack](https://www.shortstack.com/blog/how-long-should-a-social-media-contest-last))

### Game Jam Data
- **Global Game Jam (48 hours):** 47,000 participants, 9,010 games developed in the 2019 edition -- the record for largest in-person game jam. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ludum_Dare))
- **Ludum Dare (48h Compo / 72h Jam):** Typically ~3,000-5,000 game submissions per event. LD46 had 4,959 total submissions: 3,576 in the 72h Jam (72%) and 1,383 in the 48h Compo (28%). ([ldjam.com](https://ldjam.com/events/ludum-dare/46/stats))
- **Academic finding: Shorter competitive game jams are more popular.** A study of 1,290 game jams on itch.io found that shorter jams attracted more submissions. The researchers recommend "jam hosts should introduce a tighter time limit to increase their jam's popularity." ([Vu & Bezemer, FDG 2020](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3402942.3402981))
- **itch.io submission rates:** Median 0.286 submissions per registered participant. Unranked jams have higher submission ratios (median 0.333) than ranked jams (median 0.258). ([itch.io docs](https://itch.io/docs/creators/game-jams))

### Design Contest Data
- **99designs:** Standard contests run **7 days** (qualifying + final rounds). Fast-tracked contests run **up to 3 days**. ([99designs Help](https://support.99designs.com/hc/en-us/articles/204109459))
- **Threadless:** Design challenges have a **7-day voting period** once submitted. First 24 hours are critical for visibility/votes. ([Threadless](https://artistshopshelp.threadless.com/article/1159))

## 2. Medium Contests (1-3 Months): The Working Standard

### Platform-Specific Data

**Kaggle (Data Science)**
- Featured competitions typically run **2-3 months**. Playground competitions run ~1 month each. ([Kaggle docs](https://www.kaggle.com/docs/competitions), [DataCamp guide](https://www.datacamp.com/blog/kaggle-competitions-the-complete-guide))
- **Submission activity surges at the end.** Competitors release high-scoring notebooks in the final 2 weeks, and the last days see heavy ensemble blending activity. ([Kaggle discussions](https://www.kaggle.com/general/17582))
- Early forum participation and notebook contributions boost engagement, leading to **3x more active users** in competitions with early community signals. ([Medium analysis](https://medium.com/@sbhavana1997/cracking-kaggle-engagement-behind-the-data-079491632c10))
- Each team can submit **up to 5 times per day**, with a final submission deadline 1 week before competition end.

**InnoCentive / Wazoku (Open Innovation)**
- Average challenge duration: **45-90 days** (6-12 weeks). ([InnoCentive](https://www.innocentive.com/), [Wazoku](https://www.wazoku.com/innocentive/))
- Average prize: $20,000, with some over $100,000.
- 700,000+ registered solvers globally.

**TopCoder (Software/Data)**
- Marathon Matches: **1-3 weeks**, typically giving solvers "at least 2 weekends" to iterate. ([TopCoder](https://help.topcoder.com/hc/en-us/articles/24954728938011))
- Development challenges: **1-2 weeks**. ([TopCoder](https://www.topcoder.com/thrive/articles/all-about-topcoder-challenges-tasks-and-gig-work-opportunities))
- Design challenges (LUX/RUX): **24 hours to 3 days**.

**GrabCAD (Engineering Design)**
- GE Jet Engine Bracket Challenge: **Phase 1: June 12 - July 26, 2013** (6.5 weeks), **Phase 2: Aug 15 - Nov 15** (3 months). Nearly **700 entries from 56 countries**. 8 winners. ([GrabCAD](https://grabcad.com/challenges/ge-jet-engine-bracket-challenge), [GE Press](https://www.ge.com/news/press-releases/ge-unveils-ten-global-finalists-its-open-innovation-jet-engine-bracket-design-quest))

**Roblox**
- Accelerator Program: **12 weeks** (discontinued Fall 2022). ([Roblox Wiki](https://roblox.fandom.com/wiki/Accelerator_program))
- Developer Challenge (annual): **72-hour jam** format. 500+ submissions in 2026. ([Roblox DevForum](https://devforum.roblox.com/t/roblox-developer-challenge-2026/4274705))
- Creator Challenge (discontinued): One event lasted **3 months**, while others lasted 2 weeks. ([Roblox Wiki](https://roblox.fandom.com/wiki/Roblox_Creator_Challenge_(event_series)))

### Academic Research on Medium Duration
- The **best overall marketing campaign duration is 90 days**, according to aggregated giveaway/contest statistics. ([Outgrow](https://outgrow.co/blog/stats-giveaways-contests))
- Campaigns lasting **6-8 weeks** see better conversion rates than those under 4 weeks. ([Amra and Elma](https://www.amraandelma.com/contest-planning-marketing-statistics/))
- Longer contest duration **leads to higher contest performance** in one-stage contests. ([Korpeoglu et al., M&SOM 2021](https://pubsonline.informs.org/doi/10.1287/msom.2022.0375))

## 3. Long Contests (3-6+ Months): Case Studies

### Successful Long-Running Competitions

**Netflix Prize (3 years, 2006-2009)**
- Duration: Oct 2, 2006 - June 26, 2009.
- **51,051 contestants, 41,305 teams from 186 countries.** 44,014 valid submissions from 5,169 teams. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Netflix_Prize))
- By June 2007 (8 months in): 20,000+ teams registered, 2,000 teams had submitted 13,000+ prediction sets, 9,000+ forum posts from 10,000+ registered forum users. ([Harvard case study](https://d3.harvard.edu/platform-digit/submission/the-netflix-prize-crowdsourcing-to-improve-dvd-recommendations/))
- Key dynamic: **Team mergers over time.** BellKor realized they couldn't win alone 1.5 years in, merged with Big Chaos in 2008, then merged with another team for the final submission. Second-place team "The Ensemble" also formed from previously independent teams.
- $1M grand prize.

**XPRIZE Competitions (Multi-year)**
- Quantum Applications: **3-year**, $5M competition. 133 submissions from 31 countries. ([XPRIZE](https://www.xprize.org/competitions))
- Carbon Removal: **4-year** (2021-2025). $20M awarded: $5M to 23 student teams, $15M to 15 milestone winners. ([XPRIZE](https://www.xprize.org/prizes/carbonremoval))
- Water Scarcity: **5-year** competition.
- Healthspan: **7-year**, $101M competition.
- Global Learning XPRIZE: Engaged 700+ person ecosystem, 2,500 tablets across 141 villages. Winner (onebillion) scaled to 17 countries. ([XPRIZE](https://www.xprize.org/competitions/global-learning))

**DARPA Challenges (Multi-year, Staged)**
- Robotics Challenge: **33 months** (Oct 2012 - June 2015). Three staged competitions: Virtual (June 2013), Trials (Dec 2013), Finals (June 2015). 25 teams in finals, $3.5M in prizes. ([DARPA](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/darpa-robotics-challenge))
- Subterranean Challenge: **~4 years** (2017-2021). Four competitions across multiple locations testing walking and flying robots. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DARPA_Prize_Competitions))

**Architecture Competitions**
- Buildner competitions: Typically **3-5 months** from registration opening to final submission. Multiple registration phases (Early Bird, Advance, Last Minute). ([Buildner](https://architecturecompetitions.com/))
- eVolo Skyscraper Competition: **~9 months** from announcement to submission deadline (March to December). ([eVolo](https://www.evolo.us/registration-2025-skyscraper-competition/))
- Two-stage competitions follow UIA guidelines: Stage 1 is light submissions (conceptual plans + sketches), Stage 2 is detailed development with honoraria paid to selected teams. ([UIA Competition Guide](https://www.uia-architectes.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/02/2_UIA_competition_guide_2020.pdf))

## 4. Participation Dropoff and the Engagement Curve

### The U-Shaped Engagement Curve
- **Consistently documented across contest types:** Engagement peaks at the beginning (novelty) and end (urgency/deadline), with a valley in the middle. ([RafflePress](https://rafflepress.com/how-long-should-a-giveaway-last/), [Gleam](https://gleam.io/faq/competitions/tips/best-duration))
- Longer contests lose engagement after the first few days and **require mid-contest promotions or bonus entries** to sustain interest. ([ShortStack](https://www.shortstack.com/blog/how-long-should-a-social-media-contest-last))

### Hackathon Post-Event Dropoff (Alarming Data)
- **Only 7% of hackathon projects had any activity 6 months after the event.** Study of 11,889 U.S.-based hackathon projects (2018-2019). ([Nolte et al., CSCW 2020](https://dl.acm.org/doi/abs/10.1145/3415216))
- **Only 5% of projects continued for more than 5 months** (global study). ([hackathonresearch.com](https://www.hackathonresearch.com/research-1))
- **85% of commits were made within the first month; 77% within the first week.** ([Nolte et al.](https://hackathon-planning-kit.org/files/Nolte-CSCW-2020_2.pdf))
- 35% of projects show *some* continuation activity, but sustained work is extremely rare.

### Crowdsourcing Contest Dynamics
- **Inverted U-shaped relationship** between participation time and task duration -- engagement peaks at moderate durations, then declines. ([SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5145827))
- As competition intensity increases, solver submissions **decrease** (competition is negatively associated with submissions). ([ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0963868724000210))
- Low-skill participants who experience sabotage/failure are **less likely to participate in future contests**. ([Organization Science](https://pubsonline.informs.org/doi/10.1287/orsc.2021.15163))

## 5. Does Competition Fatigue Exist?

**Yes, and it's well-documented across multiple domains:**

### Academic Evidence
- **Fatigue accumulation in dynamic contests:** Efforts exerted in early periods accumulate as fatigue, making future effort more costly. Players face a strategic tradeoff: high early effort increases early-period success but decreases late-period chances. ([ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0167637722000402), [Journal of Economic Behavior & Organization](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0167268122001275))
- **Cognitive overload near deadlines:** As competition deadlines approach, participants increase effort and become more cautious, but struggle to simultaneously manage physical fatigue and the cognitive problem of allocating effort over time. ([ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0167268122001275))
- **Post-contest displacement effect:** Contests increase contest-related UGC but **decrease regular community submissions**. Introducing contests to existing communities may be detrimental to overall content quantity. ([Korpeoglu et al., M&SOM 2022](https://pubsonline.informs.org/doi/10.1287/msom.2022.0375))

### Practical Evidence
- The external motivation of winning makes tasks seem **less intrinsically enjoyable**. People "assume that what you're bribing them to sign up for must be less fun than the prize." ([Competition design research](https://www.thecgo.org/research/the-creativity-contest-an-experimental-investigation-of-eliciting-employee-creativity/))
- **Employees lose motivation when projects take too long.** Meetings become unproductive, discussions lose focus, plans change, and research needs updating. ([Design sprint vs marathon research](https://medium.com/make-innovation-work/innovation-sprint-vs-hurdles-vs-marathon-6d1cace64702))
- Loss aversion: Ancillary effects of participating in crowdsourced contests can be **negative among non-winners**, especially in the short term. ([Journal of Consumer Psychology](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/10949968231184417))

| Platform | Primary Format | Duration | Notes |
|----------|---------------|----------|-------|

| **Roblox** | Accelerator (discontinued) | 12 weeks | Professional development program |
| **Fortnite Creative** | The Block 2.0 | ~3-4 weeks submission window | Followed by 1-week community voting periods per category |
| **Fortnite / Epic** | MegaJam | 7 days | Annual, open to UE and UEFN |

| **Global Game Jam** | Annual jam | 48 hours | 47,000 participants (2019 peak) |
| **Ludum Dare** | Biannual jam | 48h (Compo) / 72h (Jam) | 3,000-5,000 submissions per event |
| **Kaggle** | Featured competitions | 2-3 months | Late surge pattern |

**Key observation:** All major gaming platforms default to **short-duration events** (48 hours to 1 week). Kaggle is the notable outlier at 2-3 months, but it serves a fundamentally different use case (iterative model improvement) and still sees heavy back-loading of activity.

## 7. Optimal Contest Length: Synthesis of Evidence

### The Korpeoglu Model (Academic Gold Standard)
From "Optimal Duration of Innovation Contests" (M&SOM, 2021) -- the most rigorous game-theoretic analysis of contest duration:

1. **Optimal duration increases with:** novelty/sophistication of desired solutions, higher agent uncertainty (less predictable output), lower organizer urgency.
2. **Optimal duration decreases with:** availability of support tools that boost productivity over time, higher agent productivity growth.
3. **Positive correlation** between optimal contest duration and optimal total award size.
4. **Multiple unequal awards** are optimal when organizer urgency is below a threshold.
([Korpeoglu et al., SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=3064763))

### Practical Consensus Across Domains

| Duration | Best For | Entry Rate | Completion | Engagement Pattern |
|----------|----------|------------|------------|-------------------|
| **48-72 hours** | Game jams, hackathons, sprints | Highest per capita | High (compressed) | Sustained intensity |
| **1-2 weeks** | Marketing contests, design challenges | 29% more entries than longer | High | U-shaped (start + end peaks) |
| **6-8 weeks** | Innovation challenges, engineering contests | Moderate | Moderate-high | Back-loaded activity |
| **2-3 months** | Complex technical competitions (Kaggle) | Lower per capita, higher absolute | Moderate | Heavy late surge |
| **3-6 months** | Architecture, multi-phase competitions | Lower but more committed | Moderate | Phase-gated milestones required |
| **1-3+ years** | Grand challenges (XPRIZE, DARPA, Netflix) | Low but elite | Low overall, high among finalists | Team consolidation over time |

### Critical Design Principles for Longer Competitions
1. **Phase-gating is essential.** Successful long competitions (DARPA, GE, architecture two-stage) break into distinct phases with interim milestones, evaluation, and prizes.
2. **Mid-competition milestones combat the engagement valley.** Without them, the U-shaped curve deepens fatally.
3. **Community features sustain engagement.** Kaggle's 2-3 month competitions survive because forums, notebooks, and leaderboards create continuous social engagement.
4. **Team dynamics shift over time.** Netflix Prize showed teams merging and reforming -- the social layer becomes a retention mechanism.
5. **The back-loading problem is real.** Even in well-designed competitions (Kaggle), most real work happens in the final 2 weeks. The early period is exploration, not production.

---

## 8. Implications for a 2-3 Month Build Competition

Based on all evidence, a 2-3 month creative building competition is in the **challenging middle zone** -- too long for urgency-driven participation spikes, too short for the deep investment patterns of grand challenges. Key risks and mitigations:

**Risks:**
- Engagement valley between weeks 2-8 (U-shaped curve)
- 29% fewer entries vs. a 1-week format
- Competition fatigue eroding intrinsic motivation
- Post-contest community displacement effect

**Mitigations (evidence-based):**
- **Phase-gate the competition** into 2-3 week sprints with intermediate judging/prizes (DARPA, GE model)
- **Weekly leaderboards or showcases** to maintain social engagement (Kaggle model)
- **Community features** (forums, sharing, voting) to create ongoing participation beyond building (Kaggle, Netflix Prize model)
- **Multiple award categories** to keep more participants feeling competitive (Korpeoglu finding: multiple unequal awards are optimal)
- **Clear mid-point milestone** with tangible reward to combat the engagement valley
- **Urgency mechanics** in final 2 weeks (countdown, bonus categories, community voting)
