## 2. Speedrun Interview Process — Detailed Flow

Source: [A16Z Speedrun Accelerator Interview: What to Expect](https://xraise.ai/blog/a16z-speedrun-accelerator-interview-what-to-expect-and-how-to-stand-out/)

### The Pipeline
1. **Application reviewed** — team reviews every 2 weeks
2. **Video request OR 15-minute interview** — if they're excited
3. **The 10-minute call** — this is the critical gate
   > "It's like a movie trailer — they have 10 minutes with you to decide if they want to spend half an hour or an hour to go deeper."
4. **Additional screening calls** — a few more conversations with the founders
5. **Final decision** — usually within a week of last call

### What the Written Application Must Do
- **Company description carries more weight than most founders expect**
- Partners make early pass decisions based on whether they understand:
  - What you're building
  - Why it matters
  - Who wants it
- "Effective applications describe the problem clearly, define the user, explain the solution, and show what has been built so far"
- **Specific examples beat general claims**
- **Clear sentences beat ambitious buzzwords**

## 5. Paul Graham's Growth Rate Benchmarks — Original Source

Source: [Paul Graham, "Startup = Growth" (September 2012)](https://www.paulgraham.com/growth.html)

### The Exact Benchmarks
- **Good growth rate during YC**: 5-7% per week
- **Exceptional**: 10% per week
- **Best metric**: Revenue; if not charging, active users as proxy

### Compounding Math
| Weekly Growth Rate | Annual Multiple |
|-------------------|----------------|
| 1% | 1.7x |
| 2% | 2.8x |
| 5% | 12.6x |
| 7% | 33.7x |
| 10% | 142x |

Graham's core argument: "The only essential thing is growth. Everything else we associate with startups follows from growth."

### Can You Show WoW Growth with 2 Weeks of Data?

**Yes, but it requires daily granularity.** With 14 days:
- You have 2 complete weeks to compare (Week 1 vs Week 2)
- Or better: show daily active users on a line chart with a clear upward trend
- **The narrative matters more than the math**: "We launched 10 days ago. Day 1 had 200 players. Yesterday had 1,400. That's 7x in 10 days."
- Daily cohort retention is even more powerful: "Of the 200 who played on Day 1, 47 came back on Day 7 — 23.5% D7 retention."

## 7. Rapid Traction Case Studies — Under 2 Weeks

### Chicken Road — Browser Game Viral Success

Source: [How a Simple Browser Game Reached 42 Million Sessions — Digital Edge](https://digitaledge.org/how-a-simple-browser-game-reached-42-million-sessions-in-q3-q4-2025-full-case-study/)

- **Developer**: InOut Games (small team)
- **Total sessions**: 42 million in Q3-Q4 2025
- **July-August 2025**: 12 million sessions, **300% month-over-month growth**
- **Growth driver**: TikTok virality + Poki platform integration
- **Marketing budget**: Zero. No Facebook Ads, no paid influencers
- **Cost per install**: $0.02 vs $0.45 industry average
- **Social shares drove 70% of acquisitions** — the game was designed with viral mechanics baked in
- **First-session completion rate**: 97% (vs 72% industry average for hyper-casual)
- **File size**: Under 5MB — zero friction to play
- **Geography**: India 22%, US 18%, Brazil 15%

**Takeaway**: Browser games CAN achieve massive viral traction with zero budget. The key is designing for shareability from day one.

### Day.ai — 4,000+ Waitlist Signups in 72 Hours

Source: [Our Playbook for Announcing Our Seed Funding — Day AI](https://www.day.ai/resources/our-playbook-for-announcing-our-seed-funding-at-day-ai)

- **Result**: 4,000+ waitlist signups in first 72 hours
- **Strategy**: Waited a full year after closing their round before announcing
- **All traffic directed to own site** — controlled the narrative
- **Each team member wrote their own social posts** — authentic, varied angles
- **CTA**: "Get Early Access" waitlist with kickback email
- **Long tail**: 8 months later, still getting consistent signups without intentional marketing
- **Hundreds of teams onboarded**, thousands more on waitlist

### Harry's Razors — 100,000 Signups in One Week

- **Tiered referral system**: 5 referrals = free shaving cream, 25 referrals = full shaving set
- **Result**: 100,000 email signups in one week
- **Budget**: Minimal (reward cost only)

### Linear — 10,000 Waitlist via Building in Public

- Grew waitlist to 10,000+ users through Twitter "building in public" strategy
- Later reached $400M valuation
- No paid marketing for initial traction

## 8. a16z Big Ideas 2026 — Speedrun-Specific Themes

Source: [14 Big Ideas for 2026 — a16z speedrun Substack](https://speedrun.substack.com/p/14-big-ideas-for-2026)

### Themes Directly Relevant to Browser Gaming / AI Gaming

1. **World models → generative world experiences**: "World models will likely give rise to not just a single game, but an entire new category of generative world experiences." This is directly aligned with AI-native voxel worlds.

2. **Multiplayer AI — "2026 unlocks multiplayer mode"**: Vertical work is inherently multi-party. Agents need to collaborate. The collaboration layer becomes the moat.

3. **Social connectivity over productivity**: "2026 marks the year major consumer AI products shift from productivity to connectivity. Instead of helping you do work, AI will allow you to see yourself more clearly and help you build stronger relationships."

4. **Complex infrastructure as moat**: Building the hard technical things (like a dual-contouring voxel engine) that AI wrappers can't replicate.

5. **Unstructured multimodal data**: AI-native platforms that handle text, images, voice, 3D simultaneously.

### Broader a16z AI/Gaming Thesis (2026)

Source: [a16z State of Consumer AI 2025](https://a16z.com/state-of-consumer-ai-2025-product-hits-misses-and-whats-next/)

- xAI and Meta pushing entertainment and social AI
- Startups have "historic opportunity to capture niche superpowers and user loyalty"
- AI-powered gameplay running in browser tabs is now reality
- Neural networks generating dynamic content and adaptive challenges

## 9. The Sizzle Reel Strategy — a16z's Own Advice

Source: [SR005 Apps Reviewed: The 3 Signals That Get Startups In](https://speedrun.substack.com/p/sr005-apps-reviewed-the-3-signals-that-get-startups-in)

### Direct Quote from a16z
> "Even if you don't yet have an MVP, posting a sizzle reel for your product or vision that goes viral and drives waitlist signups helps you check the box for traction."

### What Counts as Traction Pre-Product
- Pilot customers
- Letters of intent
- **Viral waitlists that convert into real users**
- "Traction is just whatever validation you can get from the outside world"
- Even the "thinnest forms of traction can matter" in deep tech

### Waitlist Strategy from a16z

Source: [The Growth Meta: How to Build a Waitlist — a16z speedrun Substack](https://speedrun.substack.com/p/the-growth-meta-how-to-build-a-waitlist)

- **Pick a "spike moment"**: launch, funding, feature reveal, customer story
- **Make everything point to a single conversion surface**
- **Don't launch your waitlist prematurely** — wait until you have a coherent story and something worth pointing attention at
- **Run press + owned content + social distribution** simultaneously

## 10. 14-Day Sprint Realism Check

### What's Achievable with $1-2K in 14 Days

Based on all research compiled:

| Action | Expected Result | Cost |
|--------|----------------|------|
| Show HN launch (browser demo) | 10K-30K visitors, 3,700-11,100 plays | $0 |
| Reddit organic (r/WebGames, r/IndieGaming) | 1K-5K plays if post takes off | $0 |
| TikTok/Shorts (3-5 videos) | 200-5K views (account warmup) | $0 |
| Creator outreach (300-600 emails) | 15 content pieces, 500-2K plays | $0 |
| Reddit paid ads (if needed) | 370-1,850 plays at $0.10-$0.50 CPC | $500 |

**Realistic best case in 14 days**: 5,000-15,000 unique players with D7 retention data and 2 weeks of daily active user charts.

### The Compelling 2-Week Narrative for Speedrun

The story that works:

> "We launched [product] 12 days ago. Here's what happened:
> - Day 1: Hit #1 on Show HN. 15,000 people played it in the browser.
> - Day 7: 2,100 of those came back without being asked. That's 14% D7 retention.
> - Week 2 vs Week 1: DAU grew 40% (from avg 800 to avg 1,120).
> - Discord: 350 members, 45 daily active chatters.
> - 8 creators made videos. Combined 50K views.
> - Zero dollars spent on marketing."

**Why this works for Speedrun specifically**: It's a spark. It shows organic demand, retention (the metric they care most about), growth trajectory, and distribution capability — all in the language Josh Lu uses.

### Can You Show WoW Growth with 2 Weeks?

**Yes.** Two approaches:

1. **Week-over-week comparison**: Week 1 DAU avg vs Week 2 DAU avg. Even one data point showing growth is meaningful if the absolute direction is clearly up.

2. **Daily chart with trendline**: 14 data points on a line chart. If the line goes up and to the right, that IS the growth story. Label the slope.

3. **Cohort retention table**: Even more powerful than DAU. Show Day 1, Day 3, Day 7 retention for your launch cohort. This is the metric gaming investors actually make decisions on.
