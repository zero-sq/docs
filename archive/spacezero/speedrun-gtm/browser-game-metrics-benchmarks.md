# Browser Game Metrics & Benchmarks

Research compiled 2026-03-12. Focus on .io games, browser sandbox games, and web-based creative tools. All numbers sourced from published data.

## 1. Browser Game Retention Benchmarks

### Mobile game baselines (for comparison)

The most widely-tracked benchmarks are mobile, not browser. Use these as a floor — browser games face different dynamics.

| Metric | Industry Average (2024) | Top 25% | "Good" Traditional Target |
|--------|------------------------|---------|--------------------------|
| D1 | 26-28% | 31-33% (iOS), 25-27% (Android) | 40% |
| D7 | ~8% | 7-8% | 20% |
| D30 | <3% | ~3% | 10% |

Source: [GameAnalytics 2025 Mobile Gaming Benchmarks](https://www.gameanalytics.com/reports/2025-mobile-gaming-benchmarks), [GameAnalytics Q1 2024](https://www.gameanalytics.com/reports/mobile-games-benchmarks-q1-2024)

**Year-over-year decline**: D1 dropped from 28-29% (2023) to 26-28% (2024). D7 dropped from 4-5% to 3.4-3.9%. The industry is getting worse, not better.

75% of projects have D28 retention below 3%.

### Browser/web game-specific retention data

**Facebook Instant Games**: D1 retention averages only ~5%. Discovery is terrible for games outside the top 100. ([Jon Lai / Tocelot](https://www.jonlai.co/instant-games))

**Poki benchmarks**: Poki tracks D1, D3, and D7 retention for all games on their platform. They score performance 0-5 against category averages. Average session duration on poki.com: **15 minutes 10 seconds** (Jan 2026, 150M monthly visits). ([SimilarWeb](https://www.similarweb.com/website/poki.com/), [Poki Developers](https://developers.poki.com/guide/player-device-report))

**Chicken Road case study (2025)**: A minimalist browser game that hit 42M sessions in Q3-Q4 2025. Weekly A/B testing lifted D7 retention by 18%. Social shares drove 70% of acquisition. ([DigitalEdge case study](https://digitaledge.org/how-a-simple-browser-game-reached-42-million-sessions-in-q3-q4-2025-full-case-study/))

### Browser vs mobile vs PC retention — key differences

No published apples-to-apples study exists comparing browser game D1/D7/D30 to mobile directly. The structural difference is:
- **Browser games have higher top-of-funnel conversion** (no download barrier) but potentially lower raw D1 because users try more casually
- **Mobile games have lower top-of-funnel** (download friction) but users who install are more committed
- Facebook Instant Games D1 at ~5% vs mobile average D1 at ~27% illustrates this — but Instant Games had terrible discovery, making this an unfair comparison

### Session length

| Context | Average Session | Source |
|---------|----------------|--------|
| Mobile games (all) | 5-7 min | [Udonis](https://www.blog.udonis.co/mobile-marketing/mobile-games/session-length) |
| Top 25% mobile games | 7 min | GameAnalytics |
| Poki.com (browser) | 15 min 10 sec | SimilarWeb Jan 2026 |
| Card/Casino games | up to 35 min | GameAnalytics |
| Casual gamers (self-reported) | ~60 min | Research surveys |
| Mobile session frequency | 4-6 times/day | Industry data |

**Key insight**: Poki's 15-minute average is 2-3x longer than mobile game averages. Browser gamers who show up tend to play longer sessions — they're at a desk, not on a bus.

## 2. First-Week and First-Month Numbers for Successful Browser Games

### Agar.io

- **Launch**: April 28, 2015 — posted on 4chan by 19-year-old Brazilian developer Matheus Valadares
- **First weeks**: 5 million DAU within weeks of launch
- **Mobile launch (July 8, 2015)**: 10 million downloads in the first week
- **Peak**: Google's most searched video game of 2015
- **By Dec 2016**: 30M monthly players, 113M mobile downloads
- **Growth driver**: Zero marketing. One 4chan post. YouTube and Twitch creators drove all growth organically.
- Sources: [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agar.io), [Gamehag](https://gamehag.com/news/the-story-of-agario), [PocketGamer](https://www.pocketgamer.biz/news/64583/agario-113-million-downloads/)

### Slither.io

- **Launch**: March 25, 2016 (browser + iOS, Android 2 days later)
- **First weeks**: Hit #1 on App Store free charts in US, UK, and multiple other countries
- **PewDiePie effect**: Started making Slither.io videos in April 2016, massive boost
- **Peak revenue**: $100,000/day from advertising alone
- **By end of 2016**: Google's most searched video game in the US. 68M mobile downloads + 67M browser users = 135M total players
- **Revenue model**: Ad-supported, with small fee to remove ads
- Sources: [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slither.io), [Digital Trends](https://www.digitaltrends.com/gaming/viral-app-slither-pulls-100k-per-day/), [PocketGamer](https://www.pocketgamer.biz/news/63211/slitherio-revenue/)

### Krunker.io

- **Launch**: Beta in 2018, official January 2019
- **Growth**: Named best .IO game of 2019 by ScienceFiction.com
- **Browser peak**: Exact DAU not published for browser-only era, but described as "millions" of players
- **Steam peak**: 2,265 concurrent (Oct 2021) — much smaller than browser audience
- **Current state**: Steam down to ~28 concurrent. Browser version still active at krunker.io
- Sources: [Steam Charts](https://steamcharts.com/app/1408720), [SimilarWeb](https://www.similarweb.com/website/krunker.io/)

### Shell Shockers

- **Lifetime**: 200M+ lifetime players, 25K DAU (as of 2025)
- **8 years old** and still acquiring new players at a steady rate
- **Seasonal pattern**: Spikes during back-to-school (Sept), drops during holidays/summer — driven by school computer access
- **Competitive position**: Among the most-played browser FPS titles. Krunker.io estimated at 50-100K DAU in comparison.
- Source: [Geometry Dash UK](https://geometrydash.co.uk/how-many-people-play-shell-shockers-2025-player-count-stats/)

## 3. Browser Conversion Advantage

### The download funnel is brutal

| Metric | Rate | Source |
|--------|------|--------|
| App Store page-to-install (all categories) | 25% (iOS), 27% (Android) | [Adapty 2026](https://adapty.io/blog/app-store-conversion-rate/) |
| App Store page-to-install (games specifically) | 3-5% realistic target | [Business of Apps](https://www.businessofapps.com/data/mobile-game-conversion-rates/) |
| Racing games conversion | 20.6% | AppTweak |
| Board games conversion | 1.2% | AppTweak |
| Ad impression to completed install (mobile games) | ~10% of ad-clickers complete install | [iLogos 2026](https://ilogos.biz/instant-games-for-mobile-publishers) |
| Single-session abandonment rate | 25% of all installs | Industry data |

**iLogos stat**: "90% of users who see an ad never complete the install flow." UA costs climbed 48% while game growth was only 1% YoY. ([iLogos](https://ilogos.biz/instant-games-for-mobile-publishers))

### Browser games skip the entire funnel

- **Click a link, you're playing.** Load time can be as low as 2 seconds.
- No store page. No download bar. No storage warning. No install.
- 50% of mobile ad clicks are accidental (Google data) — browser games don't need clicks to convert, just a URL.

### Where does the "37%" number come from?

The specific 37% figure appears to originate from **word game conversion rates** on app stores (word games convert at 37%, the highest category). It is NOT a browser-specific stat. There is no single published study establishing a clean "browser visitor-to-player conversion rate" benchmark — because browsers don't have the same funnel concept. If someone lands on your game URL, they ARE playing (or bouncing within seconds). The meaningful metric for browser games is bounce rate, not conversion rate.

### Viral loop advantage

- Social shares drove **70% of acquisition** for Chicken Road (browser game, 2025)
- Agar.io: Zero marketing spend. 4chan post to 5M DAU via YouTube/Twitch sharing
- Slither.io: Organic sharing + PewDiePie to $100K/day
- Browser games have structurally superior viral loops because sharing a URL is frictionless — the recipient clicks and plays immediately. No "download this app" barrier.
- **Playable ad data**: Click-to-play demos deliver 5x higher engagement than traditional ads. Deep-linked apps see 30%+ install rates vs 5% for generic links. ([Branch](https://www.branch.io/resources/blog/click-to-install-conversion-rates/))

## 5. Growth Rates in the First 2 Weeks

### Growth curve shape

Successful browser games do NOT grow linearly. The typical pattern is:

1. **Seeding phase** (days 1-3): Small community finds it (4chan, Reddit, Discord). Hundreds to low thousands.
2. **Creator discovery** (days 3-14): A YouTuber or streamer picks it up. This is the inflection point.
3. **Viral explosion** (days 7-30): If the game has intrinsic shareability, growth goes near-exponential (logistic curve). Agar.io: 0 to 5M DAU in ~3 weeks.
4. **Plateau and decay**: Growth hits the addressable market ceiling, follows logistic curve. ([20bits](http://20bits.com/article/three-myths-of-viral-growth))

### Real growth rates

- **Agar.io**: 0 to 5M DAU in ~3 weeks. That's roughly **doubling every 2-3 days** during the explosive phase.
- **Slither.io**: Hit #1 App Store within first week of mobile launch. $100K/day revenue within ~3 months.
- **Chicken Road (2025)**: **300% month-over-month growth** in Q3 2025, driven by TikTok virality + Poki integration. 12M sessions in Q3, then 9M sessions in November alone.

### Key insight on growth curves

"Viral growth isn't exponential — it follows a logistic curve." A web product has a maximum audience. Growth starts exponential, slows as it hits harder-to-reach users, and flattens at the addressable market ceiling.

For browser games specifically, the **step function from viral moments** is the most common pattern. A single TikTok video or YouTube let's-play can cause a 10x overnight spike. The game either retains those players (and the new baseline holds) or it doesn't (spike decays back).

## 7. Key Takeaways

1. **No clean "browser game D1/D7/D30" benchmark exists** — the industry tracks mobile, not browser. Facebook Instant Games (~5% D1) is the closest published data, but that platform had uniquely bad discovery.

2. **Browser games trade lower raw retention for massively higher top-of-funnel.** The math can still be better: if 10x more people try your game but D1 is 50% lower, you still have 5x more D1 users.

3. **Session length is surprisingly strong** — Poki averages 15 min, 2-3x mobile. Desktop/browser players engage more deeply per session.

4. **Growth is creator-driven, not UA-driven.** Every .io success story (Agar.io, Slither.io, Krunker.io) was ignited by YouTube/Twitch/TikTok creators, not paid ads.

5. **DAU/MAU of 20-30% is the target** for a strong browser game, benchmarked against Roblox (~21-29%).

6. **The viral loop is the product.** Browser games that succeed have intrinsic shareability — the URL IS the distribution. No app store, no download, no friction.
