# Platform Ranking for Spacezero & Zerocap

Date: 2026-03-09

Ranking TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube by importance to our games (Spacezero and Zerocap).

## Rankings

### 1. YouTube — The Gaming Platform

Every indie breakout (Among Us, Valheim, Fall Guys) ran through YouTube creators. Shorts are the discovery layer, but they feed into a channel that compounds over time.

**Why it's #1 for Spacezero:**
- Build timelapses at 50-58 seconds are the most compelling format
- Links in description actually work for "click to play" browser games
- Content has months of shelf life vs hours on TikTok
- Gaming is YouTube's dominant vertical — the creator ecosystem is massive and established
- Shorts → channel → community pipeline builds compound growth

### 2. Instagram — The Conversion Engine

Counterintuitive over TikTok, but our own research backs it: 85-90% of creators get better organic reach on Reels than TikTok right now.

**Why it's #2:**
- DM sharing is Instagram's #1 engagement signal
- For a browser game where every world is a shareable URL, "look at this" → DM link → friend clicks is the perfect funnel
- Eyeballs that actually convert, not just scroll
- Reels organic reach currently outperforms TikTok for most creators

### 3. TikTok — The Awareness Engine

Still matters for raw discovery volume and it's the native home for Zerocap's before/after transformation format (Lensa, Ghibli filter all blew up here).

**Why it's #3:**
- Declining organic reach — our research flagged a creator dropping 425K → 6K views in 18 months
- No clickable links in posts
- Worst conversion path of the three
- Good for awareness, weakest for driving actual engagement

## The Zerocap Exception

If you split by product, TikTok and Instagram swap for Zerocap. Photo-to-pet transformation content IS TikTok's language. The before/after format was literally born there.

**Per-product ranking:**

| Rank | Spacezero (Browser Game) | Zerocap (iOS App) |
|------|--------------------------|-------------------|
| 1    | YouTube                  | TikTok            |
| 2    | Instagram                | Instagram         |
| 3    | TikTok                   | YouTube           |

Instagram stays #2 for both — it's the bridge platform. DM sharing works for both app installs and browser game links.

## Key Insight

The platform matters less than the format-platform fit:
- YouTube → build timelapses, world tours, gaming content
- Instagram → shareable moments, DM-friendly links, cute/aesthetic
- TikTok → transformation reveals, before/after, trend-riding

Don't cross-post the same content. Shoot for the platform's native format.
