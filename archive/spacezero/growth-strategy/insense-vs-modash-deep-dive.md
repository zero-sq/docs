## TL;DR

**These are fundamentally different tools solving different problems.**

- **Insense** = UGC production + creator ads (whitelisting). You hire creators, they make content, you run it as paid ads through their accounts. Best for performance marketing teams who need content AND paid social distribution.
- **Modash** = Influencer discovery + relationship management. You find creators in a 350M+ database, reach out, manage partnerships. Best for brands building ongoing influencer programs at scale.

**For an early-stage startup:** Insense gives you content faster (creator marketplace + whitelisting in one place). Modash gives you more control and a bigger pool but requires more manual work. Neither is cheap enough for pre-revenue -- but if you have $300-500/month for marketing tools, Insense likely delivers more immediate GTM value for paid social.

### Creator Whitelisting: How It Actually Works

**What it is:** The brand runs paid ads that appear to come from the creator's account, not the brand's. This means the ad shows the creator's profile picture, handle, and existing social proof.

**On Meta (Partnership Ads):**
1. Creator connects their Facebook/Instagram to Insense
2. Brand requests whitelisting access through the Insense platform
3. Brand links the creator's Facebook profile
4. Brand creates ads in Meta Ads Manager using the creator's handle
5. Ads appear as if posted by the creator

**On TikTok (Spark Ads):**
1. Creator generates a Spark Ads authorization code from their TikTok account
2. Creator sends the code through Insense's direct chat
3. Brand copies the code into TikTok Ads Manager
4. Brand runs ads using the creator's existing content/handle

**Why it matters:** Creator whitelisted ads typically outperform brand-account ads because they carry social proof and can target the creator's audience lookalikes.

**Insense claims:** "Connect with peace of mind: Without any concerns of technical issues or ad accounts being blocked or restricted by Meta." No documented reports of integration bugs found in research, though absence of complaints is not confirmation of perfection.

**Sources:** [Insense Whitelisting Guide](https://insense.pro/blog/influencer-whitelisting), [TikTok Spark Ads Help](https://help.insense.pro/4312), [Meta Partnership Ads Help](https://help.insense.pro/4297), [Insense Partnership Ads](https://help.insense.pro/4299)

### 350M+ Creator Profiles: Is It Real?

**Yes, verified as the largest database in the category.**

- Modash indexes every public profile on Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube with 1K+ followers
- This is automated scraping of public data, not an opt-in marketplace
- Competitors for comparison: HypeAuditor ~207M, Heepsy ~11M, GRIN undisclosed
- Modash doesn't have creators "on" the platform -- it has data about public profiles
- When creators make their email public (in bio), Modash surfaces it
- They do NOT access emails behind logins, paywalls, or CAPTCHAs

**Key distinction from Insense:** Modash creators haven't opted into anything. They don't know you're looking at them. This is discovery, not a marketplace. You're cold-outreaching based on public data.

**Data freshness:** Search results update within 2 weeks. Unlocked full profiles refresh every 2-4 weeks.

**Sources:** [Modash Data Page](https://www.modash.io/data), [Modash Database](https://www.modash.io/influencer-database), [Influencer Marketing Hub](https://influencermarketinghub.com/modash/)

### Features Deep Dive

#### Discovery & Search
- **AI Search:** Natural language queries (e.g., "fitness creators in Austin with 10K-50K followers")
- **37 filters:** Location (down to city on Instagram), follower count, engagement rate, audience demographics, language, interests, content topics, brand collaborations
- **Audience-level filtering:** Filter by where the AUDIENCE is, not just the creator -- critical for targeting
- **Lookalike search:** Find creators similar to ones you already like
- **Fake follower detection:** Bulk-check accounts in seconds using behavioral analysis (missing profile images, follower ratios, account age, growth patterns)

#### Outreach
- **Built-in email:** Send up to 100 personalized emails per batch
- **Smart sequences:** Automated follow-ups
- **Auto-fill variables:** UTM links, affiliate links, payment links
- **Email unlocks:** 150/month (Essentials) or 400/month (Performance) -- these are emails scraped from creator bios
- **Limitation:** Email outreach is described as "basic" by some reviewers, may need external tools for complex sequences

#### Analytics
- **Engagement rates:** Per-creator and per-campaign
- **Audience demographics:** Age, gender, location (city-level on IG), language, interests
- **Fake follower scoring:** Bulk analysis
- **Content performance:** Views, engagements, clicks, promo code redemptions
- **Sponsored post performance:** Track how well brand collabs perform vs organic
- **Missing:** No earned media value (EMV) calculation, no true sales attribution

#### Campaign Tracking
- Track total content, views, engagements, link clicks, promo code redemptions per campaign and per influencer
- Content monitoring works with ALL creators without requiring signup
- Captures Instagram Stories (notable -- many platforms miss this)
- **Limitation:** Discount code tracking "often faces inaccuracies" per some reviews

#### Payment Management
- Single invoice to brand, Modash handles all creator payouts
- 180+ countries supported
- Custom commission tiers (percentage of sales or fixed amount per order)
- 3.9% fee per payout ($10K fee-free on annual Performance plan)
- Monthly payouts on the 1st, $80 minimum threshold
- Hold period configurable by brand (e.g., 30-day hold)

## Strategic Assessment for SpaceZero

### If the primary need is: "We need UGC content for paid Meta/TikTok ads"
**Insense wins.** It's the only platform here that produces content AND handles whitelisting. You get creators, content, and ad distribution in one tool. The 10-20% marketplace fees hurt, but you're paying for a managed workflow.

### If the primary need is: "We need to find and recruit influencers for partnerships"
**Modash wins.** 350M+ profiles, better search/filters, lower price, no commitment. But you'll need to manage the relationship yourself and can't run ads through creator accounts.

### If the primary need is: "We need both, but budget is tight"
**Neither is ideal.** Consider:
- **Phase 1:** Manual outreach + Billo ($59/video) for UGC
- **Phase 2:** Modash ($199/mo) for discovery + manual whitelisting
- **Phase 3:** Insense ($300/mo) when paid social budget justifies the platform cost

### True Cost Comparison (Realistic Monthly Spend)

**Insense -- 10 UGC videos/month scenario:**
- Subscription: $300-450/month
- Creator payments: 10 x $150 avg = $1,500
- Marketplace fee (10%): $150
- Production surcharge (15%): $225
- **Total: ~$2,175-$2,325/month**

**Modash -- 10 influencer partnerships/month scenario:**
- Subscription: $199-499/month
- Creator payments (handled externally or via Modash): variable
- Payout fee (3.9%): ~$59 on $1,500 in payouts
- **Total: ~$258-$558/month + creator costs**

**Key insight:** Insense's hidden fees (marketplace + production surcharge) roughly double the effective cost beyond the subscription. Modash is more transparent but doesn't produce content for you.
