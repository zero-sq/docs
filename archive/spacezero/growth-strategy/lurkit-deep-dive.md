# Lurkit (lurkit.gg) — Comprehensive Platform Analysis

**Date:** 2026-03-07

---

## 1. Founded When? How Old Is the Company?

- **Founded:** 2015 as a side project by brothers **Sam Niskanen** (CEO) and **Tom Niskanen** (CTO) in Sweden
- **Incorporated:** 2019 — transformed from project to company (legal entity: Lurkware AB)
- **Platform launched:** Early 2021
- **Age:** ~11 years as a project, ~7 years as a company
- **HQ:** Linkoping and Stockholm, Sweden
- Sam has a background in marketing technology and sales; Tom is an engineer

The company is young but not brand-new. The long gap between founding (2015) and company formation (2019) suggests it was a bootstrapped side project that gained traction.

## 2. Is It Trending?

**Mixed signals — momentum is real but it's not a breakout story.**

### Recent press / buzz:
- **Nov 2025:** Major partnership with **Streamlabs** — Lurkit's marketplace integrated into the Streamlabs App Store, reaching 3M+ creators. Covered by PocketGamer.biz, GamesPress, Outlook Respawn, Mobidictum, and WN Hub. This is their biggest press moment in a while.
- **Aug 2024:** Payment expansion via Tipalti integration — 200+ markets, 25 currencies. Covered by GamesPress.
- **2024:** Launched "Paid Missions" — performance-based influencer marketing (pay-per-view, pay-per-hours-watched). Covered by EIN Presswire, GamesPress.
- **Jun 2022:** $2.7M seed round — biggest press cycle. Covered by Tech.eu, PRNewswire, AnimationXpress, TalkCMO, FinSMEs, InvestingNews.

### X/Twitter presence:
- Active account @lurkit_gg (formerly @LURKITcom). Posts regularly about new game campaigns on the platform. Engagement appears modest — no viral moments visible.

### Reddit:
- Essentially **no Reddit footprint**. Search returns zero results on Reddit. This is a notable gap — gaming communities live on Reddit, and Lurkit has no organic discussion there.

### Industry coverage:
- Appears in Cloutboost's competitive comparison article (Keymailer vs Lurkit vs Terminals vs Woovit)
- Featured on AllCreatorTools, SoftwareWorld, Influencer Hero
- Not appearing in major gaming press (IGN, Kotaku, PC Gamer) — strictly B2B/trade coverage

**Verdict:** Trending within gaming B2B circles. The Streamlabs partnership is a real signal. But it's not a consumer-facing brand and has almost no grassroots buzz.

- Slow or non-existent customer support
- Payment delays for completed campaigns
- Platform UI described as "terrible" for creators
- No-commentary gameplay videos get flagged/suspended
- Smaller creators feel ignored in favor of larger streamers
- Being on the platform for 6 months with zero campaign access

### What studios/publishers like:
- Key distribution is well-structured
- Creator vetting based on engagement (not just follower count)
- Case study results are impressive (DayZ doubled viewership, SULFUR sold 80K+ copies)
- Indie discount program is generous (up to 70% off)
- All-in-one platform vs. piecing together tools

**Verdict:** The publisher side seems satisfied — the case studies speak for themselves. But the creator side has real problems: opaque moderation, weak support, and a perception of favoritism toward larger creators. The Trustpilot situation (3 reviews, mostly angry) is a red flag for creator satisfaction.

## 7. Pricing

Current pricing model (as of 2026):

| Tier | Price | Key Features |
|------|-------|-------------|
| **Indie** | Contact for pricing (up to 70% discount available) | Marketing insights, Creator CRM, Key campaigns, Paid campaigns/Quests (15% commission) |
| **Studio** | Contact for pricing | All Indie features + Creator Program, Dedicated success agent, Add-ons (Launch Pack, Take-over, Community push, Highlighted game) |
| **Publisher** | Contact for pricing | Same as Studio tier (presumably volume pricing) |

### Historical pricing context:
- The Indie plan was previously listed at **$89/month**
- They've since moved to "contact for pricing" across all tiers
- Quests/Paid Missions take a **15% commission** on transactions
- **No free tier** but they offer demos
- Indie discount: up to 70% in year 1, smaller discounts in subsequent years

### For comparison:
- Keymailer starts at $24.99/month
- Terminals.io starts at $2,000 per campaign
- Woovit has a free tier

**Verdict:** Mid-range pricing. More expensive than Keymailer, much cheaper than Terminals. The move to "contact for pricing" suggests they're doing sales-led growth now, which is typical for a B2B SaaS moving upmarket.

## 8. How Does It Actually Work?

### Core workflow for a game studio/publisher:

1. **Set up your game profile** — Connect your game to Lurkit's analytics engine (tracks Twitch/YouTube viewership, engagement data across 150K+ games)

2. **Create a campaign** — Options include:
   - **Key Campaigns:** Upload Steam/console keys, set eligibility criteria (platform, region, content type), create a landing page with media assets
   - **Paid Quests:** Set a budget, define deliverables (stream X hours, create Y video), creators bid for spots
   - **Paid Missions:** Performance-based — pay per view or per hours watched (new product)
   - **Creator Programs:** Long-term community hubs for ongoing creator relationships

3. **Creator discovery & vetting** — Lurkit algorithmically ranks creators by engagement quality (not just follower count). 90K+ verified creators in the network.

4. **Creator Management** — Review applications, accept/reject creators, track deliverables, manage communications from a centralized CRM

5. **Distribution** — Keys auto-distribute to approved creators. For paid campaigns, negotiations happen in-platform with bidding/counter-bidding.

6. **Analytics & ROI** — Track hours watched, views, wishlists, sales lift. Real-time dashboards.

7. **Payments** — Tipalti integration handles global payouts to creators in 200+ markets, 25 currencies

### For creators:
- Sign up free (minimum: 250 Twitch followers or 250 YouTube subscribers)
- Browse available campaigns, request keys, or bid on paid quests
- Earn "Lurkcoins" (200 per game covered) — redeemable in their store
- Streamlabs App Store integration (Nov 2025) — browse campaigns without leaving streaming setup

### Assisted service option:
- For studios that want hands-off management, Lurkit offers a managed service where their team handles campaign setup, creator outreach, negotiations, and reporting

## 9. Competitors in Gaming-Native Influencer Space

### Direct competitors:

| Platform | Founded | Creators | Starting Price | Best For |
|----------|---------|----------|---------------|----------|
| **Lurkit** | 2015 | 90K+ | ~$89/mo (contact) | Community-focused campaigns, engagement-first |
| **Keymailer / Partnier** | 2015 | 55K | $24.99/mo | Budget indie devs, broad reach |
| **Gamesight** | ~2018 | 10K+ | Custom | Performance marketing, AAA publishers ($1B+ client spend) |
| **Woovit** | 2017 | 40K | Free | Quality-first, minimal budget |
| **Terminals.io** | 2015 | 20K | $2,000/campaign | AAA launches with full PR support |

### How Lurkit compares:

**vs. Keymailer (now Partnier):**
- Keymailer rebranded to **Partnier** in 2025, expanding beyond influencers into press, playtesting, and events
- Keymailer is cheaper ($24.99 vs ~$89) but less selective on creator quality
- Lurkit's engagement-based vetting is a differentiator
- Keymailer has a risk of keys ending up on gray markets (less curation)
- Keymailer has better Reddit/creator community awareness

**vs. Gamesight:**
- Gamesight is the 800-pound gorilla — clients spend $1B+ on marketing through the platform
- Gamesight is more performance marketing / measurement focused
- Lurkit is more creator-relationship / community focused
- Gamesight has ~45 employees vs. Lurkit's ~22
- Different positioning: Gamesight = analytics-first, Lurkit = creator-first

**vs. Woovit:**
- Woovit has a free tier (Lurkit doesn't)
- Woovit is stricter on creator quality but has a smaller pool
- Woovit skews heavily Twitch / small streamers (1K–5K followers)
- Lurkit has broader coverage and more advanced campaign types

**vs. Terminals.io:**
- Terminals is premium/white-glove at $2K+ per campaign
- Includes full PR and marketing support beyond key distribution
- Way too expensive for indies — Lurkit's indie program fills this gap
- Terminals has fewer creators (20K) but higher-touch service

### Lurkit's competitive position:
Lurkit sits in the **middle of the market** — more sophisticated than Keymailer/Woovit, more accessible than Terminals, and more creator-relationship-focused than Gamesight. Their Paid Missions product (performance-based pricing) is a differentiator that bridges the gap between organic key campaigns and traditional paid sponsorships.

## Bottom Line Assessment

**Lurkit is a real company with real traction, not vaporware.** 850+ studio clients, impressive case studies (DayZ, Once Human, SULFUR), and the Streamlabs partnership signal genuine product-market fit on the publisher side.

**Concerns:**
- Creator-side experience is rough (suspensions, weak support, payment delays)
- Almost zero organic community buzz (no Reddit presence)
- No funding since 2022 — either sustainable or hitting a ceiling
- Review presence is thin and negative (Trustpilot: 3 reviews, mostly 1-star)
- Pricing is now fully opaque (moved to "contact sales")

**Strengths:**
- Capital-efficient (~$3–6M revenue with 20 people on $3.4M total funding)
- Founder-led, startup energy, modern product
- Strong gaming-industry network (Swedish Games Industry partnership, VOID Interactive as investor-customer)
- Paid Missions / performance-based model is forward-thinking
- Streamlabs integration is a massive distribution play

**Overall:** A solid niche player in gaming influencer marketing with momentum. Not a market leader (that's Gamesight), but carving out a credible position in the mid-market with creator-relationship tools.
