## 1. ZeroCap's viral mechanic is proven — it's not a bet

The "point camera at thing, get cute output, share it" loop has worked at massive scale multiple times:

- **Lensa** — one AI photo feature generated $50M+. Hit $8M/day at peak. But crashed hard because there was no reason to come back after the novelty wore off.
- **Prisma** — 7.5M downloads in a single week with $0 marketing. Same problem: one-trick, no retention.
- **Ghibli filter** — 130M people, 700M+ images in one week. Viral explosion, zero retention.
- **Remini** — 450M downloads. 1.4B TikTok views on the hashtag alone.

The pattern is clear: **photo in → dramatic AI output → share**. It works every time. The problem every single one of these had is that the output is disposable — you share it once and you're done.

ZeroCap's edge is that the output is a *living pet with personality*. It's not a filter you forget. You name it, it has traits, you collect more. That's the retention mechanic none of the others had. This is the single most important insight from the research — **the viral loop is proven, the retention problem is what killed everyone else, and ZeroCap has a structural answer to it.**

## 2. Pet/cute content is the highest-engagement category on social media

This came up in every research file and it's not close:

- Pet influencers get **5%+ engagement rate** on TikTok (the platform average is 2.5%)
- **64% rewatch rate** on pet content — people literally watch the same video twice
- Animals are the **#1 engagement category on Instagram** at 2.00%
- Pet content hashtags have billions of cumulative views

This means ZeroCap content doesn't need to be polished. The subject matter itself (cute AI pets) carries the engagement. A shaky phone video of someone turning their shoe into a pet will outperform a professionally edited tech demo, because the cute output is what people engage with.

## 4. ZeroCap's real competitor is Born/Pengu — and we have a differentiation

Born (rebranded as Pengu) is the closest comp: 15M users, $15M Series A from Accel and Tencent. But Born uses **fixed IP characters** — you co-parent a penguin that Pengu designed.

ZeroCap's pets come from **your real objects**. You point at your coffee mug, your cat, your backpack — the pet is personal. That's a fundamentally different emotional attachment. Born is "here's our character." ZeroCap is "here's YOUR character, from YOUR stuff."

The AI companion market overall is on track for **$120M+ revenue in 2025** (64% YoY growth). 72% of US teens have tried an AI companion. This is a real market, not speculative.

## 5. SpaceZero's URL advantage is genuinely unfair — but the viral coefficient is low

The browser game research showed that zero-friction access is a proven growth hack:

- **agar.io** — one 4chan post → 5M daily players. Because clicking a link = playing.
- **Wordle** — 90 players → 2M in 2 months. URL sharing was the entire growth engine.
- **slither.io** — 67M daily browser users, most-Googled game of 2016.

SpaceZero has this same advantage. Standard game funnel converts 1-4% of viewers into players. SpaceZero's funnel converts **5-10%** because there's no download step.

But here's the honest part: **SpaceZero's estimated viral coefficient is K=0.027** — very low. Meaning each user brings 0.027 new users. You need K > 1 for organic viral growth. The levers to fix this are increasing creation rate, making sharing automatic (OG previews on world URLs), and reducing time-to-wow.

ZeroCap's estimated K is **1.0** — each user brings one new user. That's why ZeroCap scales first and SpaceZero benefits second through the Trojan Horse.

## 6. Every gaming comparable that blew up did it with $0 and creators

- **Minecraft** — 1 person, $0 budget, grew through YouTube Let's Plays on TIGSource. 4M copies before official release.
- **Among Us** — 3 people, $0, died for 2 years, then one Twitch streamer played it and it did 100M downloads in 8 weeks.
- **Lethal Company** — 1 person, $0, TikTok clips + Twitch cascade. 10M copies, 2.4B TikTok views.
- **Valheim** — 5 people, ~$0, organic YouTube/Twitch. 5M copies in 30 days.

The pattern: indie games don't grow through paid ads. They grow when **one creator makes content about them** and it cascades. The research showed that for SpaceZero, the play is to DM world links (not pitches) to Minecraft/Roblox micro-creators and let the zero-friction URL do the conversion.

## 7. Every UGC/influencer platform we evaluated is wrong for us

This is where the bulk of the platform evaluation research went. We looked at 12+ platforms deeply.

**Skip all of them right now.** Posted, Collabstr, JoinBrands, Upfluence, minisocial, SARAL, Heepsy, Billo, Ainfluencer — they're all built for DTC ecommerce brands selling physical products. Their creator pools are people who do product unboxings and testimonials. That's not what ZeroCap or SpaceZero need.

Some specific red flags from the research:

- **Upfluence** — predatory contracts, enterprise pricing, multiple class-action-level complaints
- **Posted App** — documented billing issues, creators charged without consent
- **JoinBrands** — literally TikTok Shop for ecommerce, completely irrelevant
- **minisocial** — $2,500 minimum for batch campaigns targeting DTC brands

**What to actually use:**

- **Lurkit** (free) — gaming-specific creator discovery for SpaceZero
- **TikTok hashtag search** (free) — find pet/cute/AI creators for ZeroCap manually
- **Modash** ($199/mo) — worth it later when you have budget, 350M+ creator profiles, works for both tracks
