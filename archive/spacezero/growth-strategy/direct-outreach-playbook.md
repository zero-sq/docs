## 1. Ideal Creator Profile for SpaceZero

### Follower Range Targets

| Tier | Follower Count | Role in Strategy | Budget Implication |
|------|---------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Nano** | 1K-10K | Volume seeding (50-100 creators). Highest engagement, most affordable. Many will post for free with early access only. | $0-$100/post or game key only |
| **Micro** | 10K-50K | Core campaign tier. Best balance of reach + engagement + affordability. | $100-$500/post |
| **Mid-Micro** | 50K-100K | Selective partnerships. Higher reach, still accessible. | $500-$2,000/post |

**Primary target: 5K-50K followers.** This is the sweet spot for SpaceZero's budget and goals. Creators in this range manage their own accounts (no managers gatekeeping), have genuine community relationships, and are hungry for quality partnerships.

### Engagement Rate Benchmarks by Platform (2026)

What counts as "good" engagement varies significantly by platform and tier:

**TikTok (highest baseline engagement)**
| Tier | Average ER | "Good" for SpaceZero |
|------|-----------|---------------------|
| Nano (1K-10K) | 10-18% | 12%+ |
| Micro (10K-50K) | 5-10% | 7%+ |
| Mid-tier (50K-100K) | 4-7% | 5%+ |

**YouTube (longer content, deeper engagement)**
| Tier | Average ER | "Good" for SpaceZero |
|------|-----------|---------------------|
| Nano (1K-10K) | 5-8% | 6%+ |
| Micro (10K-50K) | 3-5% | 4%+ |
| Mid-tier (50K-100K) | 2-4% | 3%+ |

*Note: YouTube Shorts pulls 3.4-5.9% engagement, closer to TikTok numbers.*

**Instagram (Reels-focused)**
| Tier | Average ER | "Good" for SpaceZero |
|------|-----------|---------------------|
| Nano (1K-10K) | 8-15% | 10%+ |
| Micro (10K-50K) | 3-8% | 5%+ |
| Mid-tier (50K-100K) | 1.5-3% | 2%+ |

**Key insight:** 5%+ engagement on any platform justifies a 40-60% premium in pricing, because it signals real audience connection.

### Content Style Indicators: What Signals a Good Fit

**Strong fit signals (prioritize these creators):**
- Build timelapses (Minecraft, Roblox, Satisfactory, Cities: Skylines, Townscaper)
- ASMR gaming content (the satisfying sounds of building, placing, crafting)
- Creative/sandbox gameplay (not competitive PvP, not speedruns)
- "Oddly satisfying" content style (satisfying loops, before/after transformations)
- Cozy gaming or chill gaming aesthetic
- Mobile gaming content (especially if they showcase quality mobile titles positively)
- "Hidden gem" or "games you need to play" series
- Building challenges, creative showcases, architectural tours in games

**Adjacent niches worth exploring:**
- ASMR creators (non-gaming) who occasionally feature games
- Architecture/design content creators who play building games
- "Satisfying video" compilators who would feature game footage
- Toy/miniature building communities (LEGO, model-making) with crossover appeal

**Weak fit (lower priority):**
- FPS/shooter-focused creators
- Esports and competitive gaming channels
- Reaction/commentary channels with no gameplay focus
- Variety streamers with no sandbox/creative history

### Red Flags to Avoid

**Fake follower indicators:**
- Sudden follower spikes (thousands overnight) with no corresponding viral content
- High follower count but very low engagement (<1% on Instagram, <2% on TikTok)
- Follower-to-engagement ratio is wildly inconsistent across posts
- Audience geography mismatch (US-focused creator but top audience in Brazil/India/Turkey)
- More than 25% "suspicious" or "doubtful" followers on audit tools (HypeAuditor, Modash)

**Engagement pod signals:**
- Comments are generic ("amazing!", "fire!", emoji-only) with no reference to actual content
- Engagement spikes happen within the first 10 minutes of posting, then flatline
- Same accounts always comment on every post (mutual engagement groups)
- High likes but zero meaningful comments or saves

**Content farm signals:**
- Reposted/stolen content (no original gameplay footage)
- Posting 5-10+ times per day across platforms with identical content
- No personal voice or on-camera presence
- Bio lists 10+ "brand ambassador" titles

**Vetting process:** Always check the last 10-20 posts manually. Authentic creators have varied engagement, real conversations in comments, and content that feels personal. Use free tiers of HypeAuditor, Social Blade, or Modash for quick follower growth audits.

### Target Audience Demographics

**Ideal creator's audience should skew toward:**
- **Age:** 16-28 (core), with 18-24 being the primary sweet spot (Gen Z)
- **Gender:** Mixed, leaning slightly male (60/40) for sandbox gaming, but creative building content attracts a more balanced audience
- **Interests:** Gaming (obviously), creative tools, building/design, ASMR, satisfying content, mobile gaming
- **Platform behavior:** 61% of Gen Z gamers discover games via influencers; 52% trust creator recommendations on products
- **Geography:** English-speaking markets (US, UK, Canada, Australia) command 1.5-2x premium but deliver higher LTV players

## 2. What to Offer Pre-Launch Micro-Influencers

### What Indie Studios Typically Offer

| Offer Type | When to Use | Cost to You |
|-----------|------------|------------|
| **Early access / beta keys** | Always include. This is table stakes. | Free (your product) |
| **Flat fee payment** | For creators 10K+ where you want guaranteed content | $100-$800 per video |
| **Revenue share / affiliate** | Long-term partnerships, performance-minded creators | % of attributed installs/revenue |
| **In-game recognition** | Naming items, featuring builds, creator codes | Development time only |
| **Content repurposing rights** | When you want to use their content in your ads | 20-50% premium on base rate |
| **Exclusive content/features** | VIP creators who become genuine advocates | Coordination time |

### Compensation Benchmarks (2026): Gaming Content, 1K-50K Followers

**TikTok:**
- Nano (1K-10K): $0-$200/video (many accept keys only)
- Micro (10K-25K): $150-$400/video
- Micro (25K-50K): $300-$800/video

**YouTube:**
- Nano (1K-10K): $0-$300/video (YouTube creators charge more due to longer content)
- Micro (10K-25K): $200-$600/video
- Micro (25K-50K): $500-$1,500/video
- YouTube Shorts: 40-60% of full video rate

**Instagram:**
- Nano (1K-10K): $0-$150/post (Reel)
- Micro (10K-25K): $100-$400/post (Reel)
- Micro (25K-50K): $300-$700/post (Reel)
- Stories (add-on): $50-$200

**Twitch (sponsored stream segment, 30-60 min):**
- Nano (1K-10K): $75-$300
- Micro (10K-50K): $300-$1,000

**Key pricing factors that push rates up:**
- US/UK audience (+50-100% premium)
- Video content vs. static (+30-50%)
- Engagement rate 5%+ (+40-60% premium)
- Content usage rights for ads (+20-50% of base)
- Exclusivity clauses (+25-50%)

### Creative Compensation Structures for Limited Budget

**Tier 1: Pure Product Seeding (50-100 creators, $0 cost)**
- Send early access / TestFlight invites to 50-100 nano creators
- No payment, no contractual obligation
- Expect ~30% to post organically (15-30 creators posting 2-3 pieces each)
- "Gifted" collaborations drive 12.9% more engagement than paid partnerships
- This should be your highest-volume activity

**Tier 2: Hybrid Micro Deals (10-20 creators, $100-$300/each)**
- Small guaranteed payment + early access + performance bonus
- Example structure: $150 flat + $0.50 per 1K views over 10K threshold
- Or: $100 flat + 10% affiliate commission on attributed installs
- Total budget: $1,000-$6,000 for 10-20 meaningful partnerships

**Tier 3: Strategic Mid-Tier Partnerships (2-5 creators, $500-$2,000/each)**
- Creators who genuinely love the game and want to be long-term ambassadors
- Offer: Paid content + in-game creator code + "founding creator" status
- Revenue share on installs from their unique referral link
- Total budget: $1,000-$10,000 for 2-5 anchor creators

**Tier 4: In-Kind / Creative Compensation (unlimited)**
- Name an in-game item or feature after them
- Feature their builds in official SpaceZero showcases
- Give them a "Creator" badge or title in-game
- Early access to every update before public release
- Credit them in the game's official social media
- Invite them to a private "Founding Creators" Discord

### Case Studies: What Worked for Indie Games

**Valheim (2021) -- UGC Flywheel**
- Sent free keys to streamers; early videos mentioned "free key"
- Within days, streamers were genuinely hooked and playing organically
- Valheim then promoted user content (streams, YouTube, fan art) on their official channels
- This created a virtuous cycle: Valheim amplified creators, more creators made content
- **Takeaway:** Amplify your creators and they'll amplify you back

**Slay the Spire (2017-2018) -- The One Streamer Effect**
- Slow early access launch (2,000 copies in first weeks)
- Sent keys to influencers; a major Chinese streamer (1M+ viewers) picked it up on Twitch
- Single streamer caused massive sales surge; became second best-selling game on Steam
- **Takeaway:** Volume seeding works because you only need ONE to hit

**Lethal Company (2023) -- Zero Budget, Pure Virality**
- Solo developer, virtually no marketing budget
- Game designed with inherently shareable moments (proximity chat, horror comedy)
- Streamers and TikTok creators picked it up organically
- Viral clips spread because the gameplay was genuinely entertaining to watch
- **Takeaway:** Make your game visually compelling to watch, not just play

**The Ouroboros King -- Wishlist Surge**
- Wishlists went from 500 to 20,000 in two weeks pre-launch
- Targeted influencer outreach + Steam Next Fest combined
- Developer pivoted strategies until finding what worked
- **Takeaway:** Combine influencer outreach with platform events (for mobile: pre-registration campaigns, App Store featuring)

**Among Us (2018-2020) -- Slow Burn to Explosion**
- Released 2018, didn't go viral until 2020
- Streamers (PewDiePie, xQc) showcased the game; millions followed
- TikTok user-created content with memorable sound effects/characters amplified reach
- **Takeaway:** Keep seeding even when results are slow; virality can come months later

### Deal Structures: Flat Fee vs. Performance vs. Hybrid

| Structure | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|-----------|------|------|----------|
| **Flat fee** | Predictable cost; creator likes guaranteed pay | No performance incentive; higher upfront cost | One-off content, established creators |
| **Performance-only** (CPI/rev share) | Zero upfront cost; aligned incentives | Harder to recruit creators; they bear the risk | Creators who already love your game |
| **Hybrid** (small flat + performance) | Best of both; creator gets security + upside | Slightly more complex to track | Core campaign tier (10K-50K creators) |
| **Product seeding** (keys only) | Zero cost; authentic content | No guaranteed output; low control | Volume approach (nano creators) |
| **Affiliate/commission** | Ongoing passive income for creator; long-term | Need tracking infrastructure | Long-term ambassador relationships |

**Recommended for SpaceZero:** Lead with product seeding at volume, then upgrade your best performers to hybrid deals. Reserve flat fees for the 2-5 anchor creators who can drive real awareness.

## 3. Outreach Templates

### General Principles

- **Keep it short.** Gaming creators get dozens of pitches daily. Respect their time.
- **Lead with them, not you.** First sentence should reference their content specifically.
- **Be transparent about compensation.** Upfront disclosure of what's on offer shows you're serious.
- **Include visuals.** Attach or link to a 15-second gameplay clip or screenshot. Let the game speak.
- **No corporate jargon.** Sound like a person, not a marketing department.
- **Platform character limits:** Instagram DMs max out at 1,100 characters. TikTok DMs are even shorter. Email can be longer but should still stay concise.

### TikTok DM Template

> Hey [Name]! Your [specific video topic, e.g., "Minecraft castle timelapse"] was incredible -- the way you captured the build process is exactly what I love about your content.
>
> I'm [Your Name] from SpaceZero, a new 3D sandbox building game for mobile. Think satisfying build timelapses meets creative construction. We're about to launch and I think your audience would genuinely enjoy it.
>
> Would love to get you early access -- no strings attached. If you're interested, I can share more details. What's the best way to chat?

*~450 characters. Fits TikTok DM limits. First line is the hook (their "subject line" preview).*

### Instagram DM Template

> Hey [Name]! Just saw your [specific Reel/post] and had to reach out -- [specific detail about what you liked, e.g., "the ASMR building sounds in your Satisfactory video were so good"].
>
> I'm [Your Name], co-founder of SpaceZero -- we're building a 3D sandbox construction game for mobile. Think beautiful build timelapses, creative construction, and satisfying gameplay. We're in pre-launch right now.
>
> I'd love to get you exclusive early access before anyone else sees it. If you're open to it, I can send over a TestFlight link and some gameplay footage. No obligations -- just want to get it in front of creators who'd genuinely appreciate it.
>
> Happy to chat more here or via email if easier!

*~650 characters. Well within Instagram's 1,100 character limit.*

### YouTube Comment + Email Combo

**Step 1: Leave a genuine comment on a recent video first.** Not "great video!" but something specific like "The way you organized the factory layout at 3:42 was genius -- I kept rewatching that section."

**Step 2: Email (if listed in bio) 1-3 days after the comment.**

**Subject line options (pick one):**
- `SpaceZero x [Creator Name] -- early access?`
- `Loved your [specific video title] -- quick question`
- `[Creator Name], want to try something before anyone else?`
- `Building game + your creative eye = perfect match`

*Keep subject lines under 50 characters. Personalized subjects increase open rates by 50%. Including their name adds another 9.1% lift.*

**Email body:**

> Subject: SpaceZero x [Creator Name] -- early access?
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> I'm [Your Name], co-founder of SpaceZero. I've been following your channel for a while -- your [specific video/series] stood out to me because [genuine reason, e.g., "you have this talent for making building games look cinematic that most creators don't have"].
>
> We're building a mobile 3D sandbox construction game, and we're about to launch. The game focuses on creative building with high production quality -- think satisfying build timelapses, beautiful environments, and a creative sandbox that rewards experimentation.
>
> I'd love to offer you exclusive early access. Here's what that looks like:
>
> - TestFlight / beta access before public launch
> - A press kit with high-quality assets for thumbnails and content
> - Complete creative freedom -- we trust your judgment on what your audience likes
> - [If applicable: A small collaboration fee to value your time]
>
> No pressure at all. I genuinely think your audience would love this, and I'd rather have 5 creators who are excited about the game than 50 who aren't.
>
> Here's a 30-second gameplay preview: [link]
>
> Would you be open to trying it out?
>
> Best,
> [Your Name]
> Co-founder, SpaceZero
> [website] | [social handle]

### Follow-Up Templates

**Follow-up #1 (3-5 days after initial outreach, same channel):**

> Hey [Name] -- just bumping this in case it got buried. Totally understand if you're busy! Wanted to make sure you saw the early access offer for SpaceZero. Happy to answer any questions if you're curious.

**Follow-up #2 (7-10 days after follow-up #1, different channel if possible):**

> Hi [Name] -- last note from me, I promise! We're opening up early access to a small group of creators this week. If the timing isn't right, no worries at all. But if you'd like to check it out, the offer stands. Here's a quick gameplay clip: [link]

**Follow-up #3: There is no follow-up #3.** If they haven't responded after two follow-ups, move on. You can re-engage months later with a different angle (launch announcement, major update, etc.) but do not keep pushing.

### What Makes Outreach Feel Premium vs. Spammy

**Premium signals:**
- Personalized first line referencing their specific content (not "I love your videos")
- Transparency about who you are and what you're offering
- Attached or linked gameplay footage (show, don't just tell)
- Mention of creative freedom ("we trust your judgment")
- Clean formatting, no walls of text
- Professional email signature with real name and title

**Spam signals:**
- Generic copy-paste with [INSERT NAME] still visible
- Leading with your product instead of their content
- Vague about compensation ("exciting opportunity!")
- Overly long messages (>300 words in a DM)
- Aggressive urgency ("reply within 24 hours!")
- No gameplay footage or proof the game exists
- Asking for too much upfront ("3 TikToks, 2 Reels, 1 YouTube video")

### Common Outreach Mistakes

1. **Not researching the creator.** If they only play horror games, don't pitch a sandbox builder.
2. **Leading with your ask.** Lead with what you admire about them, then what you can offer, then (gently) what you'd love.
3. **Being vague about money.** Creators appreciate knowing upfront if there's payment involved. "We'd love to discuss compensation" is better than silence on the topic.
4. **Mass DMs with identical copy.** Creators compare notes. If two creators in the same niche get the same message, you're toast.
5. **Ignoring platform norms.** TikTok DMs are casual and short. Emails can be slightly longer. YouTube comments are public warmup, not pitch delivery.
6. **Not including gameplay.** Words are cheap. A 15-second gameplay clip is worth 1,000 words of description.
7. **Pitching before warming up.** Follow them, like their posts, leave genuine comments for a few days before the outreach DM. This dramatically increases response rates.

## 4. Outreach Process & Workflow

### Step-by-Step: From Discovery to Deal

**Phase 1: Discovery & List Building (Ongoing)**

1. Search TikTok/YouTube/Instagram for relevant hashtags: #buildinggame, #sandboxgame, #buildtimelapse, #satisfyinggameplay, #cozygaming, #mobilegaming, #indiegame, #gamingASMR
2. Look at who's creating content about similar games: Minecraft builds, Satisfactory, Cities: Skylines, Townscaper, Roblox building
3. Check "suggested creators" on platforms -- once you find one good fit, the algorithm shows you more
4. Use free tiers of Modash, HypeAuditor, or Social Blade to pull basic stats
5. Add to your tracking spreadsheet (see Section 5)

**Phase 2: Vetting (Before Any Outreach)**

For each creator, check:
- [ ] Last 10-20 posts: Is engagement consistent? Are comments genuine?
- [ ] Follower growth: Steady growth or suspicious spikes? (Social Blade graph)
- [ ] Audience demographics: Do they mention their audience? Is it English-speaking?
- [ ] Content quality: Would you be proud to have SpaceZero featured in their style?
- [ ] Brand safety: Any controversial content, hate speech, or problematic associations?
- [ ] Engagement rate calculation: (likes + comments) / followers x 100 for last 10 posts
- [ ] Response to other brands: Have they done sponsored content before? How did it look?

**Phase 3: Warm-Up (3-7 Days Before Outreach)**

1. Follow them on their primary platform
2. Like 3-5 of their recent posts (space it out over days)
3. Leave 1-2 genuine, specific comments on their content
4. Share one of their posts to your story (with attribution) if appropriate
5. This makes your name familiar before the pitch arrives

**Phase 4: Initial Outreach**

1. Send outreach via the platform where they're most active
2. If they have email in bio, use email as primary (more professional, less cluttered)
3. If DM-only, use the DM templates above
4. Always include a gameplay clip or link
5. Log the outreach date and channel in your spreadsheet

**Phase 5: Follow-Up**

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Day 0 | Initial outreach sent |
| Day 3-5 | Follow-up #1 (same channel) |
| Day 10-14 | Follow-up #2 (different channel if possible) |
| Day 14+ | No response = move to "revisit later" list |

**Phase 6: Negotiation & Agreement**

1. Creator responds with interest -- move to direct conversation
2. Share more details about the game (TestFlight link, press kit, gameplay footage)
3. Discuss deliverables and compensation openly
4. Agree on terms and document in writing (email confirmation at minimum)
5. Send game access and press kit assets

**Phase 7: Content & Relationship**

1. Let the creator play the game and form genuine opinions
2. Provide support if they have questions (responsive = professional)
3. Review content only if agreed upfront -- avoid micromanaging
4. Amplify their content on SpaceZero's channels (reshare, comment, credit)
5. Follow up with gratitude after content goes live
6. Keep them in the loop for future updates

### Negotiation Tips for Gaming Content

**When a creator's rate is above your budget:**

1. **Acknowledge their value.** "I completely understand your rates -- your content quality justifies it."
2. **Be honest about budget.** "We're a pre-launch indie studio, so our budget is limited right now."
3. **Offer alternatives:**
   - Reduce deliverables (1 video instead of 3)
   - Offer hybrid: lower flat fee + performance bonus
   - Offer content usage rights as trade (they keep full rights = lower rate)
   - Propose a long-term deal: "Lower rate now, but first pick for paid campaigns at launch"
   - Bundle: "1 TikTok + 2 Stories" feels better than "1 TikTok" at the same price
4. **Create FOMO (gently).** "We're only partnering with 5 creators for the founding creator program."
5. **Walk away gracefully.** "Totally respect that. If our budget grows post-launch, I'd love to reconnect."

**Data-driven counteroffers:**
- If their engagement rate is average (not exceptional), you have leverage
- Calculate their CPM: rate / (average views / 1000). If it's over $40-60 for gaming content, push back
- Compare to industry benchmarks: gaming YouTube CPM is $15-$40, Twitch is $20-$60

**What to never do:**
- Don't lowball insultingly. Offering $50 to a 40K-follower creator is disrespectful.
- Don't promise future payment you can't guarantee ("we'll pay more after launch if it works")
- Don't ask for too many deliverables. One great piece of content > three forced ones.
- Don't ask for content approval rights at nano/micro level -- it kills authenticity

### Legal Considerations

**FTC Disclosure (Non-Negotiable)**
- ALL sponsored content must be disclosed -- #ad, #sponsored, or platform tools
- Disclosure must be "clear, conspicuous, and unavoidable" -- not buried in hashtag soup
- Applies even for "free product" / gifted keys -- if you gave it to them, they must disclose
- Livestreams: disclosure must be repeated verbally AND on-screen
- Stories/Reels: disclosure must be overlaid on the video itself
- Platform tools (Instagram "Paid Partnership," TikTok "Sponsored") supplement but DO NOT replace in-content disclosure
- **Your responsibility:** The FTC holds brands equally responsible as creators. Fines up to $51,744 per violation.
- **Action:** Include disclosure requirements in every creator brief. Make it easy for them.

**Content Rights & Usage**
- Creators own their content by default. You need explicit written permission to repurpose.
- Organic usage rights (creator posts on their channel) -- typically included in base rate
- Paid ad usage rights (you run their content as your ad) -- additional 20-50% of base rate
- Duration matters: 30-day, 90-day, 1-year, or perpetual rights each have different pricing
- Always specify: platforms, duration, whether you can edit/modify the content
- 2026 update: Agreements should specify whether content can be used for AI training

**Minimum agreement terms to document (even informally via email):**
1. What content will be created (format, platform, approximate timing)
2. Compensation (amount, payment method, payment timing)
3. Disclosure requirements (#ad, FTC compliance)
4. Content usage rights (organic only? paid ads? duration?)
5. Creative freedom vs. mandatory talking points
6. Timeline for delivery
7. Whether the creator can also post on other platforms

### Handling Creators Who Want More Than You Can Offer

**The conversation framework:**

1. **Listen first.** Understand what they value most (money? access? recognition? creative freedom?)
2. **Validate.** "Your rates make total sense for the value you provide."
3. **Reframe.** "Here's what we CAN offer that most brands can't..."
   - Genuine early access (not a PR build, but the real game)
   - Creative freedom (no script, no mandatory talking points)
   - Long-term relationship (not a one-and-done)
   - "Founding creator" status with ongoing benefits
   - Revenue share that could exceed flat fees if the game takes off
4. **Make the ask smaller.** Instead of a full video, can they do a Story/Short?
5. **Leave the door open.** "If this isn't the right time, I'd love to stay connected for when we have more budget."

### How to Measure ROI from Influencer Partnerships

**Attribution methods (use all three together):**

1. **Unique UTM links per creator**
   - Format: `spacezero.com/download?utm_source=influencer&utm_medium=[platform]&utm_campaign=[creator_handle]`
   - Track clicks in Google Analytics or your analytics tool
   - Gives you click-level attribution per creator per platform

2. **Unique promo codes per creator**
   - Format: `CREATOR_NAME` or `SPACEZERO_[HANDLE]`
   - Track redemptions in-app
   - Works even when UTM links break (copied links, app store redirects)
   - Double as a perk for the creator's audience ("use my code for X")

3. **Mobile Measurement Partner (MMP)**
   - Tools: Adjust, AppsFlyer, Branch, Singular
   - Assign unique deep links or QR codes to each creator
   - Tracks installs, in-app events (first build, day-7 retention), and ROAS at the creator level
   - This is the gold standard for mobile game attribution

**Key metrics to calculate:**

| Metric | Formula | Good Benchmark |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| Cost Per Install (CPI) | Total creator cost / attributed installs | <$2-3 for mobile gaming |
| Cost Per Mille (CPM) | (Total cost / total views) x 1000 | $5-$20 for gaming content |
| Engagement Rate | (Likes + comments + shares) / views x 100 | >5% on TikTok, >3% on YouTube |
| Response Rate | Creators who responded / creators contacted | 15-30% is typical |
| Conversion Rate | Creators who posted / creators contacted | 5-15% is typical |
| Content ROI | Revenue from attributed users / total creator spend | $5.78 per $1 spent (industry avg) |

**Timeline expectations:**
- Influencer content has a longer tail than paid ads
- TikTok: Can go viral weeks or months after posting (algorithm resurfaces)
- YouTube: Evergreen discovery via search for months/years
- Instagram: Shorter spike, typically peaks in 48 hours
- Track for at least 30-90 days per piece of content before calculating final ROI
