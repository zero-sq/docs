# Creator Outreach Strategy: Dual-Track GTM

**Date:** 2026-03-08
**Purpose:** Unified creator strategy for SpaceZero's two products. Different creators, different audiences, one funnel.

---

## Two Products, Two Creator Pools, One Funnel

SpaceZero has two products that need completely different types of creators:

| | ZeroCap (iOS App) | SpaceZero (Browser Game) |
|---|---|---|
| **What it is** | AI Tamagotchi -- point phone at any object, AI turns it into a cute living pet | AI-native Minecraft successor, 60 FPS in browser, every world is a URL |
| **Target creators** | Pet influencers, cute/kawaii, AI/tech, lifestyle, mom/family, college/Gen Z | Minecraft builders, cozy gaming, indie game discoverers, ASMR building |
| **Viral content** | Before/after transformation (object → cute pet) | Build timelapses, "click this link" world shares |
| **Creator hit list** | [`zerocap-creator-targeting.md`](zerocap-creator-targeting.md) | [`creator-targeting-research.md`](creator-targeting-research.md) |

**How they connect:** Pets created in ZeroCap migrate into SpaceZero worlds. ZeroCap is the acquisition engine (broad audience, viral loop). SpaceZero is the retention engine (builders, deep engagement). The pet is the bridge.

---

## Why Two Separate Creator Tracks

You can't use the same creators for both products. The audiences are fundamentally different:

- A pet TikToker's audience wants cute transformations, not Minecraft-style building
- A Minecraft builder's audience wants sandbox gameplay, not AI pet apps
- Pitching "AI Tamagotchi" to a gaming channel waters down the message
- Pitching "browser sandbox game" to a pet channel confuses the audience

**The exception: bridge creators.** Cozy gaming creators naturally span both worlds -- they play cute nurturing games (Animal Crossing, Stardew Valley) AND building games. They're the ideal creators for the transition content in Phase 2 (see sequencing below).

---

## Sequencing: Lead with ZeroCap

Don't launch both creator campaigns simultaneously. Lead with ZeroCap.

**Why ZeroCap first:**
- Simpler viral mechanic (photo → pet → share)
- Broader audience (everyone likes cute stuff vs. gamers only)
- Lower friction entry (App Store download vs. understanding a browser game)
- Photo-to-X apps have proven explosive virality (Lensa $50M+, Ghibli filter 700M images/week)
- ZeroCap's K-factor estimate (1.0) is much higher than SpaceZero's (0.027)

### The Three Phases

**Phase 1: ZeroCap Creator Blitz (Weeks 1-4)**
- Target: Pet/cute/AI/lifestyle creators
- Goal: App downloads and pet creation volume
- Content: Before/after transformations, "what my [object] turned into," pet personality reveals
- Budget: $0 (product seeding 50-100 nano creators) to $1,500 (10 paid nano creators)
- Creator list: [`zerocap-creator-targeting.md`](zerocap-creator-targeting.md)

**Phase 2: Bridge Content (Weeks 4-8)**
- Target: Cozy gaming creators who span cute + building audiences
- Goal: Introduce "your pet has a world to explore" messaging
- Content: ZeroCap pets appearing in SpaceZero worlds, "my AI pet's new home" tours
- Budget: $500-$1,500 (5-10 cozy gaming creators)
- These creators exist in both hit lists -- prioritize the overlap

**Phase 3: SpaceZero Creator Wave (Weeks 6-12)**
- Target: Gaming/builder/indie discovery creators
- Goal: SpaceZero player acquisition and world creation
- Content: Build timelapses, "no download" world links, building challenges
- Budget: $500-$2,000 (5-10 gaming creators + product seeding)
- Creator list: [`creator-targeting-research.md`](creator-targeting-research.md)

## Bridge Creators: The Overlap

These creator niches naturally span both ZeroCap's and SpaceZero's audiences:

| Bridge Niche | ZeroCap Angle | SpaceZero Angle |
|---|---|---|
| **Cozy gaming** (Animal Crossing, Stardew Valley) | Nurturing cute creatures = AI pet app | Building and decorating = sandbox game |
| **Roblox Adopt Me creators** | Pet collecting and trading | World building and customization |
| **Kawaii gaming aesthetic** | Cute pet reveals | Pink/aesthetic builds in SpaceZero |
| **Minecraft + pet mod creators** | Cute pets in sandbox worlds | Building in a new sandbox |
| **Family/kid gaming channels** | Kid-friendly AI pet app | Family building game |

**Best bridge creator format:** "I created an AI pet in ZeroCap, and now it lives in my SpaceZero world." This single video drives downloads for both products.

## The Historical Precedent

The ZeroCap → SpaceZero funnel isn't new. It maps to proven models:

| Precedent | Casual Entry Point | Deeper Product | Bridge Mechanic |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Webkinz** | Buy stuffed animal ($15) | Online virtual world | Enter code → pet appears in game |
| **Bitmoji → Snapchat** | Create avatar (free app) | Snapchat ecosystem | Avatar appears across Lenses, games, messaging |
| **Pokémon GO → Pokémon** | Catch Pokémon on walks | Full Pokémon games | Same creatures, shared emotional attachment |
| **Fortnite Creative → UEFN** | Play battle royale | Create/publish games | Same account, same island, deeper tools |
| **ZeroCap → SpaceZero** | Point camera, get pet | Build 3D worlds in browser | Pet migrates into your world |

**Critical lesson from Supercell's Squad Busters:** They spent $100M launching a casual game as a funnel to their core games. It got 60M downloads but failed retention because it was designed as marketing, not a standalone product. **ZeroCap must be a great app on its own.** Pet migration to SpaceZero should feel like a delightful bonus, not the entire point.

## Nostalgia: The Cross-Demographic Unifier

"AI Tamagotchi" is the one positioning that works across ALL creator pools:

| Generation | How They Experience It |
|---|---|
| **Millennials** (28-42) | Direct nostalgia. "I had a Tamagotchi. This is like that but with AI." |
| **Gen Z** (16-27) | Aesthetic nostalgia. They love retro aesthetics as trendy/cool, even without living through it. 68% feel more positive about brands using nostalgia. |
| **Gen Alpha** (kids) | Fresh discovery. The cute pet mechanic works on its own, no nostalgia needed. |

This means the same core pitch -- "AI Tamagotchi" -- resonates with:
- Pet influencers talking to millennial pet owners
- Kawaii creators talking to Gen Z aesthetic-lovers
- Mom creators talking to parents who grew up with Tamagotchi
- AI/tech creators talking to early adopters
- Gaming creators talking to sandbox enthusiasts
