# AI UGC Pipeline: Fit Assessment for Spacezero / Zerocap

Assessment of whether the AI-generated UGC video pipeline (HeyGen, Arcads, Kling, etc.) applies to our GTM.

See also: `arcavid/research/ai-ugc-video-pipeline.md` for how the ecosystem works.

## Verdict: AI Avatar Layer Doesn't Fit. Volume Principles Do.

## Why Talking-Head Format Doesn't Work for Us

**Zerocap's content IS the product.** The viral loop is a before/after photo-to-3D-pet transformation. That's inherently more compelling than any AI-generated person holding a phone and saying "OMG you guys have to try this app." The transformation is the hook — a talking head gets in the way.

**The AI UGC pipeline is built for physical products** — supplements, skincare, gadgets — where you need someone "reviewing" or "unboxing" something. We don't have a physical thing to hold up. Our content is screen-native.

**Our GTM already says "no humans on camera."** The winning TikTok format for apps like ours is:
- Screen recordings with text overlays
- Satisfying before/after reveals
- "Watch my photo become a pet" transformation clips
- Meme edits and trend-jacking

A synthetic influencer reading a script would perform worse than a clean transformation video with a good hook.

## What IS Worth Stealing

### Volume Testing
The principle of testing 50-100 hook variations over the same base content is directly applicable. Take one great transformation clip and generate dozens of text hook variations:
- "Turn your cat into a 3D pet"
- "This app just turned my selfie into a Tamagotchi"
- "POV: your photo comes alive"

### Hook Research Tools
Using Foreplay/Minea to spy on what hooks are working in the photo-AI app category (Lensa, Remini, Prisma) — worth doing.

### Batch Assembly
CapCut or scripted FFmpeg to stamp different caption hooks on the same base footage — scales without needing AI avatars at all.

## Bottom Line

We don't need fake people. We need a system that takes real product output (transformations, gameplay clips) and wraps them in high-volume hook variations. The content factory approach is right — the AI avatar layer is the wrong tool for it.
