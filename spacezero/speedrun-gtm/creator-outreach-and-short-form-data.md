## Topic 1: Cold Email Outreach to Gaming Nano/Micro-Influencers

### 1.1 The Full Funnel: Emails Sent to Video Published

**Synthesized from multiple sources — the realistic funnel for cold outreach to gaming creators (5K-50K subs):**

| Stage | Rate | From 500 emails |
|-------|------|-----------------|
| Emails sent | 100% | 500 |
| Emails opened | 40-60% | 200-300 |
| Emails that get a reply | 5-20% | 25-100 |
| Replies that are positive / accept key | 50-70% | 13-70 |
| Keys sent that result in published video | 20-80% | 3-56 |
| **Realistic middle estimate** | | **~15-30 videos** |

**Source breakdown for each stage:**

- **Open rate (40-60%):** General cold email open rates average 44% in 2025. Personalized subject lines push this higher. Influencer-specific emails with the creator's name in the subject line perform at the high end. ([Mailforge](https://www.mailforge.ai/blog/average-cold-email-response-rates), [LevelUp Leads](https://levelupleads.io/cold-email-benchmarks-2025-key-stats-every-marketer-should-know/))

- **Reply rate (5-20%):** Average cold email reply rate is 5.1% (2024 data), but influencer outreach specifically averages ~10% for generic pitches and 25-35% for personalized ones. The 18% figure from Influencer Marketing Hub's 2025 report is the best influencer-specific benchmark. Nano-influencers respond faster than micros. Follow-ups boost reply rates by up to 65.8%. ([Influencer Marketing Hub 2025 Report](https://influencermarketinghub.com/influencer-marketing-benchmark-report/), [Later Influence](https://help-influence.later.com/hc/en-us/articles/27971223555095-Creator-Recruitment-Outreach-Best-Practices-on-Improving-Response-Rates), [Influencer Hero](https://www.influencer-hero.com/blog-detail/achieve-40-reply-rates-with-our-top-5-influencer-outreach-rules))

- **Key-to-coverage rate (20-80%):** Industry standard is ~20% — meaning only 1 in 5 keys sent results in actual coverage. PressEngine claims 80%+ on their platform with verified, engaged creators. The gap between 20% and 80% comes down to whether you're mass-blasting keys to anyone who asks vs. carefully vetting creators who actively want to cover your genre. ([PressEngine](https://pressengine.net/), [PressEngine Features](https://pressengine.net/features))

**Bottom line:** Plan for **200-500 emails to get 10-25 videos made**. Oriol Cosp (The Ouroboros King developer) sent ~20 emails/day and generated 4.5M YouTube views from creator coverage. The Ouroboros King went from 500 to 20,000 wishlists primarily through targeted creator outreach. ([oriolcosp.com](https://oriolcosp.com/how-to-email-streamers-to-get-your-game-covered/), [Game Developer](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/business/youtuber-streamer-outreach-and-the-importance-of-starting-small))

### 1.3 What % of Creators Who Respond Actually Make a Video?

No single study isolates this number cleanly. But from triangulating:

- **PressEngine data:** 80%+ key-to-coverage rate on their platform (pre-vetted, engaged creators). This is the ceiling.
- **Industry standard key-to-coverage:** ~20%. This includes mass key distribution where many recipients never play the game.
- **Howtomarketagame.com guidance:** Of 200 emails sent, expect ~20 responses, of which 7-15 make content. That's **35-75% of responders** actually creating content.
- **Momento (wholesome game) case study:** Nearly all influencers who responded made a video. Only one asked for payment. But this was a warm-ish outreach to creators who had already interacted with their content.

**Working estimate: 40-60% of creators who positively respond will actually publish a video.** The rest will accept the key, maybe play it, but never get around to making content.

Sources: [PressEngine](https://pressengine.net/), [How To Market A Game](https://howtomarketagame.com/2024/02/07/how-to-get-tiktok-influencers-to-cover-your-game-and-earn-millions-of-impressions/)

### 1.4 Typical View Counts for Nano-Influencer Gaming Content

**View-to-subscriber ratio for YouTube gaming channels:**

| Subscriber Range | Expected Views/Video | View Rate (% of subs) |
|------------------|---------------------|-----------------------|
| 5,000 subs | 500-1,000 | 10-20% |
| 10,000 subs | 1,000-2,000 | 10-20% |
| 25,000 subs | 2,500-5,000 | 10-20% |
| 50,000 subs | 5,000-10,000 | 10-20% |

**Where these numbers come from:** The general benchmark is a 14% view-to-subscriber ratio (from a 100K-subscriber channel getting 12-15K views). Some experts cite 10% as "good." Gaming channels tend to be in the 10-20% range because gaming content has higher inherent engagement rates. Nano-influencers (under 10K subs) have 5.22% engagement rates — the highest of any tier.

**Important caveat:** A single sponsored/gifted video about an indie game will often underperform the creator's organic content. Their audience came for their usual content; a game they've never heard of will get fewer views unless the creator genuinely loves it and the thumbnail/title are compelling.

**Revenue context:** Nano-influencers (1K-10K subs) typically charge $50-$200 for a sponsored video, or accept a free game key for organic coverage.

### 1.5 Click-Through Rates from Creator Videos

**From creator video to game store page / website:**

| Metric | Rate | Source |
|--------|------|--------|
| Average video ad CTR | 0.5-2% | General digital benchmarks |
| TikTok CTR range | 0.63-3.76% | Aligned with YouTube rates |
| Gaming genre CTR (ads) | 13.72% | Mobile game ad benchmarks |
| CTA in description link clicks | 1-5% of views | Estimated from multiple sources |

**Reality check:** For a nano-influencer video about your game with 2,000 views, expect 20-100 people clicking through to your store page (1-5% CTR). Of those, maybe 10-30% wishlist or install. So one 2,000-view video might generate 2-30 wishlists/installs.

**The real conversion mechanism:** Most people don't click links in videos. They see the game, remember the name, and search for it later on Steam/App Store. This is why Chris Zukowski emphasizes that TikTok views translate to search traffic — after seeing a TikTok about "I Just Want to be Single!!", users walked to their PC and searched for it on Steam.

### 1.8 What Makes a Cold Pitch Work vs. Get Ignored

**What works (data-backed):**

1. **Personalization:** Emails with advanced personalization achieve 17% response rate vs. 7% without it. Reference a specific recent video by name.
2. **Short and scannable:** Creators get 50+ pitches during busy periods. If they can't understand your ask in 30 seconds, they skip.
3. **Comparison hook:** "Imagine Game A meets Game B" communicates your game in 3 seconds.
4. **Include the trailer link immediately.** Don't make them search. A streamer can't play screenshots.
5. **Offer early/exclusive access.** "You'd be one of the first to play" > "please check out my game."
6. **Follow up once, 3-5 days later.** A polite reminder boosts replies by up to 30%.
7. **Timing:** Avoid major Steam sales, AAA launch windows, E3/Summer Game Fest. Target quieter periods or coordinate with your own Steam Next Fest presence.

**What gets ignored:**

1. **Generic BCC blasts.** Creators recognize mass emails. Word spreads in their community.
2. **Emails with no trailer or gameplay.** No visual = no interest.
3. **Too long.** Multi-paragraph essays about your game's lore get skipped.
4. **No reason "why them."** If it's obvious you sent the same email to 500 people, they don't feel special.
5. **Bad timing.** Emails during major game releases or holidays get buried.
6. **Shortened URLs.** These trigger spam filters and look untrustworthy.

Sources: [Xsolla](https://xsolla.com/blog/how-to-distribute-game-keys-among-video-influencers), [TinyBuild](https://www.tinybuild.com/how-to-pitch-your-game), [InboxAlly](https://www.inboxally.com/blog/cold-email-to-influencer), [ProfitOutreach](https://profitoutreach.app/blog/cold-email-statistics/)

### 2.2 What Triggers the Algorithmic Boost on TikTok for Gaming Content

**The 2026 algorithm (updated from earlier research):**

1. **New videos are shown to existing followers first** (changed in 2026 — previously went to random test audience). Follower engagement determines whether the video gets pushed wider.

2. **Completion rate is king.** The threshold for viral distribution is approximately 70% completion rate (up from ~50% in 2024). Below 70%, videos almost never break 10K views.

3. **Saves and shares now outweigh likes and comments** as algorithmic signals. These are treated as stronger indicators of genuine relevance.

4. **The testing waves:**
   - Wave 1: Small group (primarily followers)
   - Wave 2: If engagement is strong, expanded to a wider audience
   - Wave 3+: Each wave tests further; metrics determine advancement
   - No fixed number of posts before the algorithm "trusts" you

5. **3-second retention:** Videos with 3-second retention above 65% receive 4-7x more impressions. Videos maintaining 70-85% retention in the first 3 seconds get 2.2x more total views.

6. **Gaming content is inherently favored** because it's visual, fast-paced, and emotionally charged — exactly what TikTok rewards. A 15-second clip of a satisfying moment can generate hundreds of thousands of organic impressions.

**CRITICAL 2025-2026 UPDATE — Organic Reach is Declining:**

- TikTok no longer offers the same level of free organic visibility it once did. The drift away from easy organic reach began in 2024 but accelerated sharply in 2025.
- Performance graphs show a dramatic collapse in organic reach for content strategies that once delivered millions of views.
- Across 30+ games tracked by Cloutboost, TikTok views are declining for most indie titles.
- Valeriia Kramp (Influencer Manager, Owlcat Games): "The reach of our paid TikTok campaigns has dropped sharply over the past several months."
- **Significant views now typically require paid promotion** — the era of pure organic is narrowing.
- The recommended approach: Post organically, then when a specific video naturally gets more traction than others, put a small budget ($200) behind it to amplify that post. This "tricks" the algorithm into thinking the content is going viral, driving more organic traffic.
- TikTok now limits posts to 5 hashtags (reduced from unlimited), forcing more strategic tag selection.

Sources: [Cloutboost: TikTok's Changing Landscape 2026](https://www.cloutboost.com/blog/tiktoks-changing-landscape-for-game-marketing-in-2026-what-developers-need-to-know), [Buffer: TikTok Algorithm](https://buffer.com/resources/tiktok-algorithm/), [Hootsuite: TikTok Algorithm](https://blog.hootsuite.com/tiktok-algorithm/)

### 2.4 How Many Posts Before the Algorithm Starts Pushing Content?

**There is no magic number.** But the data shows a pattern:

| Timeframe | What Typically Happens |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Posts 1-5 | Algorithm is testing. Expect 200-1K views unless one hits early. |
| Posts 5-15 | Algorithm starts understanding your niche. Some videos may break 5K. |
| Posts 15-30 | If you have a winning format, at least 1-2 should break 10K-50K. |
| Posts 30-50 | Compound effect. Baseline views should be climbing. |
| 3-4 weeks of daily posting | The typical "breakout" window where something catches. |

**The Mortal Rite timeline:** Months of consistent posting before explosive growth. It was compound interest, not an overnight event.

**The "almost gave up" stories:** Multiple developers describe nearly quitting after their first week showed minimal results, then their next video broke through. The lesson: you need 2-4 weeks minimum to give the algorithm enough data to work with.

**Account warming matters:** New accounts that post high-frequency content immediately get flagged for spam-like behavior. Warm the account for 7-14 days with gradual engagement activity (follow accounts, like content, comment on similar videos) before ramping to full production volume.

Sources: [TokPortal](https://www.tokportal.com/use-cases/gaming-tiktok-launch-scale-game-promotion), [Hootsuite](https://blog.hootsuite.com/tiktok-algorithm/), [RecurPost](https://recurpost.com/blog/how-often-should-you-post-on-tiktok/)

**Key takeaway:** TikTok views-to-wishlists conversion is typically **0.05-2%**, with a realistic average around **0.5%** (1 wishlist per 200 views). Smaller, more targeted videos convert at higher rates than mass-viral ones.

**Why the conversion rate is so low:** TikTok doesn't support direct links until you have 1,000 followers. Before that, you can't put a link in your bio. Users must remember the game name, walk to their PC, and search for it on Steam. Most people don't.

**The indirect conversion path:**
1. See TikTok video
2. Remember game name (maybe)
3. Later, search on Steam
4. Wishlist
5. Eventually buy (median 18-27% of wishlists convert to first-month sales)

### 2.7 YouTube Shorts vs TikTok: Which Performs Better for Indie Game Discovery (2025-2026)?

**Head-to-head comparison:**

| Factor | TikTok | YouTube Shorts |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| **Daily views (total platform)** | 90 billion+ | 70 billion+ |
| **Retention rate** | 78% | 73% |
| **Time spent per session** | 58 min 24 sec average | Longer sessions overall |
| **Discovery potential for new accounts** | Higher (algorithm favors new content) | Lower (channel history matters more) |
| **Content lifespan** | 2-3 days peak, then dies | Weeks to months (evergreen discovery) |
| **Gaming content ranking** | #1 for gaming discovery as of Q1 2025 | #3 niche by total Shorts views |
| **Direct link capability** | Only after 1K followers | Available via video description |
| **View count methodology** | 5-second qualified view | Any duration counts as view (as of March 2025) |
| **Organic reach trend (2025-2026)** | Declining — paid now often required | Stable to growing |

**The verdict for indie game discovery:**

- **TikTok wins for rapid viral moments and initial discovery.** Gaming content on TikTok outperforms Instagram Reels and YouTube Shorts for pure discovery in Q1 2025.
- **YouTube Shorts wins for sustained, long-tail visibility.** A Short from Week 1 can still gain views in Week 6. TikTok content dies in 2-3 days.
- **TikTok's organic advantage is eroding.** The platform that was "ridiculously effective" in 2021 now requires paid amplification for significant reach in many cases.
- **YouTube Shorts provides direct conversion paths** (links in descriptions, integration with full YouTube channels) that TikTok lacks until 1K followers.

**For a 2-person team starting from zero:** Post to both platforms simultaneously. TikTok for the chance at viral discovery; YouTube Shorts for steady, compounding growth. Use the same content — produce once, upload natively to both (no watermarks).

**Important caveat:** Viewers on both platforms prefer relatable, spontaneous reactions to overly polished videos. Using specific game communities, hashtags, and consistent posting helps smaller creators tap into algorithmic promotion even without large followings.
