# Influencer Marketing Platforms: What People Actually Use

**Research date:** 2026-03-07
**Companion to:** `influencer-ugc-platform-landscape.md` (financial/company-level data)
**Purpose:** Cut through marketing-speak. What are real brands, agencies, studios, and marketers actually using day to day?

---

## The Uncomfortable Truth Up Front

The influencer marketing tools space is deeply fragmented. There is no single dominant platform the way Salesforce dominates CRM or Figma dominates UI design. The real answer to "what do people use?" breaks down roughly like this:

1. **Most small-to-mid brands: Spreadsheets + manual DMs + email.** This is the dirty secret. The majority of influencer marketing, especially below the enterprise tier, is still done with Google Sheets, Instagram DMs, and email templates. Platforms like SARAL and Modash exist precisely because this is the status quo they're trying to replace.

2. **Enterprise brands (Fortune 500): CreatorIQ or Sprout Social (Tagger).** These are the two that dominate at the top end. CreatorIQ powers 500+ enterprise brands including Disney, Unilever, Tiffany & Co., Dell, Ralph Lauren, AB InBev, and Nestlé. Sprout Social acquired Tagger Media and now bundles influencer marketing into its broader social media management suite.

3. **DTC / e-commerce brands that actually pay for a tool: GRIN, Aspire, or Upfluence.** These three are the real workhorses of the mid-market. GRIN is the most Shopify-native. Aspire is best for creator community building. Upfluence has the broadest discovery database.

4. **Gaming studios: Keymailer, Lurkit, Gamesight, or direct outreach.** Gaming is its own world with specialized tools. Most indie devs just email creators directly.

5. **Agencies: CreatorIQ, Julius, or proprietary systems.** Many agencies build their own internal tools on top of APIs.

6. **Affiliate-first brands: impact.com, LTK, or ShopMy.** These blur the line between influencer marketing and affiliate marketing.

## Tier 1: Enterprise Platforms (Fortune 500 / Global Brands)

### CreatorIQ
- **G2 rating:** 4.7/5 (highest in category among enterprise tools)
- **Customers:** 500+ enterprise brands and agencies
- **Known users:** Disney, Unilever, Tiffany & Co., Dell, Ralph Lauren, AB InBev, Nestlé, Fabletics, Moose Toys
- **What it does:** Central CRM for global influencer programs. Prevents redundant creator outreach across regions. Consolidated reporting, contract management, cross-market analytics.
- **Why enterprises use it:** API integrations with social platforms, compliance/legal controls, multi-market coordination. It's the "Salesforce of influencer marketing" for big orgs.
- **Pricing:** Enterprise only, not publicly listed. Estimated $30K-$100K+/year.
- **Reality check:** Overkill for anyone under ~$5M annual influencer spend. Long onboarding.

### Sprout Social (formerly Tagger Media)
- **G2 position:** #1 in Social Listening (Winter 2026), Leader in Influencer Marketing
- **What happened:** Sprout Social acquired Tagger Media and integrated influencer marketing into its broader social media management suite.
- **Why it matters:** Many enterprise marketing teams already use Sprout Social for social media management. Adding influencer marketing to the same platform reduces tool sprawl.
- **IDC MarketScape:** Named a Leader in Worldwide Influencer Marketing Platforms for Large Enterprises (Dec 2025).
- **Reality check:** The influencer module is still being fully integrated. Teams that need deep influencer-specific features may find it less specialized than CreatorIQ.

## Tier 2: Mid-Market Workhorses (DTC / E-Commerce / Growth Brands)

### GRIN
- **G2 rating:** Positive reviews, consistently listed as Leader
- **Known users:** SKIMS, Rhode, GoPro, Warby Parker, Allbirds, Mejuri, MVMT (~172 brands tracked by 1800dtc.com)
- **Best for:** DTC / Shopify-native brands. Product seeding, affiliate tracking, content library management.
- **Key strength:** End-to-end creator management. Automates product shipping, generates affiliate codes, tracks deliveries, manages payments -- all in one dashboard.
- **Pricing:** Not public. Estimated $15K-$25K+/year.
- **Reality check:** Users complain about bugs in mass emailing, slow loading times, and weak reporting/analytics. The platform is powerful but rough around the edges.

### Aspire (formerly AspireIQ)
- **G2 rating:** 4.8/5 from 120+ reviews (very high satisfaction)
- **Best for:** Fast-growing DTC brands building long-term creator communities
- **Key strength:** Creator CRM, UGC collection, repeat collaboration workflows. Shopify integration.
- **Pricing:** Not public. Estimated $12K-$24K/year.
- **Reality check:** Less robust discovery than Upfluence or Modash. Better for managing existing relationships than finding new creators.

### Upfluence
- **G2 rating:** 4.5/5
- **Customers:** 1,000-1,300+
- **Revenue:** ~$35M (highly capital-efficient -- only $3.9M raised)
- **Key strength:** Multi-platform discovery (12M+ verified influencers), Amazon Attribution integration, social listening to identify customers who are also influencers.
- **Pricing:** Starts ~$478/month (Growth plan).
- **Reality check:** Solid all-rounder. Not the absolute best at any single thing, but covers the most ground. AI-powered "Jaice" assistant is a recent addition.

### The Big Three Comparison (GRIN vs Aspire vs Upfluence)

| Feature | GRIN | Aspire | Upfluence |
|---------|------|--------|-----------|
| Discovery database | Smaller | Medium | Largest (12M+) |
| Shopify integration | Best | Good | Good |
| Product seeding/shipping | Best | Good | Limited |
| Creator CRM | Good | Best | Good |
| UGC collection | Good | Best | Limited |
| Affiliate tracking | Good | Good | Good |
| Amazon integration | No | No | Yes |
| Pricing | $$$$ | $$$ | $$$ |

## Tier 3: Discovery & Search Tools (Often First Step Before Full Platform)

### Modash
- **Database:** 350M+ creator profiles (largest of any platform -- every public profile with 1K+ followers)
- **Revenue:** ~$9.8M, growing fast
- **Pricing:** More affordable than the Big Three
- **Best for:** Finding creators. Less robust for full campaign management.
- **Recent moves:** Acquired Promoty (Jan 2025), raised $11.9M Series A.

### HypeAuditor
- **Database:** 220M+ profiles
- **Key strength:** Fraud detection and audience authenticity analysis. Lets you verify that an influencer's followers are real before you pay.
- **Best for:** Vetting influencers, not managing campaigns.

### Heepsy
- **Database:** 11M+ influencers
- **Customers:** 10,000+ (extremely efficient -- 8-12 employees)
- **Known users:** IKEA, Red Bull, Philips
- **Pricing:** Low entry point, self-serve
- **Best for:** Quick influencer search at low cost

### SARAL
- **Revenue:** ~$1.1M (170% YoY growth, fully bootstrapped)
- **Customers:** 103
- **Best for:** Automated influencer outreach and relationship management for DTC brands
- **Key strength:** Outreach automation -- finds, emails, and follows up with creators automatically

## Tier 4: Marketplaces (Connect Brand to Creator Directly)

### Collabstr
- **Scale:** 550,000+ creators, 330,000+ brands
- **Model:** Self-serve marketplace. Browse pre-priced creator packages, book directly.
- **Average cost:** Micro-influencer posts ~$198. 80% of collaborations under $300.
- **Best for:** Small brands, one-off campaigns, UGC content

### Afluencer
- **Model:** Free for brands. Low-cost entry for small businesses and startups.
- **Best for:** Indie DTC brands (beauty, wellness, fashion, food), local businesses

### LTK (formerly rewardStyle/LIKEtoKNOW.it)
- **Scale:** 150,000+ creators, $6B in annual sales
- **Model:** Affiliate-driven. Creators earn commissions on sales.
- **Reality check:** Cutting staff, transitioning to free self-serve brand product. Under pressure from ShopMy.

### ShopMy
- **Scale:** 200,000 creators (up from 40,000 in 2023), 1,200 brand partners, $1B+ annual GMV
- **Revenue:** ~$80M in 2025 (up 196% from $27M in 2024)
- **Model:** Affiliate links + brand subscriptions. Dual revenue: ~50/50 split between subscriptions and transaction fees.
- **Reality check:** Fastest-growing platform in the affiliate-influencer space. Meghan Markle is on it. Absorbing market share from Collective Voice (which shut down Dec 2025).

### impact.com / Creator
- **Scale:** 350,000+ active partnerships, 5,000+ clients
- **Model:** Unified platform for creators, affiliates, and referrals. Influencer capabilities built into the broader partnership management platform.
- **Key stat:** During Cyber Week 2025, influencer-driven spend jumped 51% while commission costs stayed flat.
- **Best for:** Brands that want influencer marketing and affiliate marketing in a single system.

## Gaming-Specific Platforms

Gaming influencer marketing is a completely different world from DTC/e-commerce. The tools are specialized around key distribution, Twitch/YouTube analytics, and content tracking.

### What Gaming Studios Actually Use (by tier)

**Indie developers:**
- **Most common:** Manual email outreach. Look up streamers/YouTubers individually, send personal emails. Free, and the most effective approach per Cloutboost and multiple Reddit/gamedev consensus.
- **Key distribution:** Keymailer ($24.99/mo indie plan) or Woovit (free tier). These let you distribute game keys to creators who opt in to receive them.
- **Reality:** Most indie devs cannot afford platforms. They build a spreadsheet of 50-200 creators, email them individually, and track responses manually.

**Mid-tier studios:**
- **Lurkit** ($89/mo, up to 70% indie discount in year 1). 100,000+ creators, strong Twitch/YouTube analytics. Creators claimed and activated 375K+ game codes in 2024, with 81% turning into submitted content.
- **Keymailer/Partnier** (recently rebranded). 50,000+ influencers. Cheaper than Lurkit. Less strict vetting = occasional quality issues.

**AAA publishers:**
- **Gamesight.** Customers include SEGA, Tencent, Ubisoft, Capcom, Warner Bros. Games, Jagex. Customers spend $1B+ on marketing through the platform. The only company tracking gaming influencer content to actual in-game purchases for PC + console.
- **Terminals.io.** $2,000-$6,000 per campaign. Comprehensive PR + marketing suite, not just key distribution. Too expensive for indie.
- **Agencies:** Many AAA publishers use agencies like Cloutboost (Sony Interactive, Wargaming, Samsung, Rovio) or GameInfluencer (Wargaming, Miniclip, Nexon, Nvidia) rather than running platforms in-house.

### Gaming Platform Comparison

| Platform | Creators | Pricing | Best For |
|----------|----------|---------|----------|
| Keymailer/Partnier | 50,000 | $24.99/mo (indie) | Key distribution, indie |
| Lurkit | 100,000 | $89/mo (indie discount available) | Mid-tier studios, analytics |
| Gamesight | 10K+ annual partners | Enterprise pricing | AAA, performance attribution |

| GameInfluencer | 3.1M database | Not public | Mobile/online game publishers |
| Cloutboost | 650K database | Agency model | Full-service, AAA clients |

## What Agencies Use

Most brands with significant influencer budgets work through agencies. What do those agencies run on?

- **CreatorIQ** -- the most common agency platform for large accounts. Designed for multi-brand, multi-market coordination.
- **Julius** -- specifically built for agencies and internal marketing departments. Access to millions of profiles with 50+ filters, audience demographics, prior collaborations.
- **NeoReach** -- built for agencies and Fortune 500. Contract management, automated outreach, influencer hunting.
- **GRIN** -- some agencies use it for DTC clients, though it's more brand-oriented.
- **Proprietary systems** -- many agencies build their own internal tools, often on top of social media APIs + a CRM like HubSpot or Salesforce. The platform is the agency's competitive moat.

## General-Purpose Alternatives (No Dedicated Platform)

### What many brands actually do instead of buying a platform:

1. **TikTok Creator Marketplace (TCM)** -- free, built into TikTok Ads. Brands can find and collaborate with creators directly. Limitation: TikTok-only content.
2. **Instagram Creator Marketplace** -- similar to TCM but for Instagram. Free, native to the platform.
3. **YouTube BrandConnect** -- Google's native influencer matching for YouTube.
4. **Upwork / Fiverr** -- ~23% of freelancers are involved in influencer content (blogs, videos, social campaigns). Brands hire freelance "influencer marketing managers" to run campaigns. Rates vary widely.
5. **Google Sheets + Instagram DMs + email** -- still the default for the majority of small/mid brands. Templates for outreach are widely shared. Average DM reply rate: ~10%. Email response rate: 1-5%.
6. **Pearpop** -- pay-for-performance creator marketplace. Brand posts a challenge, creators participate, get paid based on engagement.

### Why this matters:
Platform marketplaces (TikTok, Instagram, YouTube) are free and provide native data. For brands doing <$50K/year in influencer spend, a dedicated SaaS platform often doesn't pencil out. The math only works when you're managing 20+ active creator relationships and need automation for shipping, payments, contracts, and tracking.

## Key Takeaways

1. **There is no "winner."** The market is deeply fragmented. No platform has >5% market share of all influencer marketing activity.

2. **Most influencer marketing still happens without a platform.** Spreadsheets, DMs, and email are the reality for the vast majority of brands. The industry loves talking about platforms, but the ground truth is manual work.

3. **The real divide is enterprise vs. everyone else.** CreatorIQ dominates enterprise. Below that, GRIN/Aspire/Upfluence fight for mid-market DTC brands. Below that, it's a free-for-all of lightweight tools and marketplaces.

4. **Gaming is a separate world.** Generic influencer platforms don't work for gaming. Studios need key distribution, Twitch/YouTube analytics, and content-to-purchase attribution. Gamesight, Lurkit, and Keymailer own this space.

5. **Affiliate platforms are eating influencer platforms.** ShopMy ($80M revenue, 196% growth) and impact.com are growing faster than traditional influencer platforms because they tie directly to revenue. The line between "influencer marketing" and "affiliate marketing" is blurring.

6. **Native platform marketplaces are underrated.** TikTok Creator Marketplace and Instagram Creator Marketplace are free, have native data, and are used by far more brands than any third-party tool. They just don't get press because there's no company behind them selling the story.

7. **77% of brands work with only 1-10 influencers at a time.** At that scale, you genuinely don't need a platform. This explains why adoption of dedicated tools remains low despite billions in industry spending.
