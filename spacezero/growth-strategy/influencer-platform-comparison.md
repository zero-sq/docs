## The Maturity Problem

There is no "Stripe Atlas of influencer marketing." The space is immature compared to the tools SpaceZero uses elsewhere in the stack (Stripe, Mercury, Carta, Slack, Notion). None of the 24 platforms listed above deliver that level of product quality, UX, or reliability.

The closest to that energy:
- **ShopMy** ($1.5B, profitable, modern) -- but lifestyle/fashion, not gaming
- **Agentio** ($340M, AI-native, VC darling) -- but requires real ad budget, not our stage
- **Archive** ($100M, Stripe/Tiger backed, operator-built) -- but captures content, doesn't hire creators

The platforms that fit SpaceZero's actual needs (Lurkit, Collabstr) are scrappy 15-22 person startups. They work, but they're not Notion-quality:
- **Lurkit** -- gaming credibility (NetEase, Bohemia Interactive, Deep Silver), but rough creator-side reviews, opaque pricing, only 3 Trustpilot reviews (mostly negative)
- **Collabstr** -- great Trustpilot (4.7/5, 377 reviews), transparent pricing, but quality control problems (fake/unreliable creators slip through) and nonexistent customer support

See `lurkit-deep-dive.md`, `collabstr-deep-dive.md`, `sprout-social-deep-dive.md`, `grin-deep-dive.md`, and `upfluence-deep-dive.md` in this folder for full research.

### What People Actually Use

77% of brands work with only 1-10 influencers at a time. At that scale, most people use spreadsheets + DMs + email. No platform needed.

**Free native tools most brands start with (and we should too):**
- **TikTok Creator Marketplace** -- free, native platform data, built into TikTok
- **Instagram Creator Marketplace** -- same, built into Instagram
- **YouTube BrandConnect** -- same, built into YouTube

More brands use these free native tools than ANY third-party platform. They don't get press because there's no company marketing them.

**The mid-market workhorses — all built for e-commerce, not gaming:**
- **GRIN** -- launched self-serve $399/mo Lite plan (Jan 2026), but in serious trouble: $145M raised, peaked at $910M valuation, then 5 rounds of layoffs (70% staff cut, down to ~112), both founders left. 48% one-star on Trustpilot. Core value is e-commerce product seeding (shipping physical goods to creators) — irrelevant for a mobile game. The self-serve pivot is a distress signal, not a feature launch.
- **Aspire** -- 4.8/5 on G2 (highest satisfaction), but starts ~$2K/mo with annual commitment only. No free trial. No Twitch. Not for us.
- **Upfluence** -- has Twitch support, but 12-month mandatory contract, no free trial, predatory auto-renewal billing (Trustpilot 3.2/5). Dated UI.

**None of these three are good fits for a mobile game studio.** They're built for DTC brands shipping lipstick to influencers. See `grin-deep-dive.md`, `upfluence-deep-dive.md`, `grin-vs-aspire-vs-upfluence.md`, and `what-people-actually-use.md` in this folder.

**For gaming specifically:** AAA studios use Gamesight (SEGA, Tencent, Ubisoft, Capcom). Mid-tier uses Lurkit. Indie devs mostly DM creators directly.

### Realistic Options for SpaceZero

1. **Start with free native tools** -- TikTok Creator Marketplace, Instagram Creator Marketplace. Put 100% of budget toward paying creators, not software. Track in a spreadsheet.
2. **Direct outreach** -- DM gaming creators on TikTok/YouTube yourself. More work, but free, personal, and high-quality. This is how most early-stage gaming studios actually do it.
3. **Lurkit + Collabstr as supplements** -- Lurkit for gaming-specific creator discovery, Collabstr for cheap one-off UGC. Accept the rough edges.
4. **Gamesight** when you have real budget and need performance marketing at scale.

At this stage, every dollar toward creators beats every dollar toward software. Use free tools, DM directly, track in a spreadsheet.

## Archive -- Deep Dive

The closest thing to a "YC-energy" platform in this space. Founded by Geoffrey Woo (Chairman) and Paul Benigeri (CEO), both ex-ecommerce operators who built it to solve their own creator marketing pain.

**Funding:** $12M total (seed + extension), **$100M post-money valuation**. Investors: Stripe, Tiger Global, Battery Ventures, Lux Capital, Human Capital, plus 50+ angels from Attentive, Brex, Ramp.

**What it does:**
- AI-powered creator content capture -- tracks 4x more content than competitors, including ephemeral posts like Stories
- AI agents automate search, vetting, gifting, and reporting -- small teams can run big programs
- Social listening across TikTok, Reels, and Shorts
- 50K+ brands (Allbirds, Notion, DoorDash, Uniqlo, Momofuku)

**What people say:**
- Saves hours of manual influencer content tracking
- Easy team access for reposting content
- Organized storage + insightful analytics

**Important distinction:** Archive is a "capture and organize creator content" tool, not a "find and hire influencers" tool. It's complementary to Lurkit or Collabstr, not a replacement. Best used once you start getting organic creator mentions and need to track/leverage them.

**Signal:** $100M valuation on $12M raised with Stripe and Tiger Global backing = investors are betting big. This is the one to watch in this space.

## Later Influence -- Deep Dive

Later acquired Mavrck in 2024 and rebranded it as Later Influence. $174M raised, G2 leader Winter 2026, big clients like Southwest Airlines.

**What it does well:**
- 10M+ creator database with niche/engagement filters
- End-to-end campaign management (brief, content approval, tracking, payment)
- Customer support gets genuine praise
- All-in-one with scheduling (convenient if you already use Later)

**What's broken (from user reviews, 4.6/5 on Capterra, 134 reviews):**
- Buggy -- pages not loading, constant refreshing, messages sending incompletely
- Social accounts disconnect every two weeks
- **TikTok content can't be pulled into the platform** -- dealbreaker for a TikTok-first strategy
- Reporting accuracy is off -- content pulls through incorrectly
- Outreach feels spammy -- influencers think a bot is contacting them
- Killed the free plan

**Bottom line:** Later is a scheduling tool that bolted on influencer marketing. The influencer side is an add-on, not the core product. Solid business ($174M raised, not struggling), but nobody's raving about Later Influence specifically. The TikTok gap alone makes it a bad fit for SpaceZero.
